Grammar 1

V-(られ)ます

可能形かのうけい — Potential Form

🔍 What does it mean?

The potential form expresses ability or possibility — "can do ~." In travel contexts, it's used to talk about what you can see, eat, do, or experience at a destination. It replaces the longer pattern V + ことができます that you learned earlier.

Group辞書形じしょけい可能形かのうけいExamples
1 グループ
-u → -e + る		 -u ending -e + る 作つくる → 作つくれる
飲のむ → 飲のめる
泳およぐ → 泳およげる
2 グループ
-る → -られる		 -る -られる 見みる → 見みられる (見みれる*)
食たべる → 食たべられる (食たべれる*)
3 グループ
Irregular		 する / 来くる できる / 来こられる する → できる
来くる → 来こられる (来これる*)

* Group 2 and 来る can drop ら in casual speech: 見れる, 食べれる, 来れる. This is common but technically informal.

⚠️ Important Particle Change: を → が

When a transitive verb becomes potential, the object particle changes from を to が:

📝 Normal Verb

カニを食たべる

I eat crab

✨ Potential Form

カニが食たべられる

I can eat crab

📝 Travel Example 1
新鮮しんせんなカニとかウニとか食たべられますよ。
You can eat fresh crab and sea urchins, you know.
📝 Travel Example 2
京都きょうとは古ふるい町まちで、お寺てらとか神社じんじゃがたくさん見みられるよ。
Kyoto is an old city where you can see many temples and shrines.

MNN L18 V ことができます (ability)

In MNN L18, you learned ピアノを弾ひくことができます ("I can play piano"). The potential form is a shorter, more natural alternative: ピアノが弾ける. Both mean the same thing, but the potential form sounds more native and is used far more often in daily conversation.

Irodori L5 初級1 V + ことができます in context

初級1 L5 also used この店みせでは、おいしいそばを食べることができます. Now you can say: この店では、おいしいそばが食べられます — same meaning, much more natural!

Grammar 2

～ところ

Describing Places

🔍 What does it mean?

ところ means "place." By adding descriptive words before ところ, you can describe what kind of place somewhere is. This is incredibly useful for travel conversations — describing destinations, recommending locations, and asking about places to visit.

📐 Formation
V (plain) + ところ = "a place where you can V"

Adj + ところ = "a place that is Adj"
📝 Example 1
遊あそべるところはありますか？
Is there a place where we can play/have fun?
📝 Example 2
一日いちにち、ゆっくりできるところに行いきたいです。
I want to go to a place where I can relax all day.
💬 Describing a City
A：大阪おおさかはどんなところですか？
What kind of place is Osaka?
B：にぎやかで、食たべ物ものがおいしいところです。
It's a lively place with delicious food.
💡 Key Insight: ところ works like a "place characterizer." Any verb or adjective before it describes what you can do or experience there: 買かい物ものができるところ ("a place where you can shop"), 景色けしきがきれいなところ ("a place with beautiful scenery"), 食たべ物ものがおいしいところ ("a place with delicious food").

MNN L22 Noun modification (plain form + noun)

In MNN L22, you learned to modify nouns: おいしい料理りょうり ("delicious food"), 友達ともだちがくれた本ほん ("a book my friend gave me"). ～ところ follows the same pattern — ところ is the noun being modified: 遊べるところ = "a place [that you can play at]."

Grammar 3

S って聞ききました

Hearsay — "I heard that ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

These expressions are used to share information you've heard from others — reputations, rumors, news, or someone's words. って is the casual spoken form of と (quotation marker). You'll use this a lot when talking about travel destinations!

📐 Formation
S (plain form) + って聞ききました = "I heard that S"

S (plain form) + って言いっていました = "Someone said that S"

って can also end a sentence alone: うどんがおいしいって。("I heard udon is delicious.")

📝 Example 1 — Reputation
うどんがおいしいって聞ききました。
I heard that the udon is delicious.
📝 Example 2 — Quoting Someone
友達ともだちが、秋あきの日光にっこうは渋滞じゅうたいして大変たいへんだって言いってました。
My friend said Nikko in autumn is terribly crowded with traffic.
📝 Example 3 — Casual Ending
田中たなかさんも一緒いっしょに旅行りょこう、行いきたいって。
Tanaka said he wants to come on the trip too.
📖 と — Formal Quotation

おいしいと聞ききました

Formal/written

💬 って — Casual Quotation

おいしいって聞ききました

Casual/spoken (most common)

MNN L18 S (plain form) と言いっていました (quoting)

MNN L18 taught the formal quotation: 「おいしい」と言いっていました. って is the casual spoken contraction of と. In daily conversation, って is overwhelmingly more common than と for reporting what someone said or what you heard.

Grammar 4

[疑問詞ぎもんし] V-たらいいですか？

Seeking Advice

🔍 What does it mean?

This pattern is used to ask for advice about means, methods, or ways to handle a situation. In travel, you'll use it to ask about transportation, lodging, souvenirs, and what to do. It pairs with interrogative words: 何なにで (by what), いつ (when), どこに/で (where), どうやって (how).

📐 Formation
[Interrogative] + V-たらいいですか？

V-たら = the verb's タ-form + ら. (V-たら will be covered in detail in Lesson 7.)

📝 Transportation
日光にっこうに行いくんですけど、何なにで行いったらいいですか？
I'm going to Nikko — how should I get there?
📝 Lodging
どんなところに泊とまったらいいですか？
What type of place should I stay at?
📝 Souvenirs
お土産みやげは、どこで買かったらいいですか？
Where should I buy souvenirs?
💡 Key Insight: V-たらいいですか is your "travel advice request" toolkit. Combine it with different question words: 何なにを (what), 何なにで (by what means), いつ (when), どこで (where), どうやって (how). You can ask about anything — transport, food, accommodation, activities!

MNN L25 ～たら (conditional)

MNN L25 introduced ～たら for conditionals: 雨あめが降ふったら ("if it rains"). V-たらいいですか uses the same たら form but adds いいですか to ask "what would be good?" — turning it into an advice-seeking question.

Grammar 5

V-た / V-ない ほうがいいです

Giving Advice

🔍 What does it mean?

This pattern is used to give advice about what someone should or shouldn't do. V-たほうがいい = "you should do V." V-ないほうがいい = "you shouldn't do V." Compared to V-るといい (casual advice), ほうがいい carries a slightly stronger nuance — suggesting that not following the advice could lead to inconvenience or trouble.

✅ V-たほうがいいです (Should do ~)

厚あつい上着うわぎを持もって行いったほうがいいですよ。

You should bring a heavy jacket.

早はやく予約よやくしたほうがいいですよ。

You should book early.

❌ V-ないほうがいいです (Shouldn't do ~)

日曜日にちようびは行いかないほうがいいですよ。

You shouldn't go on Sunday.

お土産みやげは、ここで買かわないほうがいいですよ。

You shouldn't buy souvenirs here.

📐 Formation
V-た (past plain) + ほうがいいです = "should do V"

V-ない (negative plain) + ほうがいいです = "shouldn't do V"
📝 Travel Advice
あのレストランは人気にんきなので、予約よやくして行いったほうがいいですよ。
That restaurant is popular, so you should make a reservation before going.
💡 ほうがいい vs. るといい: MNN L16 taught V-るといい for casual, light suggestions: 「早はやく寝ねるといいですよ」("You might want to go to bed early"). ほうがいい is a stronger recommendation — implying there could be negative consequences if you don't follow it: 「早はやく予約よやくしたほうがいいですよ」("You really should book early" — or you might miss out).

MNN L16 V-るといいです (light suggestion)

MNN L16 taught V-るといい for mild suggestions. V-たほうがいい is the stronger version: it implies "you should do this, or there might be inconvenience." るといい is neutral advice; ほうがいい carries more urgency.

MNN L20 Plain form (タ形 / ナイ形)

ほうがいい connects to: タ形 (past) for positive advice (行った) and ナイ形 (negative) for negative advice (行かない). Both plain forms you learned in MNN L20.

ExpressionStrengthNuanceExample
