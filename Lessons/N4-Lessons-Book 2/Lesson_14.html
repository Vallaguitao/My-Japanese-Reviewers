Grammar 1

ナA-に / イA-く V

Adverbial Adjectives — "How" You Do It

🔍 What does it mean?

In English, adjectives become adverbs by adding "-ly": quiet → quietly, short → shortly. Japanese does the same thing! When you want to describe how an action is performed — cut hair short, clean tidily, speak quietly — you convert adjectives into their adverbial form. This form places the adjective before the verb to modify it, telling the listener not what you're doing but how you're doing it.

📐 Formation
イA: い → く + V → 短みじかく切きる = "cut short"

ナA: な → に + V → きれいに掃除そうじする = "clean tidily"
💬 At the Hair Salon
客：全体的ぜんたいてきに短みじかく切きってください。
Please cut it short all over.
美容師：はい、わかりました。前髪まえがみはどうしますか？
Sure. How about the bangs?
客：前髪まえがみはもう少すこし短みじかく切きってもらえますか？
Could you cut my bangs a little shorter?
📝 Clean Tidily
部屋へやはきれいに掃除そうじしてください。
Please clean the room tidily.
📝 Apply Early
できるだけ早はやく申もうし込こんでください。
Please apply as early as possible.
💡 Adjective → Adverb Conversion Rule:
イ-adj: 短みじかい → 短みじかく (short → shortly), 早はやい → 早はやく (early → early), 長ながい → 長ながく (long → long-ly)
ナ-adj: きれいな → きれいに (tidy → tidily), 静しずかな → 静しずかに (quiet → quietly)

MNN L8 ナA-adj + N / イA-adj + N

MNN L8 taught adjectives modifying nouns: きれいな部屋, 大きい犬. This lesson teaches adjectives modifying verbs — the adverbial form. Same adjectives, different target: noun → adjectival use; verb → adverbial use.

MNN L19 イA-く なります

MNN L19 used the く form for "become": 暑くなります ("it gets hot"). This is actually the same adverbial form! く attaches to verbs — whether なる (become), する (make), 切る (cut), or any other verb.

Grammar 1

Adj-く/に する — "Make It ~"

形容詞けいようしの副詞ふくし用法ようほう — 応用おうよう

🔍 Special Pattern: Adj + する = "Make It ~"

When you combine the adverbial form with する, it means "to make something [adjective]" — to actively change something's state. 短みじかくする doesn't just mean "do it shortly" — it means "make it short." This is extremely useful at salons: "make it shorter," "make it tidier," "make it quieter."

📐 "Make It ~" Pattern
イA-く + する → 短みじかくする = "make it short"

ナA-に + する → きれいにする = "make it clean/tidy"
AdjectiveAdverb Form+ V (how)+ する (make it)
短みじかい短みじかく短みじかく切きる (cut short)短みじかくする (make shorter)
長ながい長ながく長ながく伸のばす (grow long)長ながくする (make longer)
早はやい早はやく早はやく起おきる (wake up early)早はやくする (make it sooner)
きれい（な）きれいにきれいに掃除そうじする (clean tidily)きれいにする (make it clean)
静しずか（な）静しずかに静しずかに話はなす (speak quietly)静しずかにする (make it quiet)
📝 At the Salon — Make It Shorter
もう少すこし短みじかくしてください。
Please make it a little shorter.
📝 Make It Quiet
図書館としょかんでは静しずかにしてください。
Please be quiet (make it quiet) in the library.
💡 Three Uses of the Adverb Form:
✂️ How you do something: 短みじかく切きる (cut short)
🔄 Change of state: 短みじかくなる (become short) — MNN L19
✋ Make it that way: 短みじかくする (make it short) — this lesson

Grammar 2

Nのために、～

"For the Sake of ~ / For ~'s Benefit"

🔍 What does it mean?

Nのために describes who benefits from an action or the purpose behind it. When a community center says 外国がいこくの方かたのために、いろいろなサービスがあります, it means "For the benefit of people from other countries, there are various services." The person or purpose before のために is the reason the action exists.

📐 Formation
N + のために、～

N = the person who benefits OR the goal/purpose.

Grammar 2

Nのために — Two Uses

利益りえき（Benefit）と 目的もくてき（Purpose）
👥 Use A: Benefit — "For [someone]"

外国がいこくの方かたのために、いろいろなサービスがあります。

There are various services for people from other countries.

はじめての方かたのために、図書館としょかんの使つかい方かたをご説明せつめいします。

For first-time users, we'll explain how to use the library.

🎯 Use B: Purpose — "For [goal]"

将来しょうらいのために、がんばります。

I'm working hard for my future.

健康けんこうのために、毎日まいにち歩あるいています。

I walk every day for my health.

🔍 Common Patterns with のために

PatternExampleMeaning
家族かぞくのために家族かぞくのために働はたらいていますI work for my family
友ともだちのために友ともだちのためにケーキを作つくりましたI made a cake for my friend
自分じぶんのために自分じぶんのために時間じかんを使つかうUse time for yourself
子こどものために子こどものためのスペースA space for children
💡 のために vs のための N:
子こどものために（+ verb/sentence）→ "for children, [we do X]"
子こどものための スペース（+ noun）→ "a space for children"
Before a verb/sentence, use のために. Before a noun, use のための!
📝 Living Guide for Foreigners
これは、外国がいこくの方かたのための生活せいかつガイドブックです。
This is a living guidebook for people from other countries.

MNN L42 V-る ために

MNN L42 covers ために with verbs for purpose: 日本語を勉強するために日本に来ました ("I came to Japan to study Japanese"). This lesson focuses on N + のために — nouns expressing the beneficiary or purpose noun. Both share the core "for the sake of" meaning.

L12 (Irodori) V-る ように

Compare: ように = "so that ~" (for state/ability verbs: わかるように), ために = "for the purpose of / for the benefit of." ように focuses on the desired outcome; ために focuses on who or what benefits.

Bonus

📐 Purpose Expressions — ために vs ように

目的もくてきの表現ひょうげんまとめ

Japanese has several ways to express purpose or reason. You've now encountered two of the main ones across Irodori. Here's how they compare, along with a guide for when to use each one.

ExpressionUseExampleSource
N のためにBenefit / purpose noun家族かぞくのために働はたらくL14
V-る ためにIntentional purpose日本語にほんごを勉強べんきょうするために来きたMNN L42
V-る ようにDesired state / ability goalわかるように説明せつめいするL10 (Irodori)
🎯 ために — "In order to" (intentional)

健康けんこうのために毎日まいにち歩あるく。

Walk daily for [the purpose of] health.

🌟 ように — "So that" (desired outcome)

太ふとらないように毎日まいにち歩あるく。

Walk daily so that [I] don't gain weight.

💡 Quick Decision Guide:
👥 Who benefits? → N のために (for my family, for foreigners)
🎯 What's the purpose? → V-る ために (in order to study, in order to buy)
🌟 What state do you want? → V-る ように (so that you understand, so it doesn't break)
