Grammar 1

V-るまで、～

"Until ~" — Duration Until Completion

🔍 What does it mean?

You already know まで with nouns to mark endpoints in time or place: 2時じから4時じまで ("from 2 until 4 o'clock"). This lesson extends まで to connect with verbs — expressing "until an action happens/completes." It tells you how long something takes before a certain action or event is reached. The verb before まで uses dictionary form (V-る).

📐 Formation
V-る (dictionary form) + まで、～

The action before まで is the endpoint — "until [this action happens]"

💬 Online Shopping — Delivery Time
A：パソコンが直なおるまで、どのぐらいかかりますか？
How long will it take until my computer is fixed?
B：1週間しゅうかんぐらいだと思おもいます。
I think about a week.
📝 Quick Delivery!
手元てもとに届とどくまで、2日にちしかかかりませんでした。
It only took 2 days to reach me (until it arrived).
📝 Waiting at a Store
店みせの人ひとが来くるまで、ここで待まっていてください。
Please wait here until a staff member comes.
💡 N まで vs V-るまで: You already use noun + まで for time endpoints: 5時じまで（待まちます）= "I'll wait until 5:00." Now with verbs, the endpoint is an action: 届とどくまで = "until it arrives." Same concept, expanded to actions!

MNN L4 N から N まで

MNN L4 introduced まで with nouns for time ranges: 9時から5時まで ("from 9 to 5"). This lesson upgrades まで to work with verbs: V-るまで expresses "until [action happens]."

MNN L23 V-る とき

V-るとき marks "when" an action happens; V-るまで marks "until" an action happens. Both use dictionary form but express different time relationships. とき = point in time, まで = duration endpoint.

Grammar 2

V-やすいです / V-にくいです

"Easy to ~ / Hard to ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

These expressions describe how easy or difficult an action is to perform. When describing products, they're essential: この掃除機そうじきは使つかいやすいですよ ("This vacuum is easy to use!"). They connect to the verb's ます-stem (the verb without ます) and behave like イ-adjectives after that.

📐 Formation
V-ます stem + やすい = "easy to ~"

V-ます stem + にくい = "hard to ~"

使います → 使い + やすい = 使いやすい (easy to use). The result is an イ-adjective!

✅ やすい — Easy to ~

この説明書せつめいしょは、とてもわかりやすいですね。

These instructions are very easy to understand.

このペンは書かきやすいです。

This pen is easy to write with.

❌ にくい — Hard to ~

この掃除機そうじきは、重おもくて、使つかいにくいですね。

This vacuum is heavy and hard to use.

この字じは読よみにくいです。

This handwriting is hard to read.

Verbます-stemやすい (easy)にくい (hard)
使つかう使つかい使つかいやすい使つかいにくい
わかるわかりわかりやすいわかりにくい
見みる見み見みやすい見みにくい
書かく書かき書かきやすい書かきにくい
読よむ読よみ読よみやすい読よみにくい
壊こわれる壊こわれ壊こわれやすい壊こわれにくい
動うごかす動うごかし動うごかしやすい動うごかしにくい
💡 These become adjectives! Since やすい/にくい end in い, the result is an イ-adjective. This means you can use them just like any other adjective: 使いやすい掃除機 ("an easy-to-use vacuum"), 使いやすくて ("easy to use and…"). They follow all イ-adjective grammar rules!

MNN L44 V-やすい / V-にくい

MNN L44 (初級2) covers V-やすい/にくい. At the A2 level, the focus is on practical product descriptions: 使いやすい (easy to use), 壊れにくい (durable/hard to break), 動かしやすい (easy to move). These expressions are essential for smart shopping!

Grammar 3

N1とN2、どちらが～？/ N2のほうが～

Comparing Two Products

🔍 What does it mean?

When comparing two specific products — two vacuum cleaners, two phones, two brands — you use this pattern to ask which is better and to answer. In 初級しょきゅう1 Lesson 11, you learned Nのほうがいい ("I prefer N"). This lesson extends the comparison to describe specific qualities: which is lighter, easier to use, more durable, etc.

📐 Asking — "Which is ~?"
N1 と N2（と）、どちら／どっちが ～ですか？

どちら = polite, どっち = casual. Both mean "which one?"

📐 Answering — "N is more ~"
N2 のほうが （N1 より）～です

N1より = "compared to N1" (optional but adds clarity)

💬 Comparing Vacuum Cleaners
A：どっちが使つかいやすいですか？
Which is easier to use?
B：セーシバの掃除機そうじきのほうがスギシタより軽かるくて、動うごかしやすいですよ。
The Seshiba vacuum is lighter and easier to move than the Sugishita.
💬 Comparing Refrigerator Colors
A：冷蔵庫れいぞうこの色いろは、白しろとシルバーと、どっちが人気にんきですか？
Between white and silver, which refrigerator color is more popular?
B：白しろのほうがよく売うれています。でも、若わかい人ひとには、シルバーのほうが人気にんきがあります。
White sells better overall. But among young people, silver is more popular.
💡 Useful Shopping Comparison Vocabulary:
使つかいやすい (easy to use) · 軽かるい (lightweight) · デザインがいい (good design) · 機能きのうが多おおい (multi-function) · 手入ていれが簡単かんたん (easy care) · 壊こわれにくい (durable) · 人気にんきがある (popular)

MNN L12 N1 のほうが N2 より～

MNN L12 introduced basic comparison: 東京のほうが大阪より大きいです. This lesson applies the same structure to product comparisons using shopping-specific vocabulary like 使いやすい, 軽い, 機能が多い, etc.

L11 Irodori (初級1) N のほうがいい

初級1 L11 used のほうがいい for preferences ("I prefer N"). This lesson deepens comparisons by specifying what quality is better: のほうが使いやすい ("is easier to use"), のほうが軽い ("is lighter").

Grammar 3

Comparison Patterns — Deep Dive

比較ひかくパターン練習れんしゅう

🔍 Three Ways to Compare

When shopping for products, you'll encounter several comparison patterns. Let's look at how they work together naturally, building from questions to detailed answers.

📝 Pattern A: Simple "Which?"
AとBと、どっちがいいですか？
Between A and B, which is better?
📝 Pattern B: "Which?" with Specific Quality
AとBと、どっちが使つかいやすいですか？
Between A and B, which is easier to use?
📝 Pattern C: Answer with より (than)
AのほうがBより軽かるくて、動うごかしやすいですよ。
A is lighter and easier to move than B.
💡 Grammar 2 + Grammar 3 = Power Combo! Notice how V-やすい/にくい from Grammar 2 plugs perfectly into comparisons: どっちが使つかいやすいですか？ → Aのほうが使つかいやすいです。The two grammar points work hand-in-hand for product evaluation!

🎯 All 3 Points in Action

総合そうごう練習れんしゅう
🌟 At the Electronics Store
客：すみません。この掃除機そうじき、届とどくまでどのぐらいかかりますか？ ← ①V-るまで
Excuse me. How long until this vacuum arrives?
店員：2日にちぐらいです。こちらは軽かるくて動うごかしやすいですよ。 ← ②やすい
About 2 days. This one is light and easy to move.
客：あちらの掃除機そうじきと、どっちが壊こわれにくいですか？ ← ②にくい + ③比較
Compared to that one over there, which is more durable?
店員：こちらのほうが壊こわれにくいです。でも、あちらのほうが機能きのうが多おおいですね。 ← ③のほうが
This one is more durable. But that one has more features.
客：じゃあ、こちらをください。届とどくまで楽たのしみにしています！ ← ①V-るまで
Then I'll take this one. I'll be looking forward to it until it arrives!
✨ All 3 grammar points used naturally in a real electronics store conversation! Duration (まで), ease/difficulty (やすい/にくい), and product comparison (のほうが) work together seamlessly for smart shopping.

Here's a comprehensive collection of やすい/にくい pairs that are especially useful when shopping, reviewing products, or asking store staff for advice. Since these function as イ-adjectives, they can be used in comparisons (のほうが), with すぎます (L11), and in て-form chains.
