Grammar 1

V-るつもりです

Plans & Intentions

🔍 What does it mean?

V-るつもりです expresses a concrete plan or intention that you've decided on. It's stronger and more definite than V-たいです (want to do ~). Use it when you've made up your mind about something, especially travel plans.

📐 Formation
V-る (辞書形じしょけい) + つもりです = "I plan to V"

V-ない (ナイ形) + つもりです = "I don't plan to V"
✅ Positive — I plan to ~

明日あしたは、中禅寺湖ちゅうぜんじこに行いくつもりです。

I plan to go to Lake Chuzenji tomorrow.

❌ Negative — I don't plan to ~

時間じかんがないので、神社じんじゃには行いかないつもりです。

I don't have time, so I don't plan to visit the shrine.

💬 Travel Planning Dialogue
A：休やすみはどうしますか？
What are your plans for the day off?
B：箱根はこねに行いくつもりです。
I'm planning to go to Hakone.
💡 つもり vs. たい: V-たいです = "I want to do ~" (desire, wish). V-るつもりです = "I plan/intend to do ~" (decided, concrete). たい is about what you want; つもり is about what you've decided. 京都きょうとに行いきたいです ("I want to go") vs. 京都きょうとに行いくつもりです ("I'm planning to go").

MNN L13 V-たいです (desire)

MNN L13 taught たい for desires: 沖縄おきなわに行きたいです. つもり is a step beyond — you've moved from wanting to actually planning. Use たい when dreaming, つもり when booking!

MNN L18 V-る予定よていです (schedule)

予定 is even more concrete than つもり — it implies a fixed schedule/appointment. つもり = personal intention; 予定 = confirmed plan on the calendar.

Grammar 2

V-(られ)て / なくて、～

Impressions — "Glad / Sorry I could(n't) ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

This pattern uses the potential form in て-form to state what you could or couldn't do, followed by an emotion word expressing how you felt about it. It's perfect for sharing travel impressions: "I was glad I could visit ~" or "It's a shame I couldn't see ~."

📐 Formation
V-potential て-form、emotion word

V-potential なくて、emotion word

Potential て: 行ける → 行けて / 見られる → 見られて
Potential なくて: 行けない → 行けなくて / 見られない → 見られなくて

😊 Could do → Positive feeling

いろいろなところに行いけて、よかったです。

I'm glad I could visit many places.

きれいな夕日ゆうひが見みられて、感動かんどうしました。

I was moved that I could see a beautiful sunset.

😢 Couldn't do → Negative feeling

金閣寺きんかくじに行いけなくて、残念ざんねんでした。

I was disappointed I couldn't go to Kinkakuji.

ショーが見みられなくて、残念ざんねんでした。

I was disappointed I couldn't watch the show.

Grammar 3

S1 し、S2 し、～

Listing Reasons & Experiences

🔍 What does it mean?

～し is used to list multiple reasons or experiences, often building toward a conclusion or overall impression. In Irodori Lesson 2, you used し with adjectives (かっこいいし、歌うたも上手じょうずだし). This lesson adds し to potential-form verbs — listing things you could do on a trip.

📐 Formation
S1 (plain) + し、S2 (plain) + し、conclusion

し connects to both plain form and polite form. This lesson focuses on plain form with potential verbs.

📝 Example 1 — Hot Springs Trip
温泉おんせんにも入はいれたし、ゆっくりできたし、よかったです。
I could bathe in hot springs, and I could relax — it was great.
📝 Example 2 — Okinawa
沖縄おきなわ、いいですよね。海うみで泳およげるし、おいしいものも食たべられるし。
Okinawa is great, right? You can swim in the ocean, and eat delicious food too.
📝 Example 3 — Various Activities
海うみで泳およいだし、船ふねに乗のれたし、楽たのしかったです。
I swam in the ocean, rode a boat — it was fun.
💡 し with Potential Verbs: When listing travel experiences, し + potential verbs is incredibly natural: 入れたし ("I could enter [hot springs]"), 泳げたし ("I could swim"), 食べられたし ("I could eat"). The pattern piles up positive experiences leading to a happy conclusion like よかったです or 楽しかったです!

MNN L25 ～し (listing reasons)

MNN L25 introduced し for giving multiple reasons. This lesson expands し to work with potential verbs — listing things you could do rather than just describing qualities. The structure is the same, but the content shifts to travel experiences.

L2 Irodori ～し with adjectives

Irodori L2 used し with adjectives: かっこいいし、歌も上手だし. Now you're using し with verb potential forms: 泳げたし、食べられたし. Same connector, new content!

Grammar 4

V-る / V-ている / V-た とき、～

"When ~" — 3 Timings

🔍 What does it mean?

とき means "when" or "at the time of." The verb form before とき determines the precise timing relationship. This is crucial for storytelling about travel experiences — "When I was climbing...", "When I arrived..."

FormTimingMeaningExample
V-ているとき ⏳ During While V is happening 富士山ふじさんに登のぼっているとき、空そらが晴はれました。
While I was climbing Fuji, the sky cleared up.
V-るとき ⏭️ Just before Right before V happens 富士山ふじさんに行いくとき、バスに乗のりました。
When (about to) go to Fuji, I got on a bus.
V-たとき ✅ Right after Right after V happened 家いえに着ついたとき、疲つかれて動うごけませんでした。
When I arrived home, I was too tired to move.
📝 During — ているとき
道みちを歩あるいているとき、リスを見みました。
While walking down the road, I saw a squirrel.
📝 Right after — たとき
ウミネコが近ちかくに来きたとき、ちょっとこわかったです。
When a black-tailed gull came near me, I was a bit scared.
💡 Visual Timeline:
V-るとき = ⏭️ The main event happens before the action of V starts
V-ているとき = ⏳ The main event happens while V is ongoing
V-たとき = ✅ The main event happens after V is completed
The verb form tells you exactly WHERE on the timeline the second event occurs!

MNN L15 V-るとき / Adj とき / N のとき

MNN L15 introduced とき: 熱ねつがあるとき、この薬くすりを飲のんでください ("When you have a fever, take this medicine"). This lesson expands to show 3 different verb forms before とき, each changing the precise timing.

Grammar 5

[person] と [number] 人にんで

Companions & Group Size

🔍 What does it mean?

This pattern tells who you went with and how many people total were in the group. The number includes yourself. It's essential for talking about travel experiences — who came along and the group size.

📐 Formation
[person(s)] + と + [total number]人にんで

The number includes YOU. 友達と2人で = "with a friend, the 2 of us" (friend + you = 2).

📝 Example 1 — Two People
友達ともだちと2人ふたりでハウステンボスに行いって来きました。
I went to Huis Ten Bosch with a friend (the two of us).
📝 Example 2 — Three People
今度こんど、今田いまださんとバイさんと3人にんで日帰ひがえり旅行りょこうに行いきます。
Soon, Imada, Bai, and I (the three of us) are going on a day trip.
💡 Counting Tip: Always count yourself! 友達ともだちと2人で = you + 1 friend = 2 total. If you list two names like 田中さんとリンさんと3人で, that's Tanaka + Lin + you = 3. The と after each person lists them as companions.
💬 Asking About a Trip
A：旅行りょこうは誰だれと行いきましたか？
Who did you go on the trip with?
B：会社かいしゃの友達ともだちと3人にんで行いきました。
I went with work friends — three of us total.

MNN L5 ～と行いきます (with someone)

MNN L5 taught ～と for doing things with someone: 友達ともだちと映画えいがを見ます. This lesson adds the group size: 友達と2人で. The number + 人 + で specifies exactly how many people total.

MNN L11 Counters (numbers + 人にん)

MNN L11 covered the counter ～人: 1人(ひとり), 2人(ふたり), 3人(さんにん). Remember: 1人 and 2人 have special readings!

🌟 Model Conversation — Talking About a Trip to Nikko
A：日光にっこう、どうだった？
How was Nikko?
B：会社かいしゃの友達ともだちと3人にんで行いったんだけど、すごくよかったよ。 ← [person]と[number]人で
I went with work friends — three of us — and it was amazing.
A：いいね！何なにした？
Nice! What did you do?
B：紅葉もみじが見みられたし、温泉おんせんにも入はいれたし、楽たのしかった！ ← ～し、～し
I could see autumn leaves, and bathe in hot springs — it was fun!
B：道みちを歩あるいているときにリスも見みたよ。 ← V-ているとき
While walking down the road, I even saw a squirrel.
A：いいなぁ。東照宮とうしょうぐうは？
That's great. How about Toshogu shrine?
B：時間じかんがなくて行いけなくて、残念ざんねんだった。次つぎは行いくつもり！ ← V-なくて + つもり
I couldn't go because of time — disappointing. Next time I plan to go!

⏱️ とき Visual Timeline

3つの「とき」の違ちがい

📊 When does the main event happen?

The verb form before とき determines exactly when the second event (the main event) occurs relative to the first action. Here's a visual breakdown:

V-るとき 📦 → ⭐ main event → then V action starts
富士山ふじさんに行いくとき、バスに乗のった = Before going, I got on a bus
V-ているとき ⏳ → V action ongoing ⭐ main event happens during ←
歩あるいているとき、リスを見みた = While walking, I saw a squirrel
V-たとき ✅ → V action completed → ⭐ main event happens after
家いえに着ついたとき、疲つかれていた = When I arrived home, I was tired
💡 Quick Decision Rule: Ask yourself: "Is the action in progress, not yet started, or already done?"
In progress → ているとき  |  Not yet started → るとき  |  Already done → たとき

⚖️ Plans & Intentions Compared

ExpressionCertaintyNuanceExample
