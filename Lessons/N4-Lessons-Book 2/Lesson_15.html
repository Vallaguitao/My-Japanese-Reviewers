Grammar 1

V-たままです

"Left in the State of ~" — Unchanged State

🔍 What does it mean?

まま means "no change" — things remain as they are. When combined with a verb's た-form, V-たまま describes a situation where a state continues without being changed back, often when it should have been changed. The meeting room lights were left on when someone should have turned them off. It's commonly used for gentle warnings about wasted energy, forgotten actions, or situations left unresolved.

📐 Formation
V-た (ta-form) + まま（です）

The verb before まま is in past tense (た-form) — it describes the action that created the current unchanged state.

💬 Office — Energy Warning
A：会議室かいぎしつの電気でんきがついたままでしたよ。
The meeting room lights were left on, you know.
B：あ、すみません。忘わすれてました。
Oh, I'm sorry. I forgot.
📝 Window Left Open
窓まどが開あいたままでしたよ。
The windows were left open. (implying they should have been closed)
📝 Fell Asleep with AC On
エアコンをつけたまま、寝ねてしまいました。
I fell asleep with the air conditioner on. (energy waste!)
💡 Two Key Uses of V-たまま:
⚠️ Warning/complaint: 電気がついたままでしたよ ("The lights were left on" — gentle reproach)
🔄 Doing X while in state Y: エアコンをつけたまま、寝てしまいました ("Fell asleep with the AC on" — next action in that state)

MNN L46 V-たところです / V-たばかりです

MNN L46 covers expressions about the timing of completed actions. V-たまま is related but different — it's not about when an action was completed, but about a state that persists unchanged after an action. ところ = "just finished," ばかり = "just did," まま = "still in that state."

L11 Irodori V-てしまいました

V-たまま often pairs with てしまう for regrettable situations: エアコンをつけたまま、寝てしまいました ("I ended up falling asleep with the AC on"). Both express something unintended or regrettable.

Grammar 1

そのまま + More まま Patterns

まま の応用おうようパターン

🔍 そのまま — "As Is / Leave It"

そのまま is a standalone expression meaning "as it is" or "leave it as is." When someone asks if they should clean up or change something, you can say そのままでいいです ("It's fine as is"). This is the noun/pronoun version of the same まま concept — the current state is unchanged and that's OK.

💬 Office — Don't Clear the Table
A：テーブルの上うえ、片付かたづけましょうか？
Would you like me to clear the table?
B：あ、そのままでいいです。
No, you can leave it as is.

Grammar 2

V-るのにいいです

"Good for ~ing" — Suitability & Usefulness

🔍 What does it mean?

This expression explains what something is good for or suitable for. When you say マイストローは、ごみを減へらすのにいいんですよ, you're saying "Having your own straw is good for reducing waste." It's formed by adding の to the verb's dictionary form (making it a noun-like phrase), then に (for the purpose) and いいです (is good). It's perfect for talking about eco-friendly products and habits!

📐 Formation
V-る (dictionary form) + のに + いいです

Also works with: 使つかいます (is used for), 役立やくだちます (is useful for), 便利べんりです (is convenient for).

📝 Eco Tip: Reusable Straws
マイストローは、ごみを減へらすのにいいんですよ。
Having your own straw is good for reducing waste.
📝 Traditional Wrapping Cloth
風呂敷ふろしきは、荷物にもつを包つつむのに使つかいます。
Furoshiki is used for wrapping items.
📝 Old Cloth for Oil
古ふるい布ぬのは、油あぶらを捨すてるのに役立やくだちます。
Old cloth is useful for discarding oil.
💡 Nにいい vs V-るのにいい:
🌿 環境かんきょうにいい = "good for the environment" (N + にいい)
♻️ ごみを減へらすのにいい = "good for reducing waste" (V-る + のにいい)
The のに turns the verb phrase into a purpose that something is "good for." Think of の as making the verb into a noun-like concept.

MNN L38 V-るのは / V-るのが

MNN L38 covers nominalizing verbs with の: 泳ぐのが好きです ("I like swimming"). This lesson's V-るのにいい uses the same nominalization — turning a verb into a noun phrase — then adds に (for purpose) + いい (is good).

MNN L42 V-るのに（purpose）

MNN L42 also covers のに for purpose expressions. V-るのに + time/money/effort: この仕事を終わるのに3時間かかりました ("It took 3 hours to finish this work"). Same のに structure, different ending expression.

Grammar 2

V-るのに — Extended Patterns

のに + いろいろな表現ひょうげん

🔍 Beyond いいです — Other Endings

While いいです is the most common ending, V-るのに can pair with several expressions to describe suitability, use, and convenience. All share the same のに structure — only the ending changes.

PatternMeaningExample
V-るのにいいですgood for ~ingごみを減へらすのにいい
V-るのに使つかいますis used for ~ing荷物にもつを包つつむのに使つかう
V-るのに役立やくだちますis useful for ~ing油あぶらを捨すてるのに役立やくだつ
V-るのに便利べんりですis convenient for ~ing買かい物ものするのに便利べんり

🌿 Eco-Friendly Vocabulary: Nにいい

In environmental discussions, you'll also see the simpler N + にいい pattern — where a noun replaces the verb phrase. Both patterns describe "good for [purpose]."

🌿 Nにいい — Noun Purpose

環境かんきょうにいい

Good for the environment

省しょうエネにいい

Good for saving energy

♻️ V-るのにいい — Verb Purpose

ごみを減へらすのにいい

Good for reducing waste

電気でんきを節約せつやくするのにいい

Good for saving electricity

📐 State Expressions: まま vs ている vs てある

状態じょうたいの表現ひょうげん比較ひかく

You've now learned three ways to describe states in Japanese. Each has a different nuance — here's how they compare and when to use each one.

ExpressionLessonNuanceExample
V-ているMNN L14Neutral state observation電気でんきがついている
V-てあるL13Someone did it intentionally電気でんきがつけてある

🔄 V-ている — Just Observing

電気でんきがついている。

The lights are on. (neutral — no judgment)

⚠️ V-たまま — Left Unchanged

電気でんきがついたままでした。

The lights were left on. (should have been turned off!)

💡 Quick Decision Guide:
👀 Just observing a state? → V-ている ("The lights are on.")
✋ Someone did it on purpose? → V-てある ("The lights have been turned on [for a reason].")
⚠️ Left unchanged / should have changed? → V-たまま ("The lights were left on [wasteful!].")
