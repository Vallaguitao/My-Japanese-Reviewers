Grammar 1

[疑問詞ぎもんし] V-ばいいですか？

"What should I ~?" — Asking About Rules

🔍 What does it mean?

When you're unfamiliar with customs or etiquette — like what to wear to a Japanese wedding or what to bring to a funeral — you use V-ばいいですか to ask "What should I do?" This pattern uses the ば conditional form (if I do X, is it good?) to politely seek guidance about the appropriate course of action.

📐 Formation
[疑問詞ぎもんし] + V-ば + いいですか？

The ば-form is made from the dictionary form: Group 1 verbs change -u → -e + ば, Group 2 drop -る and add -れば.

💬 Asking About a Wedding
A：友達ともだちの結婚式けっこんしきには、何なにを着きて行いけばいいですか？
What should I wear to my friend's wedding?
B：ワンピースとか、ちょっときれいな服ふくを着きて行いけばいいと思おもいます。
I think it'd be nice to wear a dress or something a bit formal.
📝 Asking About a Funeral
お葬式そうしきには、何なにを持もって行いけばいいですか？
What should I bring to a funeral?

2 グループ-る → -れば着きる / 食たべる着きれば / 食たべれば
3 グループIrregularする / 来くるすれば / 来くれば

L5 Irodori ～たらいいですか

Lesson 5 used たら for personal advice: どこに泊まったらいいですか ("Where should I stay?"). ばいいですか is similar but used more for general customs and rules rather than personal advice. Both ask "What should I do?" but ば feels more like seeking the established norm.

L7 Irodori ～たら (conditional)

L7 covered たら as a conditional. ば is another conditional form — both can express "if," but ば is used for general/habitual conditions while たら is more versatile for specific one-time events.

Grammar 2+3

V-てはいけません / V-なくちゃ

Prohibition + Casual Obligation

🔍 Grammar 2: V-てはいけません / V-ちゃだめ — "Must not ~"

These expressions describe prohibitions — things that are not allowed. The formal version (てはいけません) is used for rules and official settings, while the casual version (ちゃだめ) is for warning friends or speaking casually. Both connect to the verb's て-form.

📜 Formal — てはいけません

信号しんごうが赤あかのときは、横断歩道おうだんほどうを渡わたってはいけませんよ。

You must not cross the crosswalk when the light is red.

💬 Casual — ちゃだめ

バスの中なかでは、話はなしちゃだめだよね。

You shouldn't talk on the bus.

この写真しゃしんは見みちゃだめ。

Don't look at this photo.

💡 Contraction: ては → ちゃ is a casual spoken contraction. 渡っては → 渡っちゃ. Same meaning, different register. ちゃだめ is how you'd warn a friend; てはいけません is for signs, rules, and formal settings.

🔍 Grammar 3: V-なくちゃ — Casual "Must do"

In Lesson 9, you learned なくちゃならない ("must do" — casual). This lesson goes one step further: drop ならない entirely to get the ultra-short なくちゃ. This is extremely common in everyday speech — it implies the obligation without finishing the full phrase.

📐 Formation
V-ない → V-なくちゃ (drop ならない)
📝 Self-Obligation
食器しょっきは自分じぶんで片付かたづけなくちゃ。
You must put away your own dishes.
📝 Time Pressure
もうこんな時間じかん！私わたし、そろそろ帰かえらなくちゃ。
Look at the time! I've got to get going soon.

MNN L14 V-なければなりません

MNN L14 taught the formal obligation. This lesson's なくちゃ is the most casual version: なければなりません → なくちゃならない (L9) → なくちゃ (L10).

MNN L15 V-てはいけません

MNN L15 introduced prohibition with てはいけません. This lesson adds the casual ちゃだめ contraction for everyday speech.

Grammar 4

V-る / V-ない ように、～

"In Order to ~" / "So That ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

ように expresses a purpose or goal — what you do so that a certain state is achieved. The part before ように describes the desired state (what you want to happen or avoid), and the part after describes the action you take. This is essential for explaining etiquette rationale: why certain rules exist.

📐 Formation
V-る + ように、[action] = "so that [V happens], [action]"

V-ない + ように、[action] = "so that [V doesn't happen], [action]"
📝 Positive Purpose — "So That ~"
次つぎの人ひとがすぐに席せきに座すわれるように、食器しょっきを片付かたづけます。
I clear the dishes so that the next person can sit down right away.
📝 Negative Purpose — "So That ~ Doesn't Happen"
トイレがつまらないように、紙かみはごみ箱ばこに捨すてます。
I throw paper in the trash so that the toilet doesn't get clogged.
📝 Clear Communication
誰だれでもわかるように、簡単かんたんな日本語にほんごで説明せつめいしてください。
Please explain in simple Japanese so that anyone can understand.
⚠️ Important Restriction: The verb before ように must express a state or ability (わかる, できる, 座れる, つまらない), NOT an intentional action. You cannot say ✗ 日本にほんの会社かいしゃで働はたらくように… because 働く is intentional. Instead use ために for intentional purpose.

MNN — N4 V-ように (purpose)

ように is a standard N4 grammar point. The key distinction at this level is: ように is for non-volitional/state verbs (things you can't directly control: わかる, できる, potential forms). For intentional verbs, use ために instead.

Grammar 5

V-(ら)れます — 受身うけみ②

Passive — Person Receives Action

🔍 What does it mean?

In Lesson 7, you learned passive for events (コンサートが開催される — event as subject). This lesson covers the other major use: when a person is the subject, receiving an action from someone else — being warned, praised, scolded, or called. The person who receives the action becomes the subject, and the doer is marked with に.

👤 Active — Doer as Subject

店長てんちょうが私わたしに「今度こんどから気きをつけて」と注意ちゅういしました。

The manager warned me to be careful from now on.

🎯 Passive — Receiver as Subject

私わたしは店長てんちょうに「今度こんどから気きをつけて」と注意ちゅういされました。

I was warned by the manager to be careful from now on.

🔑 Common Passive Verbs (Person Context)

📝 Being Praised

The teacher told me I did a good job.
📝 Being Scolded (Childhood Memory)

🎪 L7: Event as subject → コンサートが開催される (event focus)

The doer is marked with に in person-subject passives.

Grammar 6+7

S の？ / V-なきゃ

Casual Questioning + Ultra-Casual Obligation

🔍 Grammar 6: S (普通形ふつうけい) の？ — Casual ～んですか

In Lesson 9, you learned ～んですか to ask for explanations. In casual speech, this becomes ～の？ — it's the plain-form equivalent. When you're surprised by something a friend did, you'd say: お金かねを払はらわないで、ジュース飲のんだの？ ("You drank juice without paying?!")

📐 Formality Levels
Formal: ～んですか？ → Casual: ～の？
💬 Surprised Question
A：どうしたの？ 元気げんきないね。何なにかあったの？
What's wrong? You don't look well. Did something happen?
B：うん……。
Well...

🔍 Grammar 7: V-なきゃ — Ultra-Casual "Must"

This is the shortest casual obligation form — even shorter than なくちゃ. It contracts なければ → なきゃ (dropping ならない/いけない entirely). You'll hear this constantly among friends and in casual situations.

📐 Formation
V-ない → V-なきゃ
📝 You Gotta Pay!
ちゃんとお金かねを払はらってから飲のまなきゃ。
You've gotta pay before you drink!
📝 Lining Up Properly
だめ。電車でんしゃに乗のるときは、ちゃんと列れつに並ならばなきゃ。
No way. When you ride the train, you've gotta line up properly.
💡 Complete Obligation Ladder:
📜 なければなりません (most formal)
📝 なければならない
💬 なくちゃならない (L9)
🗣️ なくちゃ (L10 — casual)
⚡ なきゃ (L10 — ultra-casual)
