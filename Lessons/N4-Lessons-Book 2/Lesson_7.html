Grammar 1

～たら、～

Conditional — "If / When ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

～たら is one of Japanese's key conditional expressions, used to express "if ~" or "when ~." It covers two distinct situations: ① hypothetical conditions (you don't know if it will happen) and ② fixed/certain conditions (you know it will happen, and then something follows). In the context of local events, you'll use たら for both weather contingencies and scheduled sequences.

❓ ① Hypothetical (仮定かてい条件じょうけん)

雨あめが降ふったら、盆踊ぼんおどりは日曜日にちようびにやるよ。

If it rains, the Bon Odori will be on Sunday.

Uncertain — it may or may not rain. もし can be added: もし雨あめが降ったら...

✅ ② Fixed Condition (確定かくてい条件じょうけん)

盆踊ぼんおどりが終おわったら、いっしょに居酒屋いざかやに行いきましょう。

When the dance ends, let's go to an izakaya together.

Certain — the dance will end. Then you'll do the next thing.

📐 How to Form ～たら

Take the past plain form (た form) and add ら:

Past plain form (た/だ) + ら = ～たら
📝 Hypothetical — With もし
もし、わからないことがあったら、いつでも質問しつもんしてください。
If you have any questions, please ask anytime.
📝 Fixed — Time Condition
6時じになったら、仕事しごとを終おわりにしてください。
When it becomes 6 o'clock, please finish work.
📝 Hypothetical — ナA Conditional
暇ひまだったら、ちょっと手伝てつだってください。
If you're free, please help me a little.

MNN L25 ～たら (conditional)

MNN L25 introduced ～たら as a conditional. This Irodori lesson reinforces the same pattern while focusing on local event scenarios — weather contingency plans, time-based sequences, and community invitations.

MNN L23 ～とき (when)

Don't confuse たら with とき! とき (Lesson 6) describes "when" as a time frame (walking, arriving). たら is a conditional — "if/when [condition is met], then [result]." たら focuses on the trigger-result relationship.

L5 Irodori V-たらいいですか (seeking advice)

In Lesson 5 you used たら in 何で行ったらいいですか ("How should I get there?"). That was a fixed pattern. Now you see たら as a full conditional with its own clause!

Grammar 1

～たら Conjugation Table

「～たら」の作つくり方かた

📐 Full Formation Chart

～たら works with every word type — nouns, ナ-adjectives, イ-adjectives, and verbs. The key is to get the past plain form, then add ら. Here's the complete chart:

TypeAffirmative たらNegative たら
名詞めいし (Noun) N だったら
雨あめだったら = if it's rain		 N じゃなかったら
雨あめじゃなかったら = if it's not rain
ナ形容詞けいようし ナA だったら
大変たいへんだったら = if it's tough		 ナA じゃなかったら
大変たいへんじゃなかったら
イ形容詞けいようし イA-かったら
天気てんきが悪わるかったら = if the weather is bad		 イA-くなかったら
天気てんきが悪わるくなかったら
動詞どうし (Verb) V-たら
雨あめが降ふったら = if it rains		 V-なかったら

💡 The Universal Rule: Every たら form is just the past plain form + ら. If you already know the past tense, you know たら! 雨あめだった → 雨あめだったら. 降ふった → 降ふったら. 悪わるかった → 悪わるかったら. Just add ら!
💡 もし + たら: Adding もし ("if") at the beginning makes the hypothetical nature even clearer: もし雨あめが降ふったら... This is optional but very common, especially when the condition is truly uncertain.

Grammar 2

V-(ら)れます

受身うけみ — Passive Form for Events

🔍 What does it mean?

When announcing events like concerts, festivals, or flea markets, Japanese uses the passive form. This is because the focus is on the event itself (what's being held), not on who is organizing it. Think of it like English "A concert will be held" — you don't say who's holding it, you just announce it.

👤 Active — Agent as Subject

来週らいしゅう、「ひかりホール」で、（誰だれかが）コンサートを開催かいさいします。

(Someone) will hold a concert at Hikari Hall.

🎪 Passive — Event as Subject

来週らいしゅう、「ひかりホール」で、コンサートが開催かいさいされます。

A concert will be held at Hikari Hall.

🎌 Common Passive Verbs for Events

In event announcements, you'll see these three passive verbs most often. They all mean roughly "is held/takes place" but are used in slightly different contexts:

ActivePassiveUsageExample
開催かいさいする 開催かいさいされる Formal — concerts, conferences, large events コンサートが開催かいさいされます
行おこなう 行おこなわれる Formal — ceremonies, fireworks, official events 花火はなび大会たいかいが行おこなわれます
開ひらく 開ひらかれる General — flea markets, meetings, casual events フリーマーケットが開ひらかれます
📝 Formal Event Announcement
7月がつ14日か、「ひかりホール」において、コンサートが開催かいさいされます。
A concert will be held at Hikari Hall on July 14th.
📝 Fireworks Announcement
明日あした、18時じから、海岸かいがんで、花火はなび大会たいかいが行おこなわれます。
A fireworks festival will be held at the beach from 6 PM tomorrow.
📝 Recurring Event
月つきに1回かい、公園こうえんでフリーマーケットが開ひらかれます。
A flea market is held in the park once a month.
💡 Why Passive? Event announcements on flyers, signs, and news focus on what's happening — not who's organizing it. The passive naturally puts the event in the spotlight: 「コンサートが開催されます」 — the concert is the star, not the organizer.

MNN — N4 Passive form (受身形)

The passive form is typically an N4 grammar point (covered in MNN 初級2 Lesson 37). Irodori introduces it here in a limited, practical context — only for event announcements. You don't need to master all passive uses yet; just recognize 開催される, 行われる, and 開かれる for reading event notices.

Grammar 2

Passive Form Conjugation

受身形うけみけいの作つくり方かた

📐 How to Make the Passive Form

The passive conjugation follows a clear pattern for each verb group. Notice how Group 1 adds -(a)れる while Group 2 adds -られる — the same form as the potential! Context tells you which meaning is intended.

Group辞書形じしょけいRule受身形うけみけい
1 グループ 行おこなう -u → -a + れる 行おこなわれる (okona + wa + reru)
開ひらく 開ひらかれる (hirak + a + reru)
作つくる 作つくられる (tsukur + a + reru)
2 グループ 見みる -る → -られる 見みられる
3 グループ する / 開催かいさいする Irregular される / 開催かいさいされる
来くる 来こられる
⚠️ Passive vs. Potential — Same Form for Group 2!
For Group 2 verbs, passive and potential look identical: 見みられる can mean "is seen" (passive) OR "can see" (potential). Context tells you which:
🎪 コンサートが開催かいさいされる = "A concert is held" (passive — event focus)
🎵 ギターが弾ひける = "I can play guitar" (potential — ability focus)
💡 For This Lesson: You primarily need to recognize passive forms in event announcements, not produce them from scratch. Focus on: 開催される (formal events), 行われる (official events), 開かれる (casual/recurring events).

Grammar 3

V-(られ)ます

可能形かのうけい② — Personal Abilities

🔍 What does it mean?

In Lesson 5, you learned the potential form for what's possible at a place (usage ②): 「北海道ほっかいどうでは、新鮮しんせんなカニが食たべられます」 ("You can eat fresh crab in Hokkaido"). This lesson covers usage ① — personal ability, where the focus is on a person's skill or capability. In the local events context, this is about what people can do at community activities.

🗾 Usage ② — Place Possibility (L5)

北海道ほっかいどうでは、カニが食たべられます。

You can eat crab in Hokkaido. (place focus)

👤 Usage ① — Personal Ability (L7)

ギターが弾ひけます。

I can play guitar. (person's skill)

💬 Community Event — Asking About Skills
A：何なにか楽器がっきができますか？
Can you play any instruments?
B：ギターが弾ひけます。
I can play guitar.
📝 Anyone Can Do It!
盆踊ぼんおどりは簡単かんたんだから、誰だれでも踊おどれますよ。
Bon Odori dance is easy, so anyone can dance it!
💡 Two Uses of Potential — Same Form, Different Focus:
① Person as subject: 「（私わたしは）ギターが弾けます」— "I can play guitar" (my skill)
② Place as context: 「沖縄おきなわでは泳げます」— "You can swim in Okinawa" (place allows it)
The verb form is identical — the context tells you which type is being used!

MNN L18 V ことができます (ability)

MNN L18 taught ability with ことができます: ピアノを弾くことができます. The potential form is shorter and more natural: ピアノが弾ける. Both express ability, but the potential form is far more common in conversation.

L5 Irodori Potential form (可能形①)

Lesson 5 covered the conjugation rules (Group 1: -u→-e+る, Group 2: -られる, Group 3: irregular) and the を→が particle change. All the same rules apply here — the only difference is the context (person's ability vs. place possibility).

🌟 Model Conversation — Summer Festival Planning
A：今度こんどの土曜日どようび、夏祭なつまつりが開催かいさいされますよ。 ← 受身
A summer festival will be held this Saturday!
B：いいですね！雨あめが降ふったらどうなりますか？ ← たら (hypothetical)
Sounds great! What happens if it rains?
A：雨あめだったら、体育館たいいくかんでやりますよ。 ← たら (hypothetical)
If it rains, it'll be in the gym.
B：盆踊ぼんおどりは難むずかしいですか？
Is the Bon Odori dance difficult?
A：いいえ、簡単かんたんだから、誰だれでも踊おどれますよ！ ← 可能形 (ability)
No, it's easy — anyone can dance it!
A：お祭まつりが終おわったら、一緒いっしょに食たべに行いきましょう。 ← たら (fixed)
When the festival ends, let's go eat together.
✨ Notice the pattern: This conversation uses all 3 grammar points naturally — passive for the announcement, たら for both hypothetical (rain) and fixed (after it ends) conditions, and potential for personal ability. This is exactly how they work together in real Japanese community life!

Bonus

⚖️ Japanese Conditionals Compared

条件じょうけんの表現ひょうげん

📊 The 4 Japanese Conditionals

Japanese has multiple ways to say "if." As an A2 learner, you've now encountered two of them (たら and と). Here's a preview of the full landscape so you know where たら fits:

FormLevelMain UseExample
～たら ⭐ N4 (this lesson) Most versatile "if/when." Works for hypothetical & fixed conditions. 雨あめが降ったら、中止ちゅうしです
If it rains, it's cancelled
～と N4 Natural/automatic results. "When X happens, Y always follows." 春はるになると、桜さくらが咲さく
When spring comes, cherry blossoms bloom
～ば N4–N3 General condition. "If X, then Y (as a rule)." 安やすければ、買かいます
If it's cheap, I'll buy it
～なら N4–N3 "If it's the case that ~." Responds to new info. 日本にほんに行いくなら、京都きょうとがいい
If you're going to Japan, Kyoto is good
💡 For Now: Focus on たら — it's the most versatile and commonly used conditional in everyday conversation. It works for almost any "if/when" situation. You already know なら from Irodori Lesson 4 (Nなら for topic-specific advice). The others (と, ば) will come later!
Bonus

🔀 Passive vs. Potential — Disambiguation

受身うけみ vs 可能かのう

📊 When Forms Overlap

For Group 2 verbs and 来くる, the passive and potential forms look exactly the same: ～られる. For Group 1 verbs, they're different. Here's how to tell them apart:

GroupPassive (受身うけみ)Potential (可能かのう)Same?
G1 (開ひらく) 開ひらかれる (-a + れる) 開ひらける (-e + る) ✗ Different
G2 (見みる) 見みられる 見みられる ✓ Same!
G3 (する) される できる ✗ Different
G3 (来くる) 来こられる 来こられる ✓ Same!
💡 Context is King:
🎪 「コンサートが開催かいさいされる」 → Event is subject → Passive (is held)
🎵 「ギターが弾ひける」 → Person's ability → Potential (can play)
📺 「ここから富士山ふじさんが見みられる」 → Could be either! But the meaning "can see" (potential) is more natural here.
