Grammar 1

V-(よ)うと思おもいます

Volitional Form + "I Think I'll ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

This expression describes your future hopes, plans, and intentions. By combining the 意向形いこうけい (volitional form) with と思おもいます, you express what you're thinking of doing: "I think I'll start my own company." Use と思おもいます for a fresh intention, and 思おもっています for a plan you've been thinking about for a while.

📐 Volitional Form Conjugation
Group 1: -u → -oう: 働はたらく → 働はたらこう, 買かう → 買かおう

Group 2: -る → -よう: 見みる → 見みよう

Group 3: する → しよう, 来くる → 来こよう
📝 Future Company
将来しょうらいは、タイで自分じぶんの会社かいしゃを作つくろうと思おもいます。
I'm thinking of starting my own company in Thailand in the future.
📝 Settled Plan — 思っています
将来しょうらい、ツアーガイドになろうと思おもっています。
I've been thinking about becoming a tour guide. (settled plan)
💡 と思おもいます vs 思おもっています:
💭 と思おもいます = fresh intention ("I think I'll ~" — just decided)
🧠 思おもっています = ongoing plan ("I've been thinking about ~" — settled since a while ago)

MNN L31 V-(よ)うと思おもっています

MNN L31 covers volitional + と思う for expressing intentions. This is the same grammar point, applied here to discussing life dreams and future plans in the context of personal reflection.

MNN L31 Volitional form

The volitional form (意向形) was introduced in MNN L31. Group 1: -u→-oう, Group 2: -る→-よう. The pronunciation of -oう is オー (long vowel).

Grammar 2

V-るために、～

"In Order to ~" — Purpose for Goals

🔍 What does it mean?

V-るために describes the purpose behind an action — why you're doing something. 日本にほんに住すむために、もっと日本語にほんごをがんばろうと思おもいます = "In order to live in Japan, I think I'll work harder at Japanese." It uses dictionary-form verbs and specifically works with volitional verbs (actions you can control): 就職しゅうしょくする, 会社かいしゃを作つくる, 結婚けっこんする.

📐 Formation
V-る (dictionary) + ために、～
📝 Studying for a Dream
日本料理にほんりょうりの料理人りょうりにんになるために、勉強べんきょうしています。
I've been studying in order to become a Japanese cuisine chef.
📝 Saving for a Store
将来しょうらい、自分じぶんの店みせを持もつために、貯金ちょきんしています。
I've been saving money in order to have my own shop in the future.
💡 ために vs ように — The Critical Distinction:
🎯 ために + controllable/volitional verbs: 就職しゅうしょくするために (in order to get employed — you control this)
🌟 ように + uncontrollable/potential verbs: 日本語にほんごが上手じょうずになるように (so that my Japanese improves — you can't directly control the outcome)
ために = purpose YOU do. ように = desired state you hope FOR.

MNN L42 V-るために

MNN L42 covers ために for purpose. This lesson applies it specifically to life goals and future dreams.

MNN L36 V-ように

ように expresses desired states with non-volitional/potential verbs. Cannot be used with volitional verbs that express your own will — that's ために's job.

Irodori L14 Nのために

Irodori L14 taught Nのために (for someone's sake): 家族のために. This lesson adds V-るために (in order to do ~) — same ために, different connection (noun vs verb).

Grammar 3

V-てあげます

"I'll Do ~ For Someone" — Giving a Favor

🔍 What does it mean?

V-てあげる means doing something for another person's benefit. The speaker (or someone else) performs an action that benefits the receiver. 両親りょうしんに家いえを建たててあげるつもりです = "I plan to build a house for my parents." The receiver is marked with に. Important: Don't use てあげる when the receiver is your superior — it sounds pushy and rude!

📐 Formation
V-て + あげます (Receiver に / のために)
📝 Building for Parents
両親りょうしんに、家いえを建たててあげるつもりです。
I plan to build a house for my parents.
📝 Showing Around Town
後輩こうはいに、この町まちのいろいろなお店みせを案内あんないしてあげました。
I showed my junior colleague around various shops in town.
⚠️ Don't use てあげる with superiors! Saying 先生せんせいに教おしえてあげます sounds like "I'll do you the favor of teaching you, teacher" — very rude! Use plain て-form instead for superiors.
Grammar 4

V-てもらいます

"I Receive the Favor of ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

V-てもらう expresses that you received a beneficial action from someone. The focus is on the receiver's perspective — you're grateful for what someone did. 旅行りょこうのとき、友ともだちに写真しゃしんを撮とってもらいました = "During the trip, I had my friend take photos for me." The giver is marked with に. For requests: V-てもらえませんか？ For superiors: V-ていただきました.

📐 Formation
(Giver に) V-て + もらいます
📝 Friend Took Photos
旅行りょこうのとき、友ともだちに写真しゃしんを撮とってもらいました。
During the trip, I had my friend take photos for me.
📝 Superior — ていただきました
山田やまださんには、たくさんのことを教おしえていただきました。
Yamada-san taught me many things. (respectful — to a superior)

MNN L17 もらいます (receiving objects)

MNN L17 taught receiving things: 友だちにもらいました ("I received from a friend"). This lesson extends it to receiving actions: 友だちに写真を撮ってもらいました.

MNN L9 V-てもらえませんか (requests)

Book 1 L9 taught V-てもらえませんか for polite requests. This is the request form of てもらう.

Grammar 5

V-てくれます

"Someone Does ~ For Me"

🔍 What does it mean?

V-てくれる means someone does something beneficial for the speaker. The focus shifts to the giver — you're expressing gratitude for their kindness. 山内やまうちさんは、私わたしにたくさん日本語にほんごを教おしえてくれました = "Yamauchi-san taught me a lot of Japanese (and I'm grateful)." The giver is the subject (が), and 私わたしに is often omitted since it's understood.

📐 Formation
Giver は/が V-て + くれます
📝 Teaching Japanese
山内やまうちさんは、私わたしにたくさん日本語にほんごを教おしえてくれました。
Yamauchi-san taught me a lot of Japanese. (grateful perspective)
📝 Someone Found My Wallet
だれかが、私わたしの財布さいふを拾ひろって交番こうばんに届とどけてくれました。
Someone picked up my wallet and took it to a police station for me.
💡 てもらう vs てくれる — Same Event, Different Focus:
👤 友ともだちに教おしえてもらいました = "I had my friend teach me" (MY perspective — I received)
👥 友ともだちが教おしえてくれました = "My friend taught me" (THEIR kindness — they gave)
Both express gratitude, but もらう focuses on the receiver, くれる on the giver.
