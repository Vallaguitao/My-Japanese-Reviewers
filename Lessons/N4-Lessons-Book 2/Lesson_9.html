Grammar 1

～んですか？

Asking for Explanation

🔍 What does it mean?

～んですか asks for a detailed explanation about a situation or reason you don't know well. It signals genuine curiosity — "Tell me about it!" When you hear about 成人せいじんの日ひ for the first time, you'd naturally ask: 何なにをするんですか？ ("What do people do?"). It's formed by adding んですか to the plain form of the sentence.

📐 Formation
V/イA plain form + んですか？

ナA/N + な + んですか？
💬 Learning About Coming-of-Age Day
A：成人せいじんの日ひは、何なにをするんですか？
What do people do on Coming-of-Age Day?
B：成人式せいじんしきの着物きもの、高たかそうですね。自分じぶんで、買かうんですか？
The ceremony kimono looks expensive. Do you buy it yourself?
💡 んです vs ですか: Plain ですか just asks a question. んですか shows you're curious about the background/reason and want an explanation. It's like adding "so, tell me…" to your question.

MNN L26 ～んですが / ～んですか

MNN L26 introduced んです for giving/requesting explanations. んですか asks "what's the deal?" while んですが sets up a request or context. This lesson focuses on the question form for cultural curiosity.

Grammar 2+3

N しか + neg / N だけ

"Only" — Two Flavors

🔍 Both mean "only" — but with different feelings

Japanese has two main ways to say "only." しか always pairs with a negative verb and carries a sense of disappointment or frustration that the amount is less than expected. だけ is neutral — it simply states a limitation without emotional weight.

😔 しか + negative = "only" (disappointed)

乗のり物ものに3つしか乗のれませんでした。

I could only ride 3 rides. (was hoping for more!)

財布さいふにお金かねが500円えんしかありません。

I only have 500 yen in my wallet. (not enough!)

📋 だけ = "only" (neutral fact)

今年ことしは、1日にちだけ実家じっかに帰かえりました。

This year, I went home for only 1 day. (just stating fact)

資料しりょうのこのページだけ、コピーしてください。

Please copy only this page of the document.

💡 Key Difference:
😔 しか + negative verb = "only (and it's not enough!)" — expresses feelings of limitation
📋 だけ + positive verb = "only (and that's fine)" — neutral statement of fact
しか must use a negative verb. だけ uses a positive verb.
📝 しか can express POSITIVE feelings too!
10分ぷんしか待まちませんでした。
I only waited 10 minutes! (surprisingly short — happy about it!)

MNN L23 しか + negative

MNN L23 introduced しか for limited amounts: 英語しか話せません (I can only speak English). The negative verb is mandatory.

MNN L15 だけ (only)

MNN L15 used だけ for neutral limitation. Remember: だけ is emotionally neutral; しか carries weight.

Grammar 4

V-たりして、～

Listing Representative Examples

🔍 What does it mean?

V-たりして is used to list activities as representative examples — "I did things like shopping" (implying there was more). It's different from し (Lesson 6) which lists reasons. たりして lists sample activities to give a general picture of what happened.

📐 Formation
V-たり（、V-たり）して、～

Can list one or more examples. Even one example + して implies "among other things."

💬 How Was Your Holiday?
A：休やすみはどうでしたか？
How was your day off?
B：家いえで音楽おんがくを聞きいたりして、のんびり過すごしました。
I listened to music and stuff, and spent the day relaxing.
📝 Shopping & More
友達ともだちといっしょに買かい物ものをしたりして、楽たのしかったです。
I did things like going shopping with friends — it was fun.

MNN L16 V-たり、V-たりします

MNN L16 introduced たり for listing activities: ジョギングしたり、ヨガをしたりしています. This lesson shows that even one example + たりして works as a representative listing.

Grammar 5

V-なくちゃならない

Casual "Must Do ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

This is a casual spoken form of ～なければならない ("must do"). During New Year's, you have to give otoshidama to nieces and nephews — it's an obligation! The なくちゃ form is what you'll hear in everyday conversation.

📐 Formation
V-ない → V-なくちゃならない

Change ない → なくちゃならない. It's a casual contraction of なければならない.

📝 New Year's Obligation
甥おいや姪めいにお年玉としだまをあげなくちゃならないし、大変たいへんでした。
I had to give New Year's money to my nephews and nieces — it was tough.

StandardV-なければならないWriting, polite conversation
CasualV-なくちゃならないDaily conversation ⭐ This lesson

MNN L14 V-なければなりません

MNN L14 taught the formal "must" pattern: 役所に行かなければなりません. なくちゃならない is its casual spoken cousin — same meaning, more natural in conversation.

Grammar 6+7

V-れます / 人ひとが多おおい

Potential ③ + People Expressions

🔍 Grammar 6: V-れます — ら抜ぬき言葉ことば (ら-dropped words)

For Group 2 verbs, the standard potential is ～られる. But in casual speech, many Japanese people drop the ら: 食べられる → 食たべれる. This is called ら抜ぬき言葉ことば. While some consider it grammatically incorrect, it's extremely common in everyday speech.

DictionaryStandard Potentialら-dropped (casual)
見みる見られる見れる
食たべる食べられる食べれる
来くる来られる来れる
📝 ら-dropped Example
すみません。明日あした、用ようがあって、仕事しごとに来これません。
Sorry, I have errands tomorrow and can't come to work.

🔍 Grammar 7: S + 人ひとが多おおい / 人ひともいます

To describe what many people or some people do, use plain form + 人が多い ("many people") or 人もいます ("some people also"). These are essential for describing cultural customs and trends.

📐 Formation
S (plain form) + 人ひとが多おおい = "many people [do S]"

S (plain form) + 人ひともいます = "some people also [do S]"
📝 Cultural Trends
春節しゅんせつは、田舎いなかに帰かえる人ひとが多おおいです。
During the Lunar New Year, many people go back to their hometown.
📝 Some People Do Differently
最近さいきんは、海外かいがいに旅行りょこうする人ひともいます。
Recently, some people also travel abroad.

L5 Irodori Potential form (可能形)

L5 covered standard potential. ら抜き is the casual spoken version for Group 2/3 verbs. Know both forms!

Grammar 8+9

V-ながら / N によって

Simultaneous Actions + "Depending on"

🔍 Grammar 8: V-ながら — "While doing ~"

V-ながら expresses two actions done simultaneously by the same person. The ながら action is secondary; the main action comes after. During the Songkran festival, people 歩あるきながら水みずをかけ合あう — they splash water on each other while walking.

📐 Formation
V-ます stem + ながら、main action

Take the ます form, remove ます, add ながら: 歩きます → 歩き + ながら

📝 Festival
お祭まつりでは、歌うたを歌うたいながら、みんなで踊おどります。
At the festival, everyone dances while singing.
📝 New Year's
お正月しょうがつは、食事しょくじをしながら、両親りょうしんといろいろ話はなしました。
During New Year's, I talked about various things with my parents while having a meal.

🔍 Grammar 9: N によって — "Depending on ~"

によって expresses that something varies or changes depending on a certain factor. Festival dates depend on the year; customs depend on the family or country.

📐 Formation
N + によって + 違ちがいます / 変かわります / いろいろあります
📝 Varies by Year
レバランは、年としによって違ちがいます。
Lebaran varies depending on the year.
💬 New Year's Cooking
A：お正月しょうがつは、どんな料理りょうりを作つくりますか？
What kind of dishes do people make for New Year's?
B：家いえによって違ちがいますね。
It depends on the family.

MNN L20 V-ながら (simultaneous)

MNN L20 introduced ながら: 音楽を聞きながら勉強します. Same pattern — ます stem + ながら. The secondary action attaches ながら; the main action follows.
