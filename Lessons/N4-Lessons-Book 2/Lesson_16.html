Grammar 1

V 命令形めいれいけい / V-るな

Imperative & Prohibition — Emergency Commands

🔍 What does it mean?

These are the most direct, forceful ways to give instructions in Japanese. The 命令形めいれいけい (imperative form) commands someone to do something: 急いそげ！ ("Hurry!"), 逃にげろ！ ("Run!"). The prohibition form V-るな commands someone not to do something: 走はしるな！ ("Don't run!"). These forms are stronger than て-form instructions and are used in emergencies, sports cheering, and urgent warnings.

📐 Formation — Imperative (Do it!)
Group 1: -u → -e: 行いく → 行いけ, 待まつ → 待まて

Group 2: -る → -ろ: 逃にげる → 逃にげろ

Group 3: する → しろ, 来くる → 来こい
📐 Formation — Prohibition (Don't!)
All groups: V-dictionary + な: 走はしるな, 見みるな, するな, 来くるな
GroupDictionary命令形めいれいけい禁止きんし (V-るな)
1行いく行いけ行いくな
1待まつ待まて待まつな
1急いそぐ急いそげ急いそぐな
2逃にげる逃にげろ逃にげるな
2集あつまる*集あつまれ集あつまるな
3するしろするな
3来くる来こい来くるな

*集あつまる is Group 1 despite ending in -る (the vowel before る is あ, not い/え)

🚨 Imperative — Emergency

急いそげ。こっちに集あつまれ。

Hurry up. Gather over here.

🏟️ Cheering — Sports

行いけ！がんばれ！負まけるな！

Go for it! Keep it up! Don't lose!

📝 Emergency — Don't Go There
危あぶないから、そっちに行いくな！
It's dangerous — don't go over there!

MNN L14 V-て ください (polite request)

MNN L14 taught て-form requests: 走ってください (Please run). The imperative form 走れ！ carries the same instruction but is much more direct and forceful. Use て-form for everyday politeness, imperative for emergencies and sports.

MNN L17 V-ないで ください (polite prohibition)

MNN L17 taught polite negative requests: 走らないでください (Please don't run). The prohibition form 走るな！ is the blunt emergency version. Same meaning, vastly different tone.

Grammar 2

V-ないようにしてください

"Please Try Not to ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

This is a polite way to ask someone to make an effort not to do something. Unlike V-ないでください (a direct "please don't"), this expression acknowledges that avoiding the action requires effort: "Please try to not inhale smoke." It's commonly used in evacuation drills and safety instructions, where you're asking people to be careful and mindful.

📐 Formation
V-ない + ようにしてください

ように = "so that ~" + してください = "please do" → "please make it so that you don't ~"

📝 Evacuation — Avoid Smoke
できるだけ煙けむりを吸すわないようにしてください。
Please try not to inhale smoke as much as possible.
📝 Evacuation — Don't Trip
逃にげるときに、あわてて転ころばないようにしてください。
When evacuating, please try not to trip in a hurry.
📝 Safety — Don't Go Out Alone
夜よるは、1人ひとりで外そとに出でないようにしてください。
Please try not to go outside alone at night.
💡 Politeness Scale for "Don't ~":
🔴 走はしるな！ — Blunt command (emergency only)
🟡 走はしらないでください — Polite request
🟢 走はしらないようにしてください — Gentle: "please try not to" (safety instructions)

MNN L36 V-ように、～

MNN L36 covers ように for goals: 日本語が上手になるように、毎日勉強しています ("I study daily so that my Japanese improves"). V-ないようにする = the negative version: "make an effort so that [something doesn't happen]."

Irodori L12 V-ないようにしています

Irodori Book 1 L16 taught the personal habit version: 食べすぎないようにしています ("I try not to eat too much"). This lesson changes しています to してください — making it a request to others rather than a personal habit.

Grammar 3

V-(ら)れなくなります

"Become Unable to ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

This expression describes a change to a state where something becomes impossible. In disaster contexts, it appears constantly: doors that can't open anymore, trains that stop running, supplies that can't reach stores. It combines the negative form of verbs with なくなります ("come to not ~" = "become unable to"). It works with both intransitive verbs (state change) and potential forms (ability loss).

📐 Formation
Intransitive V: V-ない → V-なく + なります

Potential V: V-(ら)れない → V-(ら)れなく + なります

ない → なく (adverb form) + なる (become) = "come to not ~" / "become unable to ~"

📝 Earthquake — Doors Won't Open
ドアが開あかなくなると、外そとに逃にげられなくなります。
If doors won't open, people become unable to escape outside.
📝 Supply Disruption
地震じしんの影響えいきょうで、水みずや食たべ物ものが届とどかなくなりました。
Due to the earthquake, water and food stopped being delivered.
📝 Typhoon — Trains Unavailable
台風たいふうが近ちかづいて、電車でんしゃが利用りようできなくなります。
With the typhoon approaching, trains become impossible to use.
💡 Remember MNN L19! なります expresses change. In MNN L19 you learned 暖あたたかくなります ("becomes warm"). Here, the same なります combines with ない: 開あかなくなります = "comes to not open" = "stops opening."

MNN L19 Adj-く なります

MNN L19 taught change of state: 暖かくなります. Here, ない (which behaves like an イ-adjective) → なく + なります. Same grammar, applied to negation!

MNN L27 Potential form V-(ら)れます

MNN L27 introduced potential forms. Combining potential negative + なります gives you "become unable to": 逃げられない + なります = 逃げられなくなります.

Grammar 4

S ても、～

"Even If ~" — Adversative Conditional

🔍 What does it mean?

～ても means "even if / even though" — the result holds true regardless of the condition. 大おおきな地震じしんが来きても、あわてて動うごかないでください = "Even if a large earthquake comes, please don't move in a panic." It contrasts with ～たら ("if"), because with ても the result doesn't change regardless of the condition.

📐 Formation
Verbs: V-ても (て-form + も): 来きても

イ-adj: ～くても: 寒さむくても

ナ-adj/N: ～でも: 大変たいへんでも, 台風たいふうでも
📝 Earthquake Safety
大おおきな地震じしんが来きても、あわてて動うごかないでください。
Even if a large earthquake comes, please don't move in a panic.
📝 We Have Supplies
1週間分しゅうかんぶんの水みずと食料しょくりょうがありますから、断水だんすいしても、しばらくはだいじょうぶです。
We have a week's worth of water and food, so even if the water stops, we'll be fine for a while.
🔄 ～たら — "If/When" (condition matters)

雨あめが降ふったら、イベントは中止ちゅうしになります。

If it rains, the event will be canceled. (rain changes the outcome)

💪 ～ても — "Even if" (result unchanged)

雨あめが降ふっても、イベントは開催かいさいされます。

Even if it rains, the event will be held. (rain doesn't change outcome)

MNN L25 V-ても

MNN L25 covers V-ても for concession. Irodori L13 extended this with 疑問詞+V-ても (no matter what/where/when). This lesson applies it to disaster contexts: "even if X happens, do Y."

MNN L23 V-たら

MNN L23 taught たら conditionals. The key distinction: たら = the condition affects the result; ても = the result holds despite the condition.

Grammar 5

V（普通形ふつうけい）かどうか、～

"Whether or Not ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

かどうか expresses uncertainty about whether something is true or not. 店みせが開あいているかどうか、わからない = "I don't know whether the store is open or not." Unlike 疑問詞+か (which uses question words), かどうか works with yes/no type questions — is it or isn't it? The verb before かどうか uses plain form.

📐 Formation
V-plain form + かどうか、わかりません / 知しっていますか

かどうか = "whether or not." Often followed by わかりません, 知っていますか, 教えてください.

📝 Store Open?
今いま、スーパーに行いっても、水みずが売うってるかどうか、わかりません。
Even if I go to the supermarket now, I don't know whether they're selling water or not.
📝 Water Supply Truck
明日あした、給水車きゅうすいしゃが来くるかどうか、知しっていますか？
Do you know whether a water supply truck is coming tomorrow?
💡 かどうか vs 疑問詞+か:
❓ 何時なんじからか、わかりますか？ — "Do you know what time from?" (疑問詞 = specific info sought)
🤔 開あいているかどうか、わかりません — "I don't know whether it's open or not" (yes/no uncertainty)

MNN L40 疑問詞+か

MNN L40 covers embedded questions with question words: 何時に来るか、わかりません. This lesson's かどうか is for yes/no questions without question words: 来るかどうか、わかりません ("whether they'll come or not").

Irodori L8 疑問詞+か

Irodori L8 used 疑問詞+か: フリーマーケットは何時からか、わかりますか？. This lesson teaches the non-interrogative version: かどうか — for when you don't know IF something is true, rather than asking for specific details.
