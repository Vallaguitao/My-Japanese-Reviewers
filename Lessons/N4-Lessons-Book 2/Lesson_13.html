Grammar 1

V-(ら)れます — 受身うけみ④

Passive for Creations & Objects

🔍 What does it mean?

This is the fourth and final type of passive in the Irodori series. When describing buildings, artworks, tools, and other created objects, the object becomes the subject: "This building was built two years ago." The creator can optionally be mentioned using （N）によって ("by [creator]"). This passive is used constantly in museums, guides, signs, and descriptions of public facilities.

📐 Formation
[Object] は (N によって) V-(ら)れました

によって = "by [agent/creator]" — optional but common for specifying who made something.

👤 Active — Creator as Subject

（誰だれかが）この建物たてものを2年前ねんまえに建たてました。

(Someone) built this building 2 years ago.

🏛️ Passive — Creation as Subject

この建物たてものは、2年前ねんまえに建たてられました。

This building was built 2 years ago.

📝 Artwork with Creator (によって)
「太陽たいようの塔とう」は、1970年ねんに岡本太郎おかもとたろうによって作つくられた作品さくひんです。
The "Tower of the Sun" is an artwork created by Okamoto Taro in 1970.
📝 Community Center
この交流こうりゅうプラザは、市民しみん講座こうざやボランティア教室きょうしつなどに使つかわれています。
This community plaza is used for public lectures and volunteer classes.

作つくる作つくられるto be made/created

MNN L37 Passive form

MNN L37 covers passive voice comprehensively. This lesson's focus — objects/creations as passive subjects — is very common in written Japanese, museum descriptions, and guidebooks. によって is the formal way to mark the creator/agent.

Grammar 2

V-てあります

Resultant State from Intentional Action

🔍 What does it mean?

V-てあります describes a current state that exists because someone intentionally did something. When you see いろいろな資料しりょうを展示てんじしてあります, it means materials "are on display" — someone deliberately placed them there, and they remain in that state. This is different from V-ている, which simply describes a state without implying intentional action.

📐 Formation
N が/を V-てあります

Uses transitive verbs (他動詞) — verbs where someone does the action on purpose.

🔄 V-ている — Simple State

電気でんきがついています。

The lights are on. (just stating the current situation)

Uses intransitive verb つく (turn on by itself)

✋ V-てある — Someone Did It

電気でんきがつけてあります。

The lights are turned on. (someone turned them on purposely)

Uses transitive verb つける (turn on deliberately)

V-ている (Intransitive)V-てある (Transitive)
ドアが開あいているドアが開あけてある
ドアが閉しまっているドアが閉しめてある
電気でんきがついている電気でんきがつけてある
電気でんきが消きえている電気でんきが消けしてある
📝 Art Displayed at Community Center
入口いりぐちの壁かべには、住民じゅうみんの作品さくひんが飾かざってあります。
Artworks by residents are displayed on the walls by the entrance.
📝 Pamphlets Available
多言語たげんごのパンフレットを用意よういしてあります。
Pamphlets in multiple languages have been prepared (are available).

MNN L30 V-てあります

MNN L30 introduced V-てあります. The key insight: ている describes a state using intransitive verbs (the door is open), while てある describes a state using transitive verbs (someone opened the door, and it remains open). Both describe current states, but てある implies someone's intention.

Grammar 3

N1だけじゃなくて、N2も～

"Not Only ~, But Also ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

This expression is used to emphasize that both N1 and N2 share the same quality or action — it's not limited to just one. When a library says 本だけじゃなくて、CDも借りられます, they're emphasizing the range of services: "You can borrow not just books, but CDs too!" It adds a pleasant surprise or emphasis about breadth of service.

📐 Formation
N1 だけじゃなくて、N2 も ～

Formal: N1 だけでなく、N2 も ～
📝 Library Services
本ほんだけじゃなくて、CDとか、DVDもただで借かりられます。
You can borrow not only books, but also CDs and DVDs for free.
📝 Open to Everyone
このフリースペースは、子こどもだけじゃなくて、大人おとなも利用りようできます。
This free space can be used not only by children, but also by adults.
📝 Open on Holidays Too
交流こうりゅうプラザは、平日へいじつだけでなく、休やすみの日ひも開あいています。
The community plaza is open not only on weekdays, but also on holidays.
💡 だけじゃなくて vs だけでなく: じゃなくて is the casual/spoken form; でなく is the formal/written form. Same meaning — use じゃなくて in conversation and でなく in signs, brochures, and formal descriptions.

MNN L15 N だけ ("only")

MNN L15 introduced だけ for "only": 日本語だけ話します. This lesson extends it: だけじゃなくて = "not only ~" = beyond just one thing. It's だけ + じゃなくて (negative of だ) = "it's not just ~."

Grammar 4

Nなら、～

"If It's N, Then ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

なら picks up on a topic or condition mentioned earlier and provides relevant information or advice about it. When someone asks about returning library books, you'd say: 市内しないの図書館としょかんなら、どこに返かえしてもいいです ("If it's a library within the city, you can return it anywhere"). It sets the scope of a condition — "given that it's X, then..."

📐 Formation
N + なら、～

Also connects to V/Adj plain forms and ナ-adj (大変なら). With イ-adj, no い change: 高いなら.

💬 Library Policy
A：返却へんきゃくは、市内しないの図書館としょかんなら、どこに返かえしてもいいです。
For returns, if it's a library within the city, you can return it anywhere.
📝 Library Card Eligibility
市内しないにお住すまいの方かた、お勤つとめの方かたなら、誰だれでも図書館としょかんの利用者りようしゃカードが作つくれます。
Anyone living or working in the city can create a library user card.
📝 Free Entry for Kids
この施設しせつは、小学生以下しょうがくせいいかなら無料むりょうで入はいれます。
If you're an elementary school student or younger, you can enter this facility for free.

L4 Irodori Nなら (advice)

Irodori L4 used なら to give advice based on what someone said: この近くなら、「平兵衛」がおすすめです. This lesson extends なら to express hypothetical conditions — "if [condition X], then [rule Y]." Same grammar, broader usage.

Grammar 5

[疑問詞ぎもんし] V-ても、～

"No Matter ~ / Even If ~"

🔍 What does it mean?

When a question word (何, どこ, いつ, だれ) combines with V-ても, it creates an expression meaning "no matter what/where/when/who": the result stays the same regardless. どこに返かえしてもいいです = "You may return it anywhere (no matter where)." It's formed by adding も to the verb's て-form, right after the question word.

📐 Formation
[疑問詞ぎもんし] V-ても、～

て-form + も = "even if ~." Combined with question words = "no matter what/where/when/who."

Question Word+ V-てもMeaning
何なに何なにを食たべてもno matter what you eat
どこどこへ行いってもno matter where you go
いついつ来きてもno matter when you come
誰だれ誰だれがやってもno matter who does it
どこどこから乗のってもno matter where you board
📝 Library — Return Anywhere
市内しないの図書館としょかんなら、どこに返かえしてもいいです。
If it's a library in the city, you may return it anywhere.
📝 Always Empty Library
この図書館としょかんはいつ来きても、空すいていて静しずかなので、よく利用りようしています。
This library is always uncrowded and quiet whenever I come, so I use it often.
📝 Flat-Rate Bus
市しバスは、どこから乗のっても、どこで降おりても200円えんです。
City buses are 200 yen no matter where you get on and where you get off.

MNN L15 V-てもいいですか

MNN L15 introduced V-てもいいですか (May I ~?). The ても here is the same grammar — "even if" — but when combined with interrogative words, it becomes "no matter what/where/when." The meaning shifts from permission to universality.

MNN L25 V-ても（条件）

MNN L25 covers V-ても for concession: 雨が降っても、行きます ("Even if it rains, I'll go"). This lesson extends it with question words for universal statements: いつ来ても = "whenever/no matter when."

Bonus

📊 V-ている vs V-てある — Complete Guide

状態じょうたいの表現ひょうげん：ている vs てある

Both describe current states, but they differ in whether someone intentionally caused that state. Understanding this distinction is essential for describing facilities accurately: was the door left open on purpose, or did it just happen to be open?

StateV-ている (自動詞)V-てある (他動詞)
Door openドアが開あいているドアが開あけてある
Door closedドアが閉しまっているドアが閉しめてある
Lights on電気でんきがついている電気でんきがつけてある
Lights off電気でんきが消きえている電気でんきが消けしてある
🔄 V-ている — Neutral State

「あ、窓まどが開あいているね。」

Oh, the window is open. (just observing — no implication about who or why)

✋ V-てある — Someone's Intention

「窓まどが開あけてあるのは、換気かんきのためです。」

The window is left open for ventilation. (someone opened it purposely)

💡 For describing facilities: Use V-てある when highlighting services that have been deliberately set up: パンフレットを用意してあります, 作品が飾ってあります, 資料を展示してあります. These all imply staff prepared things for visitors.

📐 Conditional Comparison: なら vs たら vs ば

条件じょうけんの表現ひょうげんまとめ

You've now learned three conditionals across the Irodori series. Each one has a slightly different nuance, though they overlap in many situations. Here's a practical guide to choosing the right one.

ConditionalLessonNuanceExample
V-たらL7Specific one-time events: "when/if [X happens], [Y]"雨あめが降ふったら、行いきません
V-ばL10General rules/customs: "what should I do?"何なにを着きて行いけばいいですか
N ならL4/L13Topic-based condition: "if it's [N], then"市内しないの図書館としょかんなら、OK

🎯 Specific event? → たら (if it rains, when you arrive…)
📜 General rule/custom? → ば (what should one wear…)
📌 Topic/scope condition? → なら (if it's a city library, if you're a student…)

📋 疑問詞 + V-ても — "No Matter ~" Cheat Sheet

疑問詞ぎもんし＋ても の表現集ひょうげんしゅう

Combining question words with V-ても creates universal statements — "no matter what/where/when/who." These expressions are extremely useful for describing policies, rules, and universal truths.

Question WordPatternMeaningExample
何なに何なにを V-てもno matter what何なにを食たべても太ふとらない
どこどこに/で/から V-てもno matter whereどこに返かえしてもいい
いついつ V-てもno matter whenいつ来きても空すいている

💡 Quick Pattern: [疑問詞] + V-ても = universal/concessive. Without 疑問詞, V-ても = "even if": 雨あめが降ふっても行いきます ("even if it rains, I'll go"). With 疑問詞, it becomes "no matter what/where/when."
