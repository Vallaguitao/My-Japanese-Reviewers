Grammar 1

Nに行いきたいんですが…

Politely Asking for Directions
📝 Example
マルイチデパートに行いきたいんですが…。
I would like to go to Maruichi Department Store... (could you help me?)

📖 How It Works

Adding 〜んですが… at the end of a sentence allows you to explain your situation while implicitly asking for help. It's softer and more polite than asking directly. The trailing "…" indicates you're waiting for the other person's response.

💡 Intonation Tip:
When saying 〜んですが…, use a softer pitch and lower intonation at the end. This makes it sound more polite and less demanding.
🔵 Direct Question
駅えきはどこですか？
Where is the station?
駅えきは、どう行いったらいいですか？
How do I get to the station?
🩷 Soft/Polite Request
駅えきに行いきたいんですが…。
I'd like to go to the station... (hint for help)
↑ Implies: "Could you help me?"
📐 Structure
N + に + 行いきたいんですが…

んです = explanatory form | が = "but" (left hanging to invite response)

🔊 Pronunciation Guide
郵便局ゆうびんきょく yuu-bin-kyo-ku (post office)
行いきたいんですが i-ki-ta-in-de-su-ga ↘

Grammar 2

V-て、〜

Connecting Actions for Directions
📝 Example
この道みちをまっすぐ行いって、2つ目めの信号しんごうを左ひだりに曲まがってください。
Go straight on this road, and turn left at the second traffic light.

📖 How It Works

The te-form (V-て) is used to connect two or more actions in sequence. This is extremely common when giving directions. You learned te-form for connecting in Lesson 2; here we apply it specifically to direction-giving.

💬 More Examples
マルイチデパートは、あの信号しんごうを右みぎに曲まがって、そのあと、ずっとまっすぐですよ。
To get to Maruichi Department Store, turn right at that light and go straight without turning.
科学博物館かがくはくぶつかんは、ここをまっすぐ行いって、橋はしを渡わたって、すぐ右みぎに曲まがったところにあります。
To get to the Science Museum, go straight on this road, cross the bridge, and take an immediate right.
💡 Giving Directions in a Car:
When someone is driving you, it's easier to give an approximate location first, then landmarks when you get closer:

「もうすぐ小島高校こじまこうこうだけど…」→「この先さきに、白しろくて大おおきな家いえがあります。その角かどを右みぎに入はいってください。」

Grammar 2: V-て、〜 Directions

Question 1 of 2
Give directions: "Go straight, then turn right."
Convert 行いく to te-form:
まっすぐ行い 、右みぎに曲まがってください。
"Go straight, then turn right."
Question 2 of 2
Give directions: "Cross the bridge, then it's on the left."
Convert 渡わたる to te-form:
橋はしを渡わた 、左ひだりにあります。
"Cross the bridge, and it's on the left."
✅ Answers

Grammar 2: V-て、〜 Directions

✓ Question 1 Answer
まっすぐ行いって、右みぎに曲まがってください。
Explanation: 行いく is a Group 1 verb ending in く. Te-form: く → いて. So 行いく → 行いって (irregular - one of the few exceptions!).
✓ Question 2 Answer
橋はしを渡わたって、左ひだりにあります。
Explanation: 渡わたる is a Group 1 verb ending in る (not ru-verb!). Te-form: る → って. So 渡わたる → 渡わたって.
Grammar 3

N1じゃなくて、N2

Correcting Information
📝 Example
1つ目めじゃなくて、2つ目めです。
It is the second one, not the first one.

📖 How It Works

This expression is used to correct what another person said. You deny N1 and replace it with the correct N2. It's very useful when giving directions and someone misunderstands!

📐 Structure
N1 + じゃなくて、N2 + です

じゃなくて = connecting form of じゃない(です) = "not N1, but..."

❌ What They Said (Wrong)
1つ目めの信号しんごうですか？
"The first traffic light?"
✓ Your Correction
いいえ、1つ目めじゃなくて、2つ目めです。
"No, not the first—the second."
💬 Conversation Example
A:すみません。博物館はくぶつかんはどこですか？
Excuse me. Where is the museum?
B:博物館はくぶつかん？ 市立博物館しりつはくぶつかんですか？
Museum? Is it the municipal museum?
A:いいえ、市立博物館しりつはくぶつかんじゃなくて、科学博物館かがくはくぶつかんです。
No, it is not the municipal museum. It is the Science Museum.
💡 Polite Correction:
Using じゃなくて is a gentle way to correct someone. It's softer than just saying いいえ、違います (No, that's wrong). The structure acknowledges what they said while providing the correct information.
