Grammar 1

Nはどうですか？

Making Suggestions
📝 Example
6時じはどうですか？
How about six o'clock?

📖 How It Works

This expression is used to suggest something to see if the other person agrees with you. In this lesson, it's used to arrange a time and place to meet. The N can be a time or a place.

📐 Structure
N (time/place) + は + どうですか？

どう = how | どうですか = how about ~? / what do you think of ~?

💬 Conversation Example
A:明日あしたの待まち合あわせ、どうしますか？
Where would you like to meet tomorrow?
B:ホテルのロビーはどうですか？
How about we meet at a hotel lobby?
A:わかりました。ロビーですね。
Sure. I will see you at the lobby.
💡 Common Uses:

🕐 Time: 3時じはどうですか？ (How about 3 o'clock?)
📍 Place: 駅えきの前まえはどうですか？ (How about in front of the station?)
📅 Day: 土曜日どようびはどうですか？ (How about Saturday?)
🔊 Pronunciation Guide
待まち合あわせ ma-chi-a-wa-se (meeting place/appointment)
どうしますか dou-shi-ma-su-ka (what shall we do?)

Grammar 2

Nでもいいですか？

Suggesting Alternatives
📝 Example
6時半じはんでもいいですか？
Can we meet at six thirty instead?

📖 How It Works

This expression is used to suggest an alternative and ask if it's convenient. When someone suggests one time/place but that doesn't work for you, use this pattern to propose a different option.

📊 Typical Conversation Flow
Person A suggests
6時じはどうですか？
→
Person B counters
6時半じはんでもいいですか？
→
Person A agrees
いいですよ。
📐 Structure
N (alternative) + でも + いいですか？

でも = even / also | いいですか = is it okay?
Literally: "Is even N okay?" = "Would N be alright instead?"

💬 Conversation Example
A:明日あしたの待まち合あわせ、「平和へいわの鳥とり」の像ぞうの前まえはどうですか？
How about we meet in front of the Heiwa-no-tori statue tomorrow?
B:ちょっとわかりません。駅えきの改札かいさつでもいいですか？
I am not quite sure. Can we meet at the ticket gate of the station?
A:いいですよ。
For sure.
💡 Compare the Two Patterns:

● Nはどうですか？ = Making an initial suggestion
● Nでもいいですか？ = Proposing an alternative (when the first option doesn't work)

Grammar 3

Nで、〜 / V-て、〜

Expressing Causes & Reasons (原因げんいん・理由りゆう)
🩷 With Nouns (Nで)
事故じこで電車でんしゃが止とまりました。
The trains stopped because of an accident.
💜 With Verbs (V-て)
ねぼうして、今いま、起おきました。
I overslept and just woke up.

📖 How It Works

These expressions indicate the cause or reason for what follows in the sentence. The particle で attaches to nouns, while the te-form is used for verbs. This is extremely useful for explaining why you're late!

📐 Structure
N + で、〜 (reason/result)
V-て、〜 (reason/result)

で with nouns = "because of N" | V-て = "because of V-ing"

💬 Common Excuses for Being Late
渋滞じゅうたいで、30分ぷんぐらい遅おくれます。
I will be late about 30 minutes because of traffic congestion.
道みちに迷まよって、ちょっと遅おくれます。
I will be a bit late because I got lost.
電車でんしゃが遅おくれて、すみません。
Sorry, the train was delayed.
💡 V-て for Both Sequence AND Cause:
You learned in Lesson 2 that V-て connects actions in sequence. Here, V-て also shows cause and effect. Context determines the meaning!

Sequence: 起おきて、朝あさごはんを食たべました。 (I woke up and ate breakfast.)
Cause: ねぼうして、遅おくれました。 (I overslept, so I was late.)
🔊 Pronunciation Guide
渋滞じゅうたい juu-tai (traffic jam)
道みちに迷まよう mi-chi ni ma-yo-u (to get lost)
ねぼうする ne-bou-su-ru (to oversleep)

Grammar 3: Nで / V-て (Cause)

Question 1 of 2
The train was delayed because of rain (雨あめ = rain, noun).
Complete with the cause:
雨あめ 、電車でんしゃが遅おくれました。
"The train was delayed because of rain."
Question 2 of 2
You got lost, so you'll be late. (迷まよう = to get lost)
Convert the verb to te-form:
道みちに迷まよ 、遅おくれます。
"I got lost, so I'll be late."
✅ Answers

Grammar 3: Nで / V-て (Cause)

✓ Question 1 Answer
雨あめで、電車でんしゃが遅おくれました。
Explanation: For nouns indicating a cause, add で. 雨あめ (rain) + で = "because of rain." This is different from the で used for location or means!
✓ Question 2 Answer
道みちに迷まよって、遅おくれます。
Explanation: 迷まよう is a Group 1 verb ending in う. Te-form: う → って. So 迷まよう → 迷まよって. The te-form here shows cause: "because I got lost..."
