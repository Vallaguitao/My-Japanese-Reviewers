Grammar 1

V-てみたいんですが…

"I'd like to try ~"
📝 Example
合気道あいきどうをやってみたいんですが…。
I would like to try aikido...

📖 How It Works

This expression is used to tell someone that you wish to try something new for the first time, and to ask about it. In Lesson 8, you learned V-たいんですが… (expressing wishes). Here, we add てみる (to try doing) to express wanting to try something you've never done before.

📐 Structure
V-て + みたいんですが…

V-て = te-form | みる = to try | たいんですが = want to (soft request)
Combined: "I'd like to try doing V..."

🩷 Lesson 8: V-たいんですが
合気道あいきどうをやりたいんですが…
I want to do aikido...
🔵 Lesson 10: V-てみたいんですが
合気道あいきどうをやってみたいんですが…
I'd like to try aikido... (first time)
💬 Conversation Example
A:書道しょどう教室きょうしつに参加さんかしてみたいんですが…。
I would like to take a calligraphy class...
B:体験たいけん教室きょうしつですか？それとも毎週まいしゅうのコースですか？
Do you mean a trial lesson or a weekly course?
💡 When to Use てみたい:
Use this when you want to experience something for the first time. The trailing んですが… invites the listener to respond with information or help.

日本にほん料理りょうりを作つくってみたいんですが… (I'd like to try making Japanese food...)
着物きものを着きてみたいんですが… (I'd like to try wearing a kimono...)

Grammar 2

【期間きかん】(に)〜回かい

Frequency Expressions
📝 Example
日本語にほんごクラスは週しゅうに1回かいです。
Japanese-language classes are held once weekly.

📖 How It Works

This expression is used to describe frequency. に is used before expressions that describe a period such as 週しゅう (week), 月つき (month), and 年ねん (year). 回かい is used before a number to indicate the number of times.

📐 Structure
【Period】に + Number + 回かい

Note: に can be omitted in some cases for general frequency.
For approximate frequency, add ぐらい after 回かい.

週しゅうに1回かい
once a week
週しゅうに2回かい
twice a week
月つきに1回かい
once a month
月つきに3回かい
3 times a month
年ねんに1回かい
once a year
4年ねんに1回かい
once every 4 years
💬 More Examples
月つきに2回かい、国際こくさい交流会こうりゅうかいがあります。
International exchange events are held twice per month.
オリンピックは4年ねんに1回かいあります。
The Olympic Games are held once every four years.
💡 Period Words:

週しゅう / 〜週間しゅうかん (week / for ~weeks)
月つき / 〜か月げつ (month / for ~months)
年ねん / 〜年ねん (year / for ~years)

Grammar 3

V-ましょうか？

Offering Assistance
📝 Example
教室きょうしつのチラシを持もって来きましょうか？
Would you like me to bring you a flyer for the class?

📖 How It Works

This expression is used to offer assistance to someone. Change V-ます to V-ましょうか？ to politely offer to do something for someone.

📐 Structure
V-ます → V-ましょうか？

持もって来きます → 持もって来きましょうか？ (Shall I bring it?)
手伝てつだいます → 手伝てつだいましょうか？ (Shall I help?)

💬 Conversation Example
A:よかったら、手伝てつだいましょうか？
I will help you if you'd like.
B:ありがとうございます。
Thank you.
🟢 Common Offers
説明せつめいしましょうか？
Shall I explain?
窓まどを開あけましょうか？
Shall I open the window?
荷物にもつを持もちましょうか？
Shall I carry your bags?
💡 よかったら, V-ましょうか？
Adding よかったら ("if you'd like") makes the offer even more polite and less pushy.

よかったら、案内あんないしましょうか？ (If you'd like, shall I show you around?)

Related Lessons in MNN

Lesson 14 V-ましょうか (offering help)

Key Point: MNN distinguishes V-ましょう (let's do together) from V-ましょうか？ (shall I do for you?). The question form with か makes it an offer of assistance rather than a suggestion to do something together.

Grammar 4

聞きき返かえし

Asking for Clarification
📝 Example
すみません。「ぼご」は、どういう意味いみですか？
Excuse me. What does "bogo" mean?

📖 How It Works

There are two ways of asking again what someone said:

① Ask what the person said (repetition)

② Ask the meaning of what the person said

This lesson focuses on ②—asking about meaning when you don't understand a word.

📐 Patterns for Asking Meaning
「〜」は、どういう意味いみですか？
「〜」は、何なんですか？

Both patterns can be used to ask about unfamiliar words.

🔄 Alternative: Repeat with Rising Intonation

You can also simply repeat the word you don't understand with rising intonation:

A:合気道あいきどうの道着どうぎ、持もってますか？
B:どうぎ？↗
A:合気道あいきどうの服ふくです。
A: Do you have a dogi? B: Dogi? A: It is clothing for aikido.
💬 Useful Phrases
すみません、もう一度いちど言いってください。
Sorry, please say that one more time.
「体験たいけん教室きょうしつ」は、どういう意味いみですか？
What does "taiken kyoushitsu" mean?
💡 Remember:
• どういう意味いみですか = What does it mean?
• 何なんですか = What is it?
• Word? ↗ (rising tone) = Simple repetition for clarification

Grammar 5

Nのとき、〜 / ナA-なとき、〜 / イA-いとき、〜

"When ~" Expressions
📝 Example
高校こうこうのとき、少すこし日本語にほんごを勉強べんきょうしました。
I studied a bit of Japanese when I was in high school.

📖 How It Works

These expressions are used to describe when the event in the latter part of the sentence happened. The word とき means "when" or "at the time of."

📐 Structures
N + のとき、〜
ナA + なとき、〜
イA-いとき、〜
Noun + の
高校こうこうのとき
when in high school
Noun + の
買かい物もののとき
when shopping
Noun + の
子供こどものとき
when I was a child
ナA + な
暇ひまなとき
when free/bored
ナA + な
元気げんきなとき
when energetic
ナA + な
静しずかなとき
when it's quiet
イA-い
若わかいとき
when young
イA-い
忙いそがしいとき
when busy
イA-い
寂さびしいとき
when lonely
💬 More Examples
買かい物もののとき、お店みせの人ひとと日本語にほんごで話はなします。
I talk to store staff in Japanese when I am shopping.
暇ひまなとき、日本にほんのドラマを見みます。
When I am bored, I watch Japanese dramas.
若わかいとき、いろいろな国くにのことばを習ならいました。
When I was young, I learned the languages of various countries.

✓ Question 1 Answer

Explanation: 大学生だいがくせい is a noun, so it takes の before とき. This describes when in the past the action happened.
