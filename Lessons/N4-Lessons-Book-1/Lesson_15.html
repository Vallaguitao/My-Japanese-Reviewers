文法 1

～んです ②

Explaining Symptoms
昨日きのうから38度どの熱ねつがあって、のどがすごく痛いたいんです。
I have had a 38-degree fever since yesterday, and my throat is very sore.

💡 What is ～んです?

  • Used to explain a situation to someone (like a doctor)
  • In Lesson 14, we learned ～んです with verbs
  • This lesson adds nouns and adjectives
  • Adds a sense of "the reason is..." or "the thing is..."

🏥 Common Questions at the Doctor

😟 Casual: どうしたんですか？

What's the matter?

Used when someone looks different than usual

👨‍⚕️ Formal: どうしましたか？

What's wrong? (professional)

Used by doctors to ask about symptoms

～んです Formation

「～んです」の作つくり方かた
Type Pattern Example
Noun
名詞(めいし)		 N + なんです
風邪かぜなんです
I have a cold
ナ-adjective
ナ形容詞		 ナA + なんです
大変たいへんなんです
It's terrible
イ-adjective
イ形容詞		 イA + んです
のどが痛いたいんです
My throat hurts
Verb
動詞(どうし)		 V-る + んです
(辞書形)
熱ねつがあるんです
I have a fever
V-た + んです
(タ形)
足あしをくじいたんです
I twisted my ankle
V-ない + んです
(ナイ形)
せきが止とまらないんです
I can't stop coughing

⚠️ Important Difference

  • Nouns/ナ-adj: Use ～なんです
  • イ-adj/Verbs: Use ～んです (no な)
  • Multiple symptoms? Connect with て-form!

文法 2

V-ないでください

Negative Instructions
今週こんしゅうは仕事しごとを休やすんで、できるだけ人ひとに会あわないでください。
Please take some time off from work this week, and do not see people if at all possible.

💡 What is V-ないでください?

  • Used to instruct or request someone NOT to do something
  • Common at hospitals when doctors give instructions
  • Connects to the verb's ナイ-form
  • Polite way to say "Please don't..."

📊 Politeness Levels

Expression Politeness When to Use
V-ないで Casual Friends, family
V-ないでください Polite ✓ Standard polite request
V-ないでくれる？ Casual request Asking a favor (casual)
V-ないでもらえませんか？ Very polite Formal situations

文法 3

V (plain-form) + N

Describing What Medicine Does
こちらは、せきを抑おさえる薬くすりです。
This is medicine that suppresses coughing.

💡 Qualifying Nouns with Verbs

  • In Japanese, modifiers come BEFORE the noun
  • Use the plain/dictionary form of verbs to describe nouns
  • Very useful for explaining what medicine does
  • Pattern: [verb plain-form] + [noun]

🔄 How It Works

Combining two sentences:

これは、薬くすりです。 + この薬くすりは、せきを抑おさえます。
"This is medicine." + "This medicine suppresses coughing."
↓
これは、せきを抑おさえる薬くすりです。
"This is medicine that suppresses coughing."

文法 4

V-る前まえに / V-たあと

Before/After Doing
1日にち1回かい、寝ねる前まえに飲のんでください。
Please take it once daily before bedtime.
飲のむと、眠ねむくなります。飲のんだあと、運転うんてんしないでくださいね。
You will be sleepy after taking this medicine. Please do not drive after taking it.

💡 Before & After with Verbs

  • V-る前まえに = Before doing V (uses dictionary form)
  • V-たあと = After doing V (uses た-form)
  • Very common for medicine instructions!
  • Lesson 8 covered N の前まえに/あとで with nouns

文法 5

～とき(に)、～

When (situation/timing)
この薬くすりは、痛いたくてがまんできないときに、飲のんでください。
As for this medicine, please take it when the pain becomes unbearable.

💡 What is ～とき?

  • Indicates a certain point in time or situation
  • Used to give advice: "When X happens, do Y"
  • Perfect for medical advice about when to take medicine
  • ～ときは can introduce a topic: "As for when..."

Grammar Review Quiz

Question 1 of 2
🏥 At the doctor's office, explaining your symptoms:
Complete: "昨日きのうから頭あたまが痛いた___。"
頭あたまが痛いた。
Question 2 of 2
👨‍⚕️ The doctor gives you instructions:
Complete: "今日きょうはお風呂ふろに___。" (Please don't take a bath)
お風呂ふろに。
✅ Answers

Quiz Review

✓ Question 1 Answer
頭あたまが痛いたいんです。
Explanation: When explaining symptoms with イ-adjectives like 痛いたい (painful), you keep the い and add んです directly. This creates "The thing is, my head hurts" — a natural way to explain symptoms to a doctor.
✓ Question 2 Answer
お風呂ふろに入はいらないでください。
Explanation: To give a negative instruction "please don't," use V-ないでください. The ない-form of 入はいる (to enter) is 入はいらない, so the full expression is 入はいらないでください (please don't take a bath).
🎯 Practice

Medicine Instructions Quiz

Question 1 of 2
💊 The pharmacist explains when to take medicine:
Complete: "ご飯はんを食たべた___、飲のんでください。" (after eating)
食たべた、飲のんでください。
Question 2 of 2
🩹 The doctor explains what the medicine does:
Complete: "これは、熱ねつを___薬くすりです。" (medicine that reduces fever)
熱ねつを薬くすりです。
✅ Answers

Quiz Review

✓ Question 1 Answer
食たべたあと、飲のんでください。
Explanation: "After doing V" uses V-たあと. Since 食たべる's た-form is 食たべた, we get 食たべたあと (after eating). Remember: 前まえに uses dictionary form, あと uses た-form!
✓ Question 2 Answer
熱ねつを下さげる薬くすりです。
Explanation: To describe what medicine does, use V(plain-form) + N. The verb 下さげる means "to lower/reduce," so 熱ねつを下さげる薬くすり = "medicine that lowers fever." Remember: modifiers come BEFORE the noun in Japanese!
