文法 1

V (plain-form) + N ②

Describing Gifts
ネパールから持もって来きた飾かざりです。
This is a decoration I brought from Nepal.

💡 What is V (plain-form) + N?

  • Used to describe/explain things like gifts and belongings
  • In Lesson 15, we learned V + N for medicine (subject of explanation)
  • This lesson: the noun is the OBJECT being described
  • Uses past tense plain form (た-form) for completed actions

🔄 How It Works

Combining two sentences:

これは、飾かざりです。 + 私わたしは、この飾かざりをネパールから持もって来きました。
"This is a decoration." + "I brought this decoration from Nepal."
↓
これは、ネパールから持もって来きた飾かざりです。
"This is a decoration (that) I brought from Nepal."

文法 2

[人ひと]に[物もの]をもらいます

Receiving from Someone
このマグカップ、誕生日たんじょうびに、友ともだちにもらったんです。
This mug was given to me by a friend for my birthday.

💡 What is もらいます?

  • Indicates that YOU receive something from someone
  • Focus is on the receiver's perspective
  • The particle に marks the giver (can also use から)
  • Very common when talking about birthday gifts!

文法 3

[人ひと]が[物もの]をくれます

Someone Gives (to You)
これは、兄あにがくれたお守まもりです。
This is a personal amulet my older brother gave me.

💡 What is くれます?

  • Used when someone gives something TO YOU
  • Focus is on the giver's action toward you
  • The particle が marks the giver
  • Often implies gratitude - "they kindly gave me"
  • 私わたしに (to me) is usually omitted because it's obvious

比較

もらいます vs くれます

Receiving vs Being Given

Both express receiving a gift, but from different perspectives. The meaning is essentially the same!

🙋 もらいます (I receive)

父ちちに時計とけいをもらいました。
I received a watch from my father.
Focus: Me receiving
Particle: [giver]に or から

👤 くれます (Someone gives me)

父ちちが時計とけいをくれました。
My father gave me a watch.
Focus: Giver's action
Particle: [giver]が
🎁 Same Event, Different Perspectives
👨
父ちち
→ 🎁 →
🙋
私わたし

もらいました = "I received" (my action of receiving)

くれました = "He gave me" (his action toward me)

💡 When to Use Which?

  • もらいました — When YOU are the subject (I received...)
  • くれました — When the GIVER is the subject (Someone gave me...)
  • くれます implies gratitude - "they kindly gave me"
  • Both are natural and commonly used!

✅ Answers

Quiz Review

✓ Question 1 Answer
ネパールから持もって来きた飾かざりです。
Explanation: To describe something you brought, use V(た-form) + N. 持もって来きます becomes 持もって来きた in plain past form. This creates "a decoration (that) I brought from Nepal."
✓ Question 2 Answer
母ははにセーターをもらいました。
Explanation: With もらいます, the giver is marked with に (or から). 母ははに = "from my mother." The pattern is [person]に[thing]をもらいます.

✅ Answers

Quiz Review

✓ Question 1 Answer
兄あにがくれたお守まもりです。
Explanation: With くれます, the giver is marked with が. This shows your brother as the subject who performed the action of giving to you. The た-form くれた modifies お守まもり (amulet).
✓ Question 2 Answer
父ちちが誕生日たんじょうびにくれました。
Explanation: When talking about what someone gave you, use くれました. The pattern is [person]が[occasion]に[thing]をくれました. Here, the thing (watch) is implied from context.

🎌 CULTURE

Gift-Giving in Japan

お土産みやげの文化ぶんか

Gift-giving (お土産みやげ) is an important part of Japanese culture! 🎁

✈️
Travel Souvenirs
When traveling, it's customary to bring back souvenirs for coworkers, friends, and family. This is called お土産みやげ.
⛩️
お守まもり (Amulets)
Amulets from shrines/temples are popular gifts, believed to bring protection and good luck to the recipient.
🍫
Regional Specialties
Each region has famous products. Hokkaido butter, Kyoto sweets, and local crafts make perfect gifts!

💡 Useful Phrases When Giving

  • よかったら、どうぞ。— If you'd like, please (have some).
  • つまらないものですが...— It's nothing special, but... (humble)
  • お口くちに合あうといいんですが。— I hope it suits your taste.
