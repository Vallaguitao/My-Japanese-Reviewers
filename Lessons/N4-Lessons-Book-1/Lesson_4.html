Grammar 1

S ね 〈共感きょうかん〉

Seeking/Expressing Sympathy
📝 Example
今日きょうはいい天気てんきですね。
It is a beautiful day today, isn't it?

📖 How It Works

The particle ね at the end of a sentence is used to seek sympathy from the listener or to express sympathy with them. It shows you're sharing a feeling or observation that you both know to be true.

💡 Weather as Conversation Starter:
Topics about weather, temperature, and climate are often used as conversation starters because they're easy to discuss with anyone. The speaker uses ね because both people can observe the same weather!
💬 Conversation Examples
A:今日きょうはいい天気てんきですね。
It is a beautiful day today.
B:そうですね。
Yes, it is.
A:だいぶ暖あたたかくなりましたね。
It got much warmer, didn't it?
B:本当ほんとうですね。
That's true.
⚠️ Different from 入門 (A1):
In A1, you learned ね for confirmation (like コピー20枚まいですね "20 copies, right?"). Here, ね is used to share feelings and observations, not to confirm information.

Grammar 1: Sね (Sympathy)

✓ Question 1 Answer

Grammar 2

非過去ひかこ・過去かこ

Non-past & Past Forms
📝 Example
昨日きのうはすごい雨あめでしたね。
It rained heavily yesterday, didn't it?

📖 How It Works

When talking about weather, you use non-past forms for current weather and past forms for yesterday's weather or weather changes. This is a review from 入門 level.

イ-adjective		 イA-いです
暑あついです		 イA-かったです
暑あつかったです
動詞どうし

💬 Example Conversations
A:おはようございます。朝あさから暑あついですね。
Good morning. It is a hot morning.
B:そうですね。ゆうべも暑あつかったですね。
Yes, it is. It was hot last night, too.

✏️ Quick Check

Grammar 2: Non-past & Past

Question 1 of 2
Yesterday was cold. (寒さむい = cold)
Convert to past tense:
昨日きのうは寒さむ です。
"Yesterday was cold."
Question 2 of 2
Last week was rainy. (雨あめ = rain, noun)
Convert to past tense:
先週せんしゅうは雨あめ 。
"Last week was rainy."
✅ Answers

Grammar 2: Non-past & Past

✓ Question 1 Answer
昨日きのうは寒さむかったです。
Explanation: イ-adjectives form past tense by changing い to かった. 寒さむい → 寒さむかった + です = "was cold."
✓ Question 2 Answer
先週せんしゅうは雨あめでした。
Explanation: Nouns form past tense by changing です to でした. 雨あめです → 雨あめでした = "was rain/rainy."
Grammar 3

V-ています ③

Describing Current Weather
📝 Example
今いまも少すこし雨あめが降ふっています。
It is still raining a little.

📖 How It Works

The pattern V-ています expresses the present situation. You've seen this pattern before:

🔄 Three Uses of V-ています So Far:

Lesson 1: Occupation → レストランで働はたらいています
(I work at a restaurant)

Lesson 2: Ongoing habits → 空手からてを習ならっています
(I'm learning karate)

Lesson 4: Current weather → 雨あめが降ふっています
(It is raining)
☀️
晴はれています
It is sunny
☁️
くもっています
It is cloudy
🌧️
雨あめが降ふっています
It is raining
❄️
雪ゆきが降ふっています
It is snowing
💬 More Examples
今朝けさは、よく晴はれていますね。
It is nice and sunny this morning.
外そとは、風かぜが吹ふいています。注意ちゅういしてください。
It is windy outside. Please be careful.
今日きょうはくもっていて、あまり天気てんきがよくないですね。
It is cloudy and not very fine today.

Grammar 3: V-ています③

Question 1 of 2
Describe that it's currently snowing.
Complete the sentence:

"It is snowing now."

Say it's sunny today.

✅ Answers

Grammar 3: V-ています③

✓ Question 1 Answer
今いま、雪ゆきが降ふっています。
Explanation: 降ふる (to fall/rain/snow) becomes 降ふっています to describe current weather. 雪ゆきが降ふっています = "It is snowing."
✓ Question 2 Answer
今日きょうは晴はれていますね。
Explanation: 晴はれる (to clear up/be sunny) becomes 晴はれています. This describes the current sunny state. Note: くもっています for cloudy works the same way.
Grammar 4

〜でしょう

Weather Forecasts & Predictions
📝 Example
明日あしたは晴はれるでしょう。
It will clear up tomorrow.

📖 How It Works

〜でしょう is used in weather forecasts to express a prediction. It means "probably" or "it will likely be." This is the language you hear on TV weather reports!

📐 How to Form 〜でしょう
Type Formation Example
名詞めいし
Noun		 N + でしょう
(です → でしょう)		 雨あめでしょう
ナ形容詞けいようし
ナ-adjective		 ナA + でしょう
(です → でしょう)		 大変たいへんでしょう
イ形容詞けいようし
イ-adjective		 イA-い + でしょう 寒さむいでしょう
動詞どうし
Verb		 V-る + でしょう
(dictionary form)		 晴はれるでしょう
📺 Weather Forecast Examples
明日あしたは晴はれるでしょう。
It will be sunny tomorrow.
明日あしたも寒さむいでしょう。
It will be cold again tomorrow.
雨あめは、今日きょうの夕方ゆうがたにはやむでしょう。
The rain will stop in the early evening today.
💡 Usage Note:
〜でしょう for predictions is mainly used in weather forecasts and formal announcements. In daily conversation, it's not used as often for guessing. The goal here is to understand weather forecasts, not necessarily to use でしょう yourself.
🔊 Pronunciation Guide
やむ ya-mu (to stop [rain])
夕方ゆうがた yuu-ga-ta (early evening)

Grammar 4: 〜でしょう

Question 1 of 2
Weather forecast: "Tomorrow will be hot."
Complete the prediction:

"Tomorrow will (probably) be hot."
Question 2 of 2
Weather forecast: "It will rain tomorrow."
Complete using the verb (降ふる):
明日あしたは雨あめが でしょう。
"It will probably rain tomorrow."
✅ Answers

Grammar 4: 〜でしょう

✓ Question 1 Answer
明日あしたは暑あついでしょう。
Explanation: For イ-adjectives, simply add でしょう after the adjective (keep the い). 暑あつい + でしょう = "will probably be hot."
✓ Question 2 Answer
明日あしたは雨あめが降ふるでしょう。
Explanation: For verbs, use the dictionary form (plain non-past form) before でしょう. 降ふります → 降ふる + でしょう = "will probably fall/rain."
