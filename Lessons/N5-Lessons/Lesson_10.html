📍 あります / います — 存在そんざいと場所ばしょ
Describe what exists where, and master position words!

📚 Grammar Points

1N が あります / います — "There is ~" (existence)
2Place に N が あります / います — What's at a place
3N は Place に あります / います — Where something is
4N₁ の N₂ (position words) — 上うえ, 下した, 前まえ, 後うしろ, etc.
5N₁ や N₂ — Listing representative items
6Confirmation echo — アジアストアですか
Grammar 1

N が あります / います

"There is ~" — Existence

🎉 Two Verbs for "Exist"!

Japanese uses two different verbs for existence, depending on whether the subject is animate (living, self-moving) or inanimate (non-living, stationary). This distinction doesn't exist in English — "there is" works for everything — but in Japanese, you must choose correctly every time.

🪑 あります — Inanimate

Objects, plants, buildings — things that cannot move by themselves.

コンピューターが あります。 ①
There's a computer.
桜さくらが あります。 ②
There are cherry trees.
公園こうえんが あります。 ③
There's a park.
🐕 います — Animate

People, animals — things that can move by themselves.

男おとこの 人ひとが います。 ④
There's a man.
犬いぬが います。 ⑤
There's a dog.
💡 The Rule: Can it move by itself? → います (people, animals, insects, fish). Can't move by itself? → あります (books, trees, buildings, mountains). Plants use あります even though they're alive — they can't walk!

あります has multiple meanings across lessons:

L9 N が あります = "I HAVE ~" (possession).

L10 N が あります = "There IS ~" (existence at a place).

L21 V と 思おもいます = "I think that ~" — different grammar but same verb pool.

L6 で for action location vs L10 に for existence location — critical distinction!

Grammar 2

Place に N が あります / います

"At [place], there is ~"
📐 Structure
Place + に + N + が + あります / います

📖 How it works

This pattern tells the listener what exists at a particular place. The particle に marks the location of existence — this is different from で which marks where an action happens (L6). Think of it this way: に is for static existence, で is for dynamic action.

📝 Example ⑥
わたしの 部屋へやに 机つくえが あります。
There's a desk in my room.
📝 Example ⑦
事務所じむしょに ミラーさんが います。
Mr. Miller is in the office.
💬 Example ⑧ — Asking what's there
A: 地下ちかに 何なにが ありますか。
What's in the basement?
B: レストランが あります。
There's a restaurant.
💬 Example ⑨ — Asking who's there
A: 受付うけつけに だれが いますか。
Who's at the reception?
B: 木村きむらさんが います。
Ms. Kimura is.
⚠️ Critical Note from textbook: After an interrogative (question word), the particle is always が (× なにはありますか, × だれはいますか). Use 何なにが and だれが. Never use は after question words in this pattern!
Grammar 3

N は Place に あります / います

"N is at [place]"
📐 Structure
N + は + Place + に + あります / います

📖 How it works

This pattern flips the word order! Instead of saying what's at a place (Grammar 2), you take a specific, known thing and say where it is. The thing uses は (topic marker) because both speaker and listener already know what it is. This is one of the most practical patterns in daily life — answering "Where is ~?"

📝 Example ⑩
東京とうきょうディズニーランドは 千葉県ちばけんに あります。
Tokyo Disneyland is in Chiba prefecture.
📝 Example ⑪
ミラーさんは 事務所じむしょに います。
Mr. Miller is in the office.
💬 Example ⑫ — Where is ~?
A: 東京とうきょうディズニーランドは どこに ありますか。
Where's Tokyo Disneyland?
B: 千葉県ちばけんに あります。
It's in Chiba prefecture.
💡 Grammar 2 vs Grammar 3: Grammar 2 (Place に N が) answers "What's there?" — introduces new info with が. Grammar 3 (N は Place に) answers "Where is it?" — tells the location of something known. The thing in Grammar 3 uses は because it's the topic (already known to both parties).

Grammar 4

N₁ の N₂ — Position Words

位置いちの言葉ことば

📖 How it works

When N₂ is a position word (上うえ, 下した, 前まえ, etc.), the phrase N₁ の N₂ shows a positional relationship. These position words work like nouns in Japanese — they connect to N₁ with の, and then take に to mark the location.

📝 Example ⑮
机つくえの 上うえに 写真しゃしんが あります。
There's a photograph on the desk.
📝 Example ⑯
郵便局ゆうびんきょくは 銀行ぎんこうの 隣となりに あります。
The post office is next to the bank.
📝 Example ⑰
本屋ほんやは 花屋はなやと スーパーの 間あいだに あります。
The bookshop is between the florist and the supermarket.
💡 Formula: [Reference Object] の [Position Word] に あります/います. Example: 机つくえの 上うえに = "on the desk" (literally: "at the desk's above").
Grammar 4b

Position Words + で (Action)

位置いち + で

📖 Extended Usage

The textbook notes that these position words can also be used with で to mark the location of an action (not existence). This directly connects to the で you learned in L6 — the same position words work with both に and で, depending on whether you're describing existence or action.

📝 Example ⑱
駅えきの 近ちかくで 友達ともだちに 会あいました。
I met a friend near the station.
📍 に — Existence
駅えきの 近ちかくに コンビニが あります。
There's a convenience store near the station.
🏃 で — Action
駅えきの 近ちかくで 友達ともだちに 会あいました。
I met a friend near the station.
💡 Same place, different particles: に = something IS there (static). で = you DO something there (dynamic). This is the L6 vs L10 distinction applied to position words!

Grammar 5

N₁ や N₂

Listing Representative Items

📖 How it works

You learned と in L4 for making complete lists (A と B = A and B, that's all). The particle や creates incomplete lists — it lists a few representative items and implies there are more. The optional など at the end makes this "and so on" meaning even clearer.

📋 と — Complete list (L4)
本ほんと ノートが あります。
There are books and notebooks. (only those two)
📋 や — Partial list (L10)
本ほんや ノートが あります。
There are books, notebooks, and other things.
📝 Example ⑲ — や
箱はこの 中なかに 手紙てがみや 写真しゃしんが あります。
There are letters, photographs, and other things in the box.
📝 Example ⑳ — や + など
箱はこの 中なかに 手紙てがみや 写真しゃしんなどが あります。
There are things like letters and photographs in the box.
💡 Key Difference: と = exhaustive list (A and B and nothing else). や = representative list (A and B, among others). や...など = "things like A and B" (even more explicitly open-ended). Use や when you're giving examples, not a complete inventory.
Grammar 6

Confirmation Echo

アジアストアですか

📖 How it works

In natural conversation, when someone asks a question, the listener often echoes the key word back as a confirmation before answering. This shows they've understood the question correctly and buys time to formulate a good answer. It's a very Japanese conversational habit!

💬 Example ㉑
A: すみません。アジアストアは どこですか。
Excuse me, where is Asia Store?
B: アジアストアですか。（中略ちゅうりゃく）あの ビルの 中なかです。
Asia Store? (confirming) ...It's in that building over there.
💡 Conversation Skill: This echo technique (repeating the key word + ですか) is used across many Japanese conversations. It's similar to the そうですか (L2) and そうですね (L5/L8) patterns — all are listener responses that show engagement before giving your answer.

Complete listener response chart:

L2 そうですか — acknowledging new info ("I see").

L5 そうですね — agreeing ("Indeed").

L6 ～ですか — echoing with interest.

L8 そうですね... — thinking/hedging ("Well...").

L10 [Key word] ですか — confirming before answering.

Evolution

あります — Two Meanings

あります の意味いみ
💰 L9: Possession ("have")
車くるまが あります。
I have a car.
No location marker — about what you possess
📍 L10: Existence ("there is")
部屋へやに 机つくえが あります。
There's a desk in the room.
Location に present — about where something exists
💡 Context Clue: If there's a Place に in the sentence, あります means "exists at." If there's no location marker, it means "I have." The presence of に is the giveaway!
