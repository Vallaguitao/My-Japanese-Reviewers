Topic 1a

Polite → Plain: Verbs

丁寧体ていねいたい → 普通体ふつうたい

📖 Two Styles of Japanese

Japanese has two speech styles: polite style (丁寧体, using ます/です) and plain style (普通体, using dictionary/ない/た forms). Until now, you've been learning polite style. Lesson 20 is where all four plain forms (L14, L17, L18, L19) come together as a real communication system. The table below shows the complete verb conversion. You already know every form — this lesson simply teaches you when and how to use them in conversation.

Polite Style (丁寧体ていねいたい)Plain Style (普通体ふつうたい)
Affirm. non-past行いきます行いく (dict. form, L18)
Neg. non-past行いきません行いかない (ない-form, L17)
Affirm. past行いきました行いった (た-form, L19)
Neg. past行いきませんでした行いかなかった
💡 The negative past (なかった) is new! Take ない-form, drop い, add かった: 行いかない → 行いかなかった. This mirrors how い-adjectives form their past tense (L12): 高たかい → 高たかかった. In fact, ない IS an い-adjective — so it conjugates exactly like one!

🔵 Polite Style

あした 東京とうきょうへ 行いきます。

毎日まいにち 忙いそがしいです。

🔴 Plain Style

あした 東京とうきょうへ 行いく。

毎日まいにち 忙いそがしい。

相撲すもうが 好すきだ。

💡 Key insight: For い-adjectives, plain style just drops です. For な-adjectives and nouns, です becomes だ. For verbs, ます becomes the dictionary/ない/た form depending on tense and polarity.

When to Use Which?

📝 Writing: Letters

🔴 Plain Style — Use with:

Close friends

Family members

Close colleagues

📝 Writing: Diaries, reports, dissertations

⚠️ Using plain style with the wrong person is rude!

Unlike English, where casual speech is usually fine, using plain style with a stranger, a senior, or in formal settings is considered discourteous in Japanese. When in doubt, always use polite style. It's never wrong to be too polite, but it can be very wrong to be too casual.

💡 Think of it this way: Polite style = your default "safe mode." Plain style = something you unlock as a relationship deepens. Many Japanese friendships actually have a specific moment where someone suggests "let's drop the ます!" (タメ口で 話はなそう) — it's a sign of growing closeness.
Rule 3a

Questions: か → Rising ↗

質問しつもんのしかた

📖 How casual questions work

In plain style, questions drop the particle か and instead use rising intonation (your voice goes up at the end). You might sometimes see this intonation written with ？ in informal text. The particle か sounds too formal or interrogation-like in casual speech.

💬 Example ① — Verb question
A: コーヒーを 飲のむ？ ↗
Want some coffee?
B: うん、飲のむ。
Yeah, sure.
🔵 Polite
コーヒーを 飲のみますか。
🔴 Plain
コーヒーを 飲のむ？ ↗
💡 Notice: Not only does か disappear, but ます becomes dictionary form (飲みます→飲む). Also note the particle を is often dropped too — more on that in slide 8!
Rule 3b

Noun/な-adj: だ → Omitted

「だ」の省略しょうりゃく

📖 How it works

For noun and な-adjective questions, the plain copula だ (the plain form of です) is omitted. In affirmative answers, だ may be kept, omitted, or softened with sentence-ending particles like よ. Ending with a bare だ can sound a bit blunt, so speakers often add softeners.

💬 Example ② — Noun question
A: 今晩こんばん 暇ひま？
You free tonight?
♂: うん、暇ひま / 暇ひまだ / 暇ひまだよ。
Yeah, I am. (used by men)
♀: うん、暇ひま / 暇ひまよ / 暇ひまだよ。
Yeah, I am. (used by women)
❌: ううん、暇ひまじゃ ない。
No, I'm not.
💡 Question = drop だ. Answer = keep, drop, or add particle. The question never includes だ (今晩 暇？ NOT 今晩 暇だ？). The answer is flexible — dropping だ or adding よ both soften the tone. The negative is simply じゃ ない (the plain form of じゃ ありません).
Rule 3c

Particle Dropping

助詞じょしの省略しょうりゃく

📖 How it works

In casual speech, certain particles are often omitted when the meaning is clear from context. The particles を, へ, and は are commonly dropped. However, particles like で, に, から, まで, と are NOT dropped because removing them would make the sentence ambiguous.

📝 Examples ③–⑥ — Droppable particles
ごはん[を] 食たべる？
Would you like something to eat? (を dropped)
あした 京都きょうと[へ] 行いかない？
How about going to Kyoto tomorrow? (へ dropped)
この りんご[は] おいしいね。
These apples are nice, aren't they? (は dropped)
そこに はさみ[が] ある？
Are there any scissors there? (が dropped)
⚠️ These particles are NEVER dropped:

で, に, から, まで, と — removing them would make the meaning unclear. "駅えきで 会あう" (meet at the station) → dropping で makes it "駅 会う" — confusing! Only drop particles when the meaning is still obvious without them.

Rule 3d

ている → てる

「い」の省略しょうりゃく

📖 How it works

In plain style, the い of ている (ongoing action/state, L14–15) is frequently dropped, giving てる. This is one of the most common contractions in casual Japanese — you'll hear it constantly in real speech, anime, and casual writing.

💬 Example ⑦ — ている → てる
A: 辞書じしょ、持もって[い]る？
Have you got a dictionary?
B: うん、持もって[い]る。
Yes, I have.
B: ううん、持もって[い]ない。
No, I haven't.
🔵 Polite
持もって いますか。
🔴 Plain (full → shortened)
持もって いる？ → 持もってる？
💡 This applies to all ている forms: 食たべている → 食たべてる. 知しっている → 知しってる. 住すんでいる → 住すんでる. The negative works the same: 持もっていない → 持もってない.
Rule 3e

けど = が

カジュアルな「が」

📖 How it works

けど is the casual equivalent of が (but/however, L8). It works exactly the same way — connecting two contrasting or related clauses. In casual conversation, けど sounds natural while が can sound stiff.

💬 Example ⑧ — けど for contrast
A: その カレー おいしい？
Is that curry good?
B: うん、辛からいけど、おいしい。
Yeah, it's spicy, but it's nice.
💬 Example ⑨ — けど for topic setup
A: 相撲すもうの チケット あるけど、いっしょに 行いかない？
I've got sumo tickets — would you like to go together?
B: いいね。
That'd be great.
💡 けど has two uses, just like が: (1) Contrast: "It's spicy but good." (2) Soft topic introduction: "I have tickets, and so..." This second use softens transitions — けど doesn't always mean "but."

L8 が — Polite "but" (connects two sentences)

L16 て-form can't link contrasts — must use が

L20 けど — Casual "but" (same function as が)

Polite → が. Casual → けど. Same grammar, different register!

Summary

5 Casual Speech Rules

カジュアルの5つのルール
#RulePolite → Plain
1Drop か, use ↗ intonation飲のみますか → 飲のむ？
2Drop だ in N/な-adj questions暇ひまですか → 暇ひま？ (NOT 暇ひまだ？)
3Drop particles (を, へ, は)ごはんを 食たべる → ごはん 食たべる
4ている → てる持もっている → 持もってる
5が → けど辛からいですが → 辛からいけど
💡 The casual speech cascade: A single polite sentence can undergo multiple changes at once. 映画えいがを 見みて いますか。 → 映画えいが 見みてる？ (dropped を, dropped い, dropped か, added ↗). It's not just one rule — they stack!

Caution

Common Mistakes

よくある間違まちがい
❌ Using だ in な-adj/noun questions

× 暇ひまだ？ → ○ 暇ひま？
In questions, だ is always dropped. Keeping it sounds unnatural or aggressive.

❌ Using plain style with strangers/seniors

Using casual speech with someone you just met or with your boss is a serious social mistake in Japanese. When in doubt, always use polite style!

❌ Dropping essential particles

× 駅えき 会あう → ○ 駅えきで 会あう
で, に, から, まで, と must NOT be dropped — they carry meaning that can't be inferred.

❌ Mixing styles mid-sentence

× 行いくます / × 食たべたです → Choose one style and be consistent within a sentence. Polite endings go with polite forms; plain endings go with plain forms.
