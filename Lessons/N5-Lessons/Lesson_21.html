🧠 思おもう・言いう — Think, Say & Confirm
Express opinions, report speech, seek confirmation, and more!

Grammar 1a

Plain form と 思おもいます

"I think ~" — Conjecture
📐 Structure
Plain form + と 思おもいます

📖 The と quotation particle

The particle と marks what comes before it as quoted content — it functions like quotation marks around a thought or statement. The clause before と MUST be in plain form (L20). This is why L20 was so important — from now on, plain form becomes the default input for most new grammar. Use 1: conjecture — expressing what you think will happen or is probably true.

📝 Example ① — Future conjecture
あした 雨あめが 降ふると 思おもいます。
I think it's going to rain tomorrow.
📝 Example ② — Conjecture about present
テレーザちゃんは もう 寝ねたと 思おもいます。
I think Teresa's already gone to bed.
⚠️ Negative conjecture: negate BEFORE と!
📝 Example ③ — Negative conjecture
ミラーさんは この ニュースを 知しって いますか。
Has Mr. Miller heard that news?
……いいえ、知しらないと 思おもいます。
No, I don't think he has. (Lit: I think he doesn't know.)

Japanese negates the THOUGHT, not 思います. × 知しっていると 思おもいません. ○ 知しらないと 思おもいます. This is opposite to English ("I don't think" → "~ないと 思おもいます")!

Grammar 1b

と 思おもいます — Opinion

意見いけんを言いう

📖 Use 2: Stating your opinion

Beyond conjecture, と 思おもいます expresses your personal opinion about something. When asking someone's opinion about a specific topic, use the pattern ～に ついて どう 思おもいますか ("What do you think about ~?"), without putting と after どう.

📝 Example ④ — Opinion
日本にほんは 物価ぶっかが 高たかいと 思おもいます。
I think that prices in Japan are high.
📝 Example ⑤ — Asking opinion
新あたらしい 空港くうこうに ついて どう 思おもいますか。
What do you think of the new airport?
……きれいですが、ちょっと 交通こうつうが 不便ふべんだと 思おもいます。
I think it's lovely, but the transport is a bit inconvenient.
📝 Example ⑥ — Agreeing with someone
ケータイは 便利べんりですね。
Mobile phones are handy, aren't they?
……わたしも そう 思おもいます。
Yes, I think so, too.
💡 Three key phrases: (1) ～と 思おもいます = "I think ~" (2) ～に ついて どう 思おもいますか = "What do you think about ~?" (3) そう 思おもいます = "I think so, too." These three cover most opinion-giving situations!

Grammar 2

と 言いいます

"Say / Said ~"
📐 Two Types of Speech Reporting
Direct: 「exact words」と 言いいます
Indirect: Plain form と 言いいます

📖 How it works

The same と particle marks reported speech. Direct quotation repeats the exact words (placed in 「 」 brackets in Japanese writing) — any speech style is preserved inside the quotes. Indirect quotation uses plain form before と to paraphrase what was said. The person spoken TO is marked with に.

📝 Example ⑦ — Direct: custom/greeting
寝ねる まえに、「お休やすみなさい」と 言いいます。
We say "Good night" before going to bed.
📝 Example ⑧ — Direct: someone's words (past)
ミラーさんは「来週らいしゅう 東京とうきょうへ 出張しゅっちょうします」と 言いいました。
Mr. Miller said, "I'm going to Tokyo on business next week."
📝 Example ⑨ — Indirect: paraphrasing
ミラーさんは 東京とうきょうへ 出張しゅっちょうすると 言いいました。
Mr. Miller said that he would be going to Tokyo on business.
📝 Example ⑩ — Listener marked with に
父ちちに 留学りゅうがくしたいと 言いいました。
I told my father that I wanted to study abroad.
💡 Direct vs Indirect: Direct (「〜」) preserves exact words — any form inside the quotes is fine. Indirect converts to plain form before と. The tense of the quoted content is independent of the main verb (言いました = past, but the quoted content's tense stays as originally meant).

Grammar 3

Plain form でしょう？

"~, right?" — Seeking Confirmation
📐 Structure
V / い-adj plain form + でしょう？ ↗
な-adj / N plain form ~だ + でしょう？ ↗

📖 How it works

でしょう？ (with rising intonation ↗) seeks agreement or confirmation from the listener — "~, right?" or "~, isn't it?" Plain form goes before でしょう, but for な-adjectives and nouns, the だ is removed (just like casual questions in L20). This is different from な-adj + だ + と 思おもいます where だ is kept!

📝 Example ⑪ — Verb
あした パーティーに 行いくでしょう？
You're going to the party tomorrow, right?
……ええ、行いきます。 Yes, I am.
📝 Example ⑫ — い-adjective (past)
北海道ほっかいどうは 寒さむかったでしょう？
I bet it was cold in Hokkaido, wasn't it?
……いいえ、そんなに 寒さむくなかったです。 No, it wasn't so cold.
⚠️ な-adj / Noun: drop だ before でしょう！

○ 静しずかでしょう？ (NOT 静かだでしょう？)
○ 学生がくせいでしょう？ (NOT 学生だでしょう？)
This is the same rule as L20's casual questions: だ disappears before question markers.

Grammar 4

N(place) で N が あります

"An event takes place"
📐 Structure
N(place) で N(event) が あります

📖 A new use for あります!

You already know あります for existence (L10: つくえの 上うえに 本ほんが あります). Here, あります means "to take place / to be held / to occur." The event (party, concert, festival, match, accident) is the subject (が), and the place uses で (not に, because it's where the event HAPPENS, not where something exists).

📝 Example ⑬ — Sports event
東京とうきょうで 日本にほんと ブラジルの サッカーの 試合しあいが あります。
There's a soccer match between Japan and Brazil in Tokyo.
💡 あります: existence (L10) vs events (L21): Existence: 部屋へやに テレビが あります (A TV exists IN the room — static location, に). Event: 東京とうきょうで 試合しあいが あります (A match takes place AT Tokyo — action location, で). The particle tells you which meaning!

L10 N に N が あります — Existence of things (static, に)

L10 N は N に あります — Location of things

L17 N(time) までに V — "By" deadline

L21 N(place) で N が あります — Events taking place (action, で)

Same verb, three different functions! The particle (に vs で) tells you which meaning is intended.

Grammar 5

N(occasion) で

"At (an occasion)"

📖 How it works

The particle で can mark the occasion or event at which an action takes place. This extends で's role as the "action context" particle — just like it marks WHERE actions happen (L6: レストランで 食たべます), it can also mark the OCCASION at which actions happen.

📝 Example ⑭
会議かいぎで 何なにか 意見いけんを 言いいましたか。
Did you say anything at the meeting?
💡 で = "context for action": レストランで 食たべる (eat AT a restaurant — place), 会議かいぎで 言いう (say AT a meeting — occasion), 日本語にほんごで 話はなす (speak IN Japanese — means). All the same particle で providing context for the action!
Grammar 6

N でも V

"How about N or something?"

📖 How it works

The particle でも after a noun softens a suggestion or recommendation by presenting the noun as one example among possibilities. It means "~ or something," without restricting the choice to that specific item. Think of it as a polite, non-pushy way to suggest something.

📝 Example ⑮ — Soft suggestion
ちょっと ビールでも 飲のみませんか。
How about drinking a beer or something?
💡 でも = "~ or something": It implies "this is just one option, I'm not insisting." Without でも: ビールを 飲のみませんか = "How about a beer?" (specific). With でも: ビールでも 飲のみませんか = "How about a beer or something?" (relaxed, open). でも makes the invitation feel more casual and pressure-free.
Grammar 7

V ないと……

"Have to ~" (casual)

📖 How it works

This is a shortened casual version of なければ なりません (L17) and ないと いけません. By dropping いけません (or なりません), you get the casual V ないと…… which trails off with the unspoken meaning understood. It means "I have to / must ~" in everyday casual speech.

📝 Example ⑯
もう 帰かえらないと……。
I have to be getting home...... (implied: いけません)
🔵 Formal (L17)
帰かえらなければ なりません
Must go home
🔴 Casual (L21)
帰かえらないと……
Gotta get home...

L17 V なければ なりません — Formal "must" (full form)

L17 V ないと いけません — Another formal "must"

L21 V ないと…… — Casual "gotta" (shortened)

All three mean the same thing! The formality decreases: なければ なりません > ないと いけません > ないと……

Key Rule

だ — Keep or Drop?

「だ」のルール
Patternな-adj / NounRule
～と 思おもいます静しずかだと 思おもいますKEEP だ ✅
～と 言いいます学生がくせいだと 言いいましたKEEP だ ✅
～でしょう？静しずかだでしょう？DROP だ ❌
Casual question？暇ひまだ？DROP だ ❌
💡 The rule: Before と (quotation particle), KEEP だ — it's part of the quoted statement. Before でしょう and casual question markers, DROP だ — it clashes with the question element. Think: statements keep だ, questions drop だ.

Caution

Common Mistakes

よくある間違まちがい
❌ Polite form before と

× 行いきますと 思おもいます → ○ 行いくと 思おもいます
The clause before と MUST be in plain form (unless it's a direct quote inside 「 」).

❌ Negating 思おもいます instead of the thought

× 来くると 思おもいません → ○ 来こないと 思おもいます
Japanese negates the CONTENT, not the thinking verb. "I think he won't come" NOT "I don't think he'll come."

❌ Adding だ before でしょう

× 元気げんきだでしょう？ → ○ 元気げんきでしょう？
な-adj/noun: drop だ before でしょう. BUT keep だ before と: 元気げんきだと 思おもいます. This is the trickiest L21 rule!
