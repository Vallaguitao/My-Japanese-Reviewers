Grammar 1

ここ / そこ / あそこ

Place Demonstratives

📖 How it works

In Lesson 2, you learned これ/それ/あれ for things. Now the same ko-so-a distance logic applies to places! The three-way system uses the same perspective: near the speaker, near the listener, and far from both.

📍 Place Distance Diagram
🙋‍♂️📍 ここ here Where the speaker is
🙋‍♂️ ↔ 📍🙋‍♀️ そこ there Where the listener is
🙋‍♂️🙋‍♀️ ↔↔ 📍 あそこ over there Far from both

🔹 Special Case: Shared Space

When the speaker considers the listener to be sharing the same territory (for example, both standing in the same room), their shared space becomes ここ. In this situation, そこ refers to a place slightly distant from both of them, and あそこ is even further away.

💡 Key Point: The ko-so-a pattern is beautifully consistent in Japanese: こ = near me, そ = near you, あ = far from us both. Whether it's things (これ), modifiers (この), or places (ここ), the logic never changes!

Building on L1-L2 & Looking Ahead:

L2 これ/それ/あれ (things) → now ここ/そこ/あそこ (places) — same system, new category!

L10 ここ に N が あります/います — combining places with existence verbs.

L14 ここで 待まってください ("Please wait here") — places with て-form requests.

L23 ここを 右みぎに 曲まがってください ("Turn right here") — directions.

Grammar 2

こちら / そちら / あちら

Polite Direction Words

📖 How it works

こちら, そちら, and あちら refer to direction and can also be used as politer alternatives for ここ, そこ, and あそこ. When speaking to customers, strangers, or in formal situations, こちら/そちら/あちら sounds much more refined.

🔵 こちら
this way / here
Polite ここ
🟢 そちら
that way / there
Polite そこ
🩷 あちら
that way (far) / over there
Polite あそこ
💬 Example ⑤ — Asking for directions
A: エレベーターは どちらですか。
Where's the lift?
B: あちらです。
It's in that direction. / It's over there.
🎩 Polite (formal / service)
こちらです。
It's this way. (refined)
💬 Casual (everyday)
ここです。
It's here. (normal)
💡 Key Point: You'll hear こちら everywhere in Japan — at stores, restaurants, and hotels. Staff say こちらへどうぞ ("This way, please") constantly. It's one of the first real-world Japanese phrases you'll recognize!

Grammar 3

N は place です

〜は 〜です（場所ばしょ）
📐 Structure
N (thing/person) + は + Place + です。

📖 How it works

This is the same N₁ は N₂ です pattern from Lesson 1, but now N₂ is a place. You can use it to state where a thing, person, or facility is located. The subject goes before は, and the location goes after it.

📝 Example ①
お手洗てあらいは あそこです。
The toilet is over there.
📝 Example ②
電話でんわは 2階かいです。
The telephone is on the second floor.
📝 Example ③
山田やまださんは 事務所じむしょです。
Mr. Yamada is in his office.
💡 Key Point: This pattern works for both things AND people! When used with a person, it means "that person is at [place]." It's essentially the same structure you mastered in L1 — only the type of information in N₂ has changed from identity to location.

Location patterns evolve:

L1 N₁ は N₂ です (identity) → now N は place です (location) — same structure, new meaning!

L10 More precise locations: つくえの 上うえに 本ほんが あります ("There's a book on the desk").

L11 Counting with locations: 教室きょうしつに 学生がくせいが 3人にん います ("There are 3 students in the classroom").

L15 Activities at locations: 公園こうえんで サッカーを します ("I play soccer at the park").

Grammar 4

どこ / どちら

Where? / Which direction?

📖 How it works

どこ means "where?" and is used for asking the location of something. どちら is the politer version, and it can also mean "which direction?" Just as こちら/そちら/あちら are polite forms of ここ/そこ/あそこ, どちら is the polite form of どこ.

Both どこ and どちら can also be used to ask about a person's country, company, school, or organization. In this usage, なん ("what") cannot be used — only どこ or どちら.

💬 Example ④ — Asking where
A: お手洗てあらいは どこですか。
Where's the toilet?
B: あそこです。
It's over there.
💬 Example ⑥ — Asking about school/company
A: 学校がっこうは どこですか。
What school do you go to? (lit. "Where is your school?")
A: 会社かいしゃは どちらですか。
What company do you work for? (polite)
🎩 どちら (polite)
会社かいしゃは どちらですか。
What company? (refined)
💬 どこ (everyday)
学校がっこうは どこですか。
What school? (casual polite)
💡 Key Point: When asking "What school/company/country?", use どこ or どちら — NOT なん. Saying × 学校がっこうは 何なんですか is unnatural. Think of it as "Where is your school affiliated?" rather than "What is your school?"

Question words grow lesson by lesson:

L1 どなた = who (polite). Now you have どこ/どちら = where.

L2 何なん = what. Remember: use どこ (not なん) for organizations.

L4 いつ = when, 何時なんじ = what time — more interrogatives!

L9 どうして = why — completing the major question word set.

Grammar 5

N₁ の N₂ — Origin

〜の〜（産地さんち・メーカー）

📖 How it works

You've already seen の for affiliation (L1), topic/ownership (L2). Now in Lesson 3, の expands again to indicate where a product is made or which company makes it.

When N₁ is a country, it means the product is made in that country. When N₁ is a company, it means the product is made by that company. The interrogative どこ is used to ask about origin.

💬 Example ⑧ — Asking about origin
A: これは どこの コンピューターですか。
Where is this computer made? / What company is this computer made by?
B: 日本にほんの コンピューターです。
It's made in Japan.
B: パワー電気でんきの コンピューターです。
It's made by Power Electric Company.
Use of の N₁ の N₂ Meaning Lesson
AffiliationIMCの社員しゃいんIMC's employeeL1
Topicコンピューターの本ほんbook about computersL2
Ownershipわたしの本ほんmy bookL2
Made in日本にほんのコンピューターcomputer made in JapanL3
Made byパワー電気でんきのコンピューターcomputer by Power ElectricL3
💡 Key Point: The particle の is the Swiss Army knife of Japanese! Each lesson adds a new use. By now you have 5 different meanings — but the structure (N₁ の N₂) never changes. Context tells you which meaning to use.

The ever-growing の:

L1 Affiliation → L2 Topic/Ownership → L3 Origin/Maker

L5 Material/type: 木きの テーブル ("wooden table").

L8 Description: 日本にほんの 映画えいが ("Japanese movie").

L22 Nominalizer: 食たべるのが 好すきです ("I like eating") — の turns verbs into nouns!

Grammar 6

こ / そ / あ / ど System

指示詞しじしの一覧いちらん

📖 The Big Picture

After 3 lessons, you now know the complete demonstrative chart! The こ/そ/あ rows show distance (near me / near you / far from both), and the ど column adds the question word ("which?" / "where?"). This is one of the most systematic and important patterns in all of Japanese.

Category こ〜 series そ〜 series あ〜 series ど〜 series
Thing これ それ あれ どれ (L16)
Thing/Person + N この N その N あの N どの N (L16)
Place ここ そこ あそこ どこ
Direction / Place (polite) こちら そちら あちら どちら
🔑 Master Tip: If you memorize this 4×4 grid, you've learned 16 words with just one logical system! The prefixes (こ/そ/あ/ど) and the suffixes (〜れ/〜の/〜こ/〜ちら) combine predictably. You'll add どれ and どの in Lesson 16 to complete the grid.

Chart milestones across lessons:

L2 これ/それ/あれ + この/その/あの — things & modifiers.

L3 ここ/そこ/あそこ + こちら/そちら/あちら + どこ/どちら — places & directions.

L16 どれ/どの — "which one?" choosing from 3+ options (completes the ど series!).

Grammar 7

お〜 Polite Prefix

お + 言葉ことば (Expanded)

📖 How it works

You first saw お in Lesson 2 (おみやげ, おさけ). Now Lesson 3 deepens this: お is added to words related to the listener or a third party to show the speaker's respect toward them. It's a way of being courteous about things that belong to or are associated with others.

📝 Example ⑨
[お]国くには どちらですか。
What country are you from? (polite — the お honors the listener's country)
🎩 With お (respectful)
お名前なまえは？
Your name? (polite — other's name)
🙋 Without お (neutral/own)
名前なまえは 田中たなかです。
My name is Tanaka. (no お for yourself)
💡 Key Point: Generally, don't use お for your own things — it's for showing respect toward others' things. Saying お国くに about your own country sounds odd. The exception is set phrases like お茶ちゃ and おみやげ where お is part of the standard word.

Politeness continues building:

L1 さん — the first honorific. L2 おみやげ, おさけ — fixed polite words.

L3 お国くに — respectful when asking about someone else.

L14 お願ねがいします — "Please" (polite request expression).

L25 お + verb stem: お待まちください ("Please wait") — full honorific verb form.
