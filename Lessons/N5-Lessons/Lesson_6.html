Grammar 1

N を V (transitive)

目的語もくてきごの を
📐 Structure
N (object) + を + V (transitive)

🎉 Another Milestone!

The particle を marks the direct object — the thing that receives the action of a transitive verb. Just like English "I drink juice," the を tells you what is being acted upon. This is one of the most important and frequently used particles in Japanese.

📝 Example ① — from textbook
ジュースを 飲のみます。
I drink juice.
📝 More examples
本ほんを 読よみます。
I read a book.
手紙てがみを 書かきます。
I write a letter.
ごはんを 食たべます。
I eat rice / a meal.
⚠️ Writing Note: The particle を is written using the hiragana を, but pronounced 「お」(o). を is only used as a particle — you'll never see it inside a regular word!

を opens up the world of transitive verbs:

L4 V ます (intransitive actions like 起おきます) → L6 N を V ます (transitive: 本ほんを 読よみます).

L5 どこ[へ]も V ません → L6 何なにも V ません ("not ~ anything").

L7 N を あげます/もらいます — giving and receiving objects.

L14 N を + て-form: 写真しゃしんを 撮とってください ("Please take a photo").

Grammar 2

N を します

Do [activity]

📖 How it works

The verb します (do) is incredibly versatile. A wide range of nouns can be used as its objects with を, and the meaning changes depending on the noun. The textbook organizes these into three categories:

⚽ 1) Sports & Games
サッカーを します
play soccer
トランプを します
play cards
🎉 2) Events & Gatherings
パーティーを します
throw a party
会議かいぎを します
hold a meeting
📋 3) Tasks & Activities
宿題しゅくだいを します
do homework
仕事しごとを します
work (do a job)
電話でんわを します
make a phone call
💡 Key Point: します is a "Swiss Army knife" verb — it turns almost any activity noun into an action. If you're not sure which verb to use, try N を します and it will often be correct! You'll use this pattern constantly throughout the course.

Grammar 3

何なにを しますか

What will you do?

📖 How it works

This is a question pattern used to ask about someone's actions or plans. 何なにを しますか literally means "What will you do?" and the answer uses the same を structure with a specific action. Note that the response doesn't always use します — any appropriate verb works.

💬 Example ② — Plans
A: 月曜日げつようび 何なにを しますか。
What are you doing on Monday?
B: 京都きょうとへ 行いきます。
I'm going to Kyoto.
💬 Example ③ — Past
A: きのう 何なにを しましたか。
What did you do yesterday?
B: サッカーを しました。
I played soccer.
💡 Key Point: The answer to 何なにを しますか doesn't have to use します! You can answer with any verb: 京都きょうとへ 行いきます, 本ほんを 読よみます, 友達ともだちと 映画えいがを 見みます, etc. The question asks about any action, not specifically about します.
Grammar 4

なん vs. なに

何の読よみ方かた

📖 How it works

Both なん and なに are readings of the kanji 何 ("what"), but they're used in different situations. The choice depends on what sound comes after 何.

📗 なん — use before:

1) た/だ/な row sounds (t, d, n)

2) Counter suffixes

それは 何なんですか。 ④
What's that?
何なんの 本ほんですか。 ⑤
What's that book about?
何なん歳さいですか。 ⑨
How old?
📙 なに — all other cases

Before particles (を, が, etc.)

Before verbs

何なにを 買かいますか。 ⑩
What will you buy?
何なにを しますか。
What will you do?

🔹 Special Case: なんで

When 何 appears before で, it's read as なんで and can mean both "How?" (by what means) and "Why?" The textbook notes that なにで is sometimes used specifically for "How?" to avoid ambiguity.

📝 Example ⑦⑧ — Transport
何なんで 東京とうきょうへ 行いきますか。
How are you getting to Tokyo? (by what means?)
新幹線しんかんせんで 行いきます。
I'm going on the Shinkansen.
💡 Quick Rule: Before d, t, n sounds or counters → なん. Everything else → なに. When unsure, listen to native speakers — this becomes intuitive with practice!

Grammar 5

N(place) で V

場所ばしょの で
📐 Structure
Place + で + Verb (action)

📖 How it works

In Lesson 5 you learned で for transport (電車でんしゃで = by train). Now で gains a second meaning: it marks the place where an action occurs. Think of it as "at" or "in" when referring to where you do something.

📝 Example ⑪
駅えきで 新聞しんぶんを 買かいます。
I'm going to buy a newspaper at the station.
📝 More examples
うちで 映画えいがを 見みます。
I watch movies at home.
図書館としょかんで 勉強べんきょうします。
I study at the library.
で MeaningExampleLesson
Means of transport電車でんしゃで 行いきますL5
Location of action駅えきで 買かいますL6
💡 Key Point: で marks where an action takes place. Don't confuse it with に, which you'll learn marks where something exists (L10: つくえの 上うえに あります). Action = で, Existence = に. This distinction is crucial!

で keeps growing:

L5 Transport: 電車でんしゃで → L6 Location: 駅えきで 買かいます.

L7 Tool/Instrument: はしで 食たべます ("eat with chopsticks").

L10 Important contrast: Place で V (action) vs. Place に あります/います (existence).

L15 公園こうえんで サッカーを します — combining place で + object を in one sentence.

Grammar 6

V ませんか

"Won't you ~?" (Invitation)
📐 Structure
V + ませんか

📖 How it works

This expression is used to invite someone to do something. It literally translates to "Won't you ~?" and is a polite, considerate way to propose an activity. It shows the speaker is giving consideration to what the listener might want.

💬 Example ⑫
A: いっしょに 京都きょうとへ 行いきませんか。
Would you like to go to Kyoto with us?
B: ええ、いいですね。
Yes, that's a nice idea.
💡 Key Point: V ませんか looks like a negative question, but it's actually a polite invitation. Think of the English "Won't you join us?" — it's technically negative, but the meaning is an invitation. It's considered softer and more considerate than V ましょう.
Grammar 7

V ましょう

"Let's ~!" (Suggestion)
📐 Structure
V + ましょう

📖 How it works

This expression is used to positively suggest doing something together with the listener, or to accept an invitation. It's like saying "Let's ~!" or "Shall we ~?" It's also used as a positive response to V ませんか.

💬 Example ⑬ — Suggesting
A: ちょっと 休やすみましょう。
Let's take a break.
💬 Example ⑭ — Invitation + response
A: いっしょに 昼ひるごはんを 食たべませんか。
Shall we have lunch together?
B: ええ、食たべましょう。
Yes, let's (eat).
🤲 V ませんか — Considerate invitation
映画えいがを 見みませんか。
Won't you watch a movie? (softer)
More considerate of listener's feelings
🙌 V ましょう — Positive suggestion
映画えいがを 見みましょう。
Let's watch a movie! (direct)
Assumes both parties are on board
💡 Key Difference: V ませんか is a tentative invitation that respects the listener's choice (softer). V ましょう is a positive, confident proposal or agreement (more direct). Use ませんか to invite, and ましょう to agree or propose.

L6 V ませんか / V ましょう — your first invitation forms.

L14 V てください — polite requests ("Please do ~").

L15 V てもいいですか — asking permission ("May I ~?").

L25 V ましょうか — offering help ("Shall I ~?" = offering to do for someone).

Grammar 8

〜か

Acknowledging with Interest

📖 How it works

The particle か at the end of a statement (not a question) is used to indicate that the listener has received and acknowledged new information. It works the same way as そうですか from L2 — showing you've registered what someone said. Often it carries a nuance of interest or mild surprise.

💬 Example ⑮
A: 日曜日にちようび 京都きょうとへ 行いきました。
I went to Kyoto on Sunday.
B: 京都きょうとですか。いいですね。
Kyoto, eh? That's nice.
💡 Listener Response Summary (L1–L6):
そうですか↓ (L2) = "I see" (acknowledging new info, neutral)
〜ですか (L6) = "~, huh?" (echoing key word with interest)
そうですね (L5) = "Indeed" (agreeing with shared knowledge)
〜ね (L4) = "~, right?" (seeking agreement)
