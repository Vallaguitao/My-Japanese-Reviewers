Grammar 1

今いま 何時なんじですか

Telling the Time
📐 Structure
Number + 時じ　Number + 分ふん

📖 How it works

To tell the time, attach 時じ (o'clock) to the hour and 分ふん (minutes) to the minutes. To ask the time, use 何時なんじ (what time?) and 何分なんぷん (what minute?). The word 今いま means "now."

💬 Example ①
A: 今いま 何時なんじですか。
What time is it now?
B: 7時じ10分ぷんです。
It's 7:10.

⚠️ Tricky Pronunciations

Some hours and minutes have irregular readings. Watch out for these:

HourReadingMinutesReading
4時じよじ (not しじ)1分ぷんいっぷん
7時じしちじ3分ぷんさんぷん
9時じくじ6分ぷんろっぷん
何時なんじなんじ8分ぷんはっぷん
10分ぷんじゅっぷん
💡 Key Point: 分 is read as ふん after 2, 5, 7, 9 and as ぷん after 1, 3, 4, 6, 8, 10. The hour readings for 4 (よじ), 7 (しちじ), and 9 (くじ) are the most commonly tested irregulars!

Time expressions grow throughout the course:

L5 Days of the week (月曜日げつようび〜), dates, months, years — a full calendar system.

L11 Counters for duration: 〜時間じかん (hours), 〜分ふん (minutes as duration).

L18 ことが あります with time references.

Grammar 2

V ます — Your First Verbs!

動詞どうしの ます形けい

🎉 A Milestone Moment!

Until now, every sentence you've made used です (the copula). Starting with this lesson, you can use verbs — the action words that bring Japanese to life! The ます form is the polite way to use verbs, and it's the form you'll use in most everyday conversations.

📐 Four Forms of ます
✅ Affirmative (Non-past)
V ます
I do / will do
❌ Negative (Non-past)
V ません
I don't / won't do
✅ Affirmative (Past)
V ました
I did
❌ Negative (Past)
V ませんでした
I didn't do

📖 How it works

The ます ending shows politeness toward the listener — just like です. The non-past form (ます/ません) covers both present habit and future actions, while the past form (ました/ませんでした) covers completed actions. Context tells you which meaning is intended.

💡 Key Point: Japanese verbs don't change based on the subject (I/you/he/she/they). おきます means "I wake up," "you wake up," "she wakes up" — all the same form! Context tells you who's doing the action.
Grammar 2

V ます — Examples

ます の 使つかい方かた
📝 Example ② — Habitual
わたしは 毎日まいにち 勉強べんきょうします。
I study every day.
📝 Example ③ — Future/Present
毎朝まいあさ 6時じに 起おきます。
I get up at six every morning.
📝 Example ④ — Future
あした 6時じに 起おきます。
I'm going to get up at six tomorrow morning.
📝 Example ⑤ — Past
けさ 6時じに 起おきました。
I got up at six this morning.

🔹 Verb Questions

Just like with です, add か at the end to make a question. The reply echoes the verb from the question.

💬 Example ⑥ — Past Question
A: きのう 勉強べんきょうしましたか。
Did you study yesterday?
B: はい、勉強べんきょうしました。
Yes, I did [study].
B: いいえ、勉強べんきょうしませんでした。
No, I didn't [study].
⚠️ Important: You cannot use そうです / ちがいます (from L2) to answer verb questions — those only work for noun sentences. For verbs, repeat the verb in your answer!

Verb forms evolve dramatically:

L1-3 Only です (noun sentences). L4 unlocks verbs for the first time!

L6 Object + を + V: 本ほんを 読よみます ("I read a book").

L14 て-form: 食たべて ください ("Please eat") — a new conjugation.

L17 ない-form: 食たべないで ください ("Please don't eat").

L19 た-form: 行いったことが あります ("I have been").

Grammar 3

N(時じかん) に V

Time Particle に
📐 Structure
Time Noun + に + Verb

📖 How it works

The particle に is added after a specific time noun to indicate when an action occurs. Think of it as the Japanese equivalent of "at" (for time). However, に is only used with specific/numerical time — not with relative time words.

📝 Example ⑧
6時半じはんに 起おきます。
I get up at six-thirty.
📝 Example ⑨
7月がつ2日かに 日本にほんへ 来きました。
I came to Japan on the 2nd of July.
✅ に USED (specific time)
6時じに 起おきます。
I wake up at 6 o'clock.
❌ に NOT USED (relative time)
きのう 勉強べんきょうしました。
I studied yesterday. (no に)
✅ に required (specific)❌ に NOT used (relative)
6時じに (at 6 o'clock)きょう (today)
7月がつ2日かに (on July 2nd)あした (tomorrow)
月曜日げつようびに (on Monday) *optionalきのう (yesterday)
来年らいねんに (next year) *optionalけさ (this morning)
毎日まいにち (every day)
💡 Key Point: Easy rule — if the time word contains a number (like 6時, 7月2日), use に. If it's a relative word (like きょう, あした, きのう, まいにち), don't use に. For days of the week, に is optional.

に has multiple lives:

L4 Time に: 6時じに 起おきます — when an action happens.

L5 Destination に: 日本にほんに 行いきます ("go TO Japan").

L7 Recipient に: 友達ともだちに あげます ("give TO a friend").

L10 Location に: つくえの 上うえに あります ("exists ON the desk").

Grammar 4

N₁ から N₂ まで

From ~ To ~
📐 Structure
N₁ から N₂ まで + Verb / です

📖 How it works

から marks a starting point (time or place) and まで marks an ending point. They can be used together ("from ~ to ~") or separately ("from ~" / "until ~"). When used as a topic, attach は to the whole phrase: ～から ～まで は.

📝 Example ⑫
9時じから 5時じまで 勉強べんきょうします。
I study from 9 to 5.
📝 Example ⑬ — Place
大阪おおさかから 東京とうきょうまで 3時間じかん かかります。
It takes 3 hours from Osaka to Tokyo. (See L11)
📝 Example ⑭ — から alone
9時じから 働はたらきます。
I start work at nine. (from 9)
📝 Example ⑮ — As a topic with は
銀行ぎんこうは 9時じから 3時じまでです。
The bank is open from 9 to 3.
📝 Example ⑯ — まで alone
昼休ひるやすみは 12時じからです。
The lunch break starts at 12.
💡 Key Point: から and まで don't always need each other! から alone = starting point ("from ~"), まで alone = ending point ("until ~"). They also work for places (from Osaka to Tokyo), not just time!

L5 月曜日げつようびから 金曜日きんようびまで ("Monday through Friday").

L11 Place + から: 駅えきから 歩あるいて 10分ぷん ("10 min walk from the station").

L23 Conditional: 駅えきに 着ついたら、電話でんわから かけてください ("When you arrive, call from the station phone").

Grammar 5

N₁ と N₂

"And" for Nouns
📐 Structure
N₁ と N₂

📖 How it works

The particle と connects two nouns in a coordinate relationship — just like "and" in English. It's used when listing specific items. Unlike English, と only connects nouns, not sentences or verbs.

📝 Example ⑰
銀行ぎんこうの 休やすみは 土曜日どようびと 日曜日にちようびです。
The bank is closed on Saturdays and Sundays.
💡 Key Point: と is an exhaustive list — it implies these are ALL the items. If you say "土曜日どようびと日曜日にちようび", it means only Saturday and Sunday, nothing else. For non-exhaustive lists ("things like A and B"), you'll learn や in a later lesson.

L4 N₁ と N₂: exhaustive "and" — lists ALL items.

L10 N₁ や N₂: non-exhaustive "and" — lists some examples from a larger group.

L6 ～と いっしょに: "together with ~" — a different use of と!

Grammar 6

〜ね

Confirmation Particle

📖 How it works

The particle ね is added at the end of a sentence to seek agreement, confirm something, or emphasize a point to the listener. It's like saying "isn't it?", "right?", or "don't you think?" in English. It creates a warm, conversational tone and shows you expect the listener to agree or acknowledge what you said.

💬 Example ⑱ — Seeking agreement
A: 毎日まいにち 10時じまで 勉強べんきょうします。
I study till ten every day.
B: 大変たいへんですね。
That's tough, isn't it?
💬 Example ⑲ — Confirming info
A: 山田やまださんの 電話番号でんわばんごうは 871の6813です。
Mr. Yamada's phone number is 871-6813.
B: 871の6813ですね。
871-6813, right?
💡 Key Point: ね is one of the most frequently used particles in casual Japanese. It makes you sound natural and engaged. Without ね, statements can sound flat or detached. But don't overuse it — adding ね to every sentence sounds unnatural too!

L2 そうですか (acknowledging) → now ね (confirming/seeking agreement).

L8 いい 天気てんきですね ("Nice weather, isn't it?") — one of the most common uses.

L21 〜でしょう？: a stronger seeking-agreement form. ～ね is softer.

🗣️ Putting it all together

A conversation about daily routines using every grammar point from this lesson.

💬 Talking About Daily Life
すずき: ミラーさん、毎朝まいあさ 何時なんじに 起おきますか。
Mr. Miller, what time do you get up every morning?
ミラー: 6時半じはんに 起おきます。
I get up at 6:30.
すずき: 仕事しごとは 何時なんじから 何時なんじまでですか。
What hours do you work? (From what time to what time?)
ミラー: 9時じから 5時じまでです。
From 9 to 5.
すずき: 土曜日どようびと 日曜日にちようびも 働はたらきますか。
Do you also work Saturdays and Sundays?
ミラー: いいえ、働はたらきません。土曜日どようびと日曜日にちようびは 休やすみです。
No, I don't. Saturdays and Sundays are days off.
すずき: いいですね。
That's nice, isn't it?
🔍 Spot the grammar! This conversation uses: 何時なんじ (time question), に (time particle), ます/ません (verb forms), から〜まで (range), と (and), も (also, from L1!), and ね (confirmation).

🚀 Why Lesson 4 is a turning point

Lessons 1–3 were all about noun sentences (N₁ は N₂ です). You could describe things, identify objects, and talk about places — but you couldn't describe actions. Lesson 4 changes everything by introducing verbs.

The ます-form you just learned is your entry point into the world of Japanese verbs. Over the next 21 lessons, verb forms will expand dramatically. Here's a roadmap of what's ahead:

🎯 You now have: Noun sentences (L1–3) + Verb sentences (L4) + Time expressions + Particles (に, から, まで, と, ね). That's a real communicative toolkit!
