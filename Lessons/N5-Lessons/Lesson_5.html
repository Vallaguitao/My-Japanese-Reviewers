Grammar 1

N(place) へ 行いきます/来きます/帰かえります

Direction Particle へ
📐 Structure
Place + へ + 行いきます / 来きます / 帰かえります

📖 How it works

When a verb indicates movement to a place, the particle へ is placed after the destination to show the direction of the move. The three movement verbs you need are: 行いきます (go), 来きます (come), and 帰かえります (go home / return).

🚶 行いきます
京都きょうとへ 行いきます。
I'm going to Kyoto.
🚶‍♀️ 来きます
日本にほんへ 来きました。
I came to Japan.
🏠 帰かえります
うちへ 帰かえります。
I'm going home.
⚠️ Pronunciation Note: The particle へ is always pronounced 「え」(e), not "he"! This is one of the most important pronunciation rules in Japanese — just like は is read as "wa" when used as a particle.

Movement verbs & destination particles evolve:

L3 ここ/そこ/あそこ — place words you already know slot right into 「place へ V」!

L4 に for time → now へ for direction. Both are essential particles.

L6 を with other verbs: 本ほんを 読よみます ("read a book") — different particle, different verb type.

L13 N へ V に 行いきます: 買かい物ものに 行いきます ("go shopping") — purpose of going.

Grammar 2

どこ[へ]も V ません

Negative with Interrogative + も
📐 Structure
Interrogative + [particle] + も + V ません

📖 How it works

When you combine a question word (like どこ, 何なに, だれ) with も and a negative verb, it creates a total negation — "not ~ anywhere," "not ~ anything," "not ~ anybody." This is a very powerful and commonly used pattern.

📝 Example ④
どこ[へ]も 行いきません。
I'm not going anywhere.
📝 Example ⑤ — preview of Lesson 6
何なにも 食たべません。
I'm not going to eat anything.
📝 Example ⑥
だれも 来きませんでした。
Nobody came.
Interrogative+ も + NegativeMeaning
どこどこ[へ]も 行いきませんnot go anywhere
何なに何なにも 食たべませんnot eat anything (L6)
だれだれも 来きませんnobody comes/came
💡 Key Point: This pattern ONLY works with negative verbs! If you use an affirmative verb, も means "also" (from L1). The negative verb is what transforms the meaning from "also" to "nothing/nowhere/nobody."

L1 N も = "also." Now interrogative + も + negative = total negation — completely different meaning!

L6 何なにも 食たべません ("not eat anything") — this pattern with を-verbs.

L10 どこにも ありません ("not anywhere / doesn't exist anywhere").

Grammar 3

N(vehicle) で 行いきます

Means of Transport
📐 Structure
Vehicle/Transport + で + 行いきます / 来きます / 帰かえります

📖 How it works

The particle で indicates a means or method. When placed after a vehicle noun, it tells you how you travel. Think of it like "by" in English (by train, by bus, by taxi).

📝 Example ⑦
電車でんしゃで 行いきます。
I'm going by train.
📝 Example ⑧
タクシーで 来きました。
I came by taxi.

🚶 Special Case: Walking

When talking about walking, use the expression あるいて (on foot). In this case, the particle で is not used because walking isn't a vehicle.

📝 Example ⑨
駅えきから あるいて 帰かえりました。
I walked home from the station.
💡 Key Point: で is used for vehicles: 電車でんしゃで, バスで, タクシーで, 自転車じてんしゃで, 飛行機ひこうきで, 船ふねで. But for walking, say あるいて (no で). This is a common test question!

L5 で for means of transport: 電車でんしゃで 行いきます.

L7 で for tools/instruments: はしで 食たべます ("eat with chopsticks").

L15 で for location of action: 公園こうえんで 遊あそびます ("play at the park").

Grammar 4

N(person/animal) と V

Doing Something With Someone
📐 Structure
Person + と + Verb

📖 How it works

In Lesson 4, you learned と to connect nouns ("A and B"). Now in Lesson 5, と has a second use: marking the person or animal you do something together with. The question word is だれと ("with whom?").

If you do something by yourself, use the expression ひとりで — in this case, と is not used.

📝 Example ⑩
家族かぞくと 日本にほんへ 来きました。
I came to Japan with my family.
📝 Example ⑪ — Alone
ひとりで 東京とうきょうへ 行いきます。
I'm going to Tokyo on my own.
👫 With someone
友達ともだちと 行いきます。
I go with a friend.
🙋 Alone
ひとりで 行いきます。
I go alone. (no と)
💡 Key Point: と now has two meanings! In L4, N₁ と N₂ = "A and B" (connecting nouns). In L5, Person と V = "with [person]" (companion). Context makes the meaning clear.

L4 N₁ と N₂ = "and" (connecting) → L5 Person と V = "with" (companion).

L6 ～と いっしょに: "友達ともだちと いっしょに" = "together with a friend" (emphatic).

L21 と 思おもいます: "I think that ~" — yet another use of と!

Grammar 5

いつ

When? (Question Word)

📖 How it works

The interrogative いつ means "when" and is used to ask when something will happen or happened. Just like other relative time words (きょう, あした, きのう), いつ does NOT take the particle に.

For specific time questions, you can also use 何時なんじ (what time), 何曜日なんようび (what day of the week), 何月なんがつ (what month), and 何日なんにち (what date).

💬 Example ⑫
A: いつ 日本にほんへ 来きましたか。
When did you come to Japan?
B: 3月がつ25日にちに 来きました。
I came on March 25th.
💬 Example ⑬
A: いつ 広島ひろしまへ 行いきますか。
When are you going to Hiroshima?
B: 来週らいしゅう 行いきます。
I'm going next week.
💡 Key Point: いつ = relative time word → NO に. But the answer might be specific time (3月がつ25日にち) → に required. This mirrors the rule from L4!

Your question word collection grows:

L1 どなた (who, polite) · L2 何なん (what) · L3 どこ/どちら (where)

L4 何時なんじ (what time) · L5 いつ (when), だれと (with whom)

L9 どうして (why) — completing the main set!

Grammar 6

〜よ

Assertive/Informing Particle

📖 How it works

The particle よ is placed at the end of a sentence to emphasize information the listener doesn't know or to assertively give your opinion or judgment. It's like saying "you know," "I tell you," or "I'm sure of it" in English.

Compare this with ね (from L4): ね seeks agreement about something the listener probably already knows, while よ gives new information or pushes your point.

💬 Example ⑭ — Providing new information
A: この 電車でんしゃは 甲子園こうしえんへ 行いきますか。
Does this train go to Koshien?
B: いいえ、行いきません。次つぎの「ふつう」ですよ。
No, it doesn't. It's the next local [train], I tell you.
🤝 ね — seeking agreement
いい 天気てんきですね。
Nice weather, isn't it? (you agree?)
☝️ よ — informing / asserting
おいしいですよ。
It's delicious, you know! (trust me)
💡 Key Point: ね = "right?" (you expect the listener agrees). よ = "I'm telling you!" (you're giving info the listener doesn't have). They can even combine: 〜よね = "It is ~ , isn't it?" (asserting AND seeking agreement). You'll hear all three constantly in real conversations!
Grammar 7

そうですね

Expressing Agreement

📖 How it works

The expression そうですね is used to show sympathy or agreement with what the speaker has said or what you already know. It's like saying "That's right, isn't it" or "Yes, indeed."

Don't confuse it with そうですか (from L2), which acknowledges new information ("I see"). そうですね agrees with something you already thought or knew.

💬 Example ⑰
A: あしたは 日曜日にちようびですね。
Tomorrow is Sunday, isn't it?
B: ああ、そうですね。
Oh, yes, so it is.
🤝 そうですね — agreement
そうですね。
"Yes, that's right." / "Indeed."
Speaker already knows/agrees
💡 そうですか — acknowledging new info
そうですか。
"I see." / "Oh, really."
Speaker just learned something new
💡 Summary of Listener Responses (L1–L5):
そうですか↓ (L2) = "I see" (new info)
～ね (L4) = "right?" (seeking agreement)
そうですね (L5) = "Indeed / that's right" (agreement)
～よ (L5) = "I'm telling you" (asserting info)
