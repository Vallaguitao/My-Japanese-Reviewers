Grammar 1

N(tool/means) で V

道具どうぐ・手段しゅだんの で
📐 Structure
Tool / Means + で + Verb

📖 How it works

You already know で for transport (L5: 電車でんしゃで) and location of action (L6: 駅えきで). Now で gains its third meaning: marking the tool, instrument, or means used to perform an action. Think of it as "with" or "using" in English.

📝 Example ① — from textbook
はしで 食たべます。
I eat with chopsticks.
📝 Example ②
日本語にほんごで レポートを 書かきます。
I'm going to write the report in Japanese.
で MeaningExampleLesson
Means of transport電車でんしゃで 行いきますL5
Location of action駅えきで 買かいますL6
Tool / Means / Languageはしで 食たべますL7
💡 Key Point: で is now a triple-threat particle! The core idea behind all three uses is "by means of" — you travel BY MEANS OF a train, you do something AT a place (the place is the means/setting), and you act BY MEANS OF a tool. This unified concept makes で easier to remember!

L5 Transport で → L6 Location で → L7 Tool/means で. Three uses, one core idea!

L10 Contrast: で (action) vs. に (existence). 公園こうえんで 遊あそびます vs. 公園こうえんに あります.

Grammar 2

〜は ～語ごで 何なんですか

"What is ~ in [language]?"

📖 How it works

This pattern uses で in its "means" sense — the language is the means by which you express something. It's an essential phrase for language learners! You use it to ask how to say a word or sentence in another language.

💬 Example ③
A: 「ありがとう」は 英語えいごで 何なんですか。
What's "Arigatou" in English?
B: 「Thank you」です。
It's "Thank you."
💬 Example ④
A: 「Thank you」は 日本語にほんごで 何なんですか。
What's "Thank you" in Japanese?
B: 「ありがとう」です。
It's "Arigatou."
💡 Survival Phrase: This is one of the most useful patterns in real life! If you're studying in Japan and don't know a word, just point at something and say: これは 日本語にほんごで 何なんですか。 ("What is this in Japanese?")

Grammar 3

N₁(person) に N₂ を あげます

Giving
📐 Structure
[わたしは] Person + に + Thing + を + あげます / 貸かします / 教おしえます

📖 How it works

Verbs like あげます (give), 貸かします (lend), and 教おしえます (teach) describe imparting things or information to someone. The particle に marks the recipient — the person who receives.

🙋‍♂️
わたし (I)
🎁 ➡️ あげます
👩
Person に
📝 Example ⑤
[わたしは] 木村きむらさんに 花はなを あげました。
I gave Ms. Kimura some flowers.
📝 Example ⑥ — Lending
[わたしは] イーさんに 本ほんを 貸かしました。
I lent Ms. Lee a book.
📝 Example ⑦ — Teaching
[わたしは] 山田やまださんに 英語えいごを 教おしえます。
I teach Mr. Yamada English.
💡 Key Point: The particle に here marks the recipient (the person you give TO). This is the fourth use of に! (L4: time, L5: destination, L10: location of existence, L7: recipient). The pattern is: [I] + Person に + Thing を + あげます.
Grammar 4

N₁(person) に N₂ を もらいます

Receiving
📐 Structure
[わたしは] Person + に + Thing + を + もらいます / 借かります / 習ならいます

📖 How it works

The flip side of giving! Verbs like もらいます (receive), 借かります (borrow), and 習ならいます (learn from) describe receiving things or information. The particle に marks the source — the person you receive from.

👨
Person に
🎁 ➡️ もらいます
🙋‍♂️
わたし (I)
📝 Example ⑧
[わたしは] 山田やまださんに 花はなを もらいました。
I received some flowers from Mr. Yamada.
📝 Example ⑨ — Borrowing
[わたしは] カリナさんに CDを 借かりました。
I borrowed a CD from Karina.
📝 Example ⑩ — Learning
[わたしは] ワンさんに 中国語ちゅうごくごを 習ならいます。
I'm learning Chinese from Mr. Wang.

⚠️ Special Case: から for Organizations

When the source is an organization (company, school, bank) rather than a person, use から instead of に. You can also use から with people, but に cannot be used with organizations.

📝 Example ⑪ — from textbook (person: に OK)
山田やまださんから 花はなを もらいました。
I received flowers from Mr. Yamada. (から also OK for people)
📝 Example ⑫ — from textbook (organization: から only!)
銀行ぎんこうから お金かねを 借かりました。
I borrowed some money from the bank.
💡 に vs から Summary: Person → に or から (both OK). Organization → から only! This is a common test question — remember that に cannot mark a company or school as a source.
Compare

あげます vs. もらいます

Giving vs. Receiving
🎁 あげます — I GIVE to Person
わたしは 木村きむらさんに 花はなを あげました。
I gave flowers TO Ms. Kimura.
に = recipient (the person who gets it)
🙋‍♂️ 🎁➡️ 👩
📥 もらいます — I RECEIVE from Person
わたしは 山田やまださんに 花はなを もらいました。
I received flowers FROM Mr. Yamada.
に = source (the person it comes from)
👨 🎁➡️ 🙋‍♂️
Verb TypeGive (あげる group)Receive (もらう group)
Thingsあげます (give)もらいます (receive)
Lending/Borrowing貸かします (lend)借かります (borrow)
Teaching/Learning教おしえます (teach)習ならいます (learn from)
💡 Tricky Part: に marks the recipient with あげます but the source with もらいます. Both use に, but the direction is opposite! Think of に as pointing to "the other person" in both cases.

Grammar 5

もう V ました

"Already" / "Have you done ~ yet?"
📐 Structures
もう + V ましたか　＝　Have you done ~ yet?

はい、もう + V ました　＝　Yes, I've already done ~

いいえ、まだです　＝　No, not yet

📖 How it works

もう means "already" and is used with V ました to indicate a completed action. When asking whether something has been done, use もう V ましたか. The affirmative answer uses もう V ました, while the negative answer is simply まだです (not yet).

💬 Example ⑬
A: もう 荷物にもつを 送おくりましたか。
Have you sent the baggage yet?
B: はい、[もう] 送おくりました。
Yes, I have [already sent it].
B: いいえ、まだです。
No, not yet.
⚠️ Critical Note: The negative response is まだです — NOT まだ V ませんでした! The textbook explicitly explains that いいえ + まだです means the action has not been completed in the present, not that it wasn't done in the past. Using ませんでした would mean "it was not done in the past" which is a different nuance (see L31 for まだ V ていません).

L7 もう V ました / まだです — simple completed/not yet.

L14 もう V てしまいました — "already finished (and it's done)" — more emphatic completion.

L15 もう + quantity: もう 一いっ杯ぱい = "one more (cup)" — different もう meaning "another/more"!

Grammar 6

助詞じょしの省略しょうりゃく

Particle Omission

📖 How it works

In informal speech, particles are often dropped when the relationship between words is obvious from context. This is very common in everyday conversation, and recognizing it will help you understand natural Japanese. However, as a learner, you should always practice with particles until they become second nature!

📝 Example ⑭ — は omitted
この スプーン[は]、すてきですね。
This spoon is lovely, isn't it? (は dropped)
📝 Example ⑮ — を omitted
コーヒー[を]、もう 一杯いっぱい いかがですか。
Would you like another cup of coffee? (を dropped)
💡 Study Advice: Particle omission is natural in spoken Japanese, but always include particles in your own speech and writing while learning. Once your particle usage is solid and automatic, you'll naturally start dropping them in casual conversation — just like native speakers do!

に — The Multi-Purpose Particle

に の使つかい方かた

📊 All uses of に so far (L1–L7)

The particle に is one of the most versatile in Japanese. By Lesson 7, you've encountered four distinct uses. Understanding this evolution helps you see the pattern.

に UsageMeaningExampleLesson
Time"at" (specific time)6時じに 起おきますL4
Destination"to" (direction)日本にほんに 来きましたL5
Recipient / Source"to" / "from" (person)友達ともだちに あげます / もらいますL7
Location (existence)"at/in" (where things are)つくえの 上うえに ありますL10
💡 Unifying principle: に always points to a target or anchor point — a point in time, a destination, a recipient, a location where something exists. The common thread is "aimed at this specific point."
