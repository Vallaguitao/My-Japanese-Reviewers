Grammar 1a

🔴 Group I ない-form

ない-form のつくり方かた

📖 The Second Major Verb Form

After the て-form (L14), the ない-form is the second crucial conjugation you need to master. It's the plain negative ("don't / doesn't"), and like て-form, how you make it depends on the verb group. For Group I, the final sound of the ます-form (always from the い-column) shifts to the corresponding あ-column sound, then you add ない.

⚠️ Exception: Verbs ending in い!

When the ます-form ends in い (like かいます, あいます), the あ-column sound would be あ — but instead of あない, it becomes わない. Examples: かいます → かわない, あいます → あわない. Also: おいます → おわない, すいます → すわない.

Grammar 1b

🔵 Group II & 🟣 III ない-form

グループ II・III
🔵 Group II — Simple!

ます-form + ない (same as て-form: just replace ending)

食たべます → 食たべない

見みます → 見みない

🟣 Group III — Irregular

Memorize these two:

します → しない

来きます → 来こない

勉強べんきょうします → 勉強べんきょうしない

💡 Pattern recognition: Group II ない-form follows the exact same principle as Group II て-form — just attach the ending to the ます-form stem. Group III is irregular as always. The challenge, as with て-form, is Group I's consonant shifts. But the rule is systematic: い-column → あ-column + ない (with the い→わ exception).
⚠️ 来きます → 来こない — Reading changes!

Notice the kanji 来 changes its reading: き (ます-form) → こ (ない-form). This is one of the trickiest points for learners. The kanji stays the same but the pronunciation shifts!

Grammar 2

V ないで ください

"Please don't ~"
📐 Structure
V ない-form + で ください

📖 How it works

This is the negative counterpart to V て ください (L14). While て ください asks someone to do something, ないで ください asks them NOT to do something. It's used both for direct prohibitions and for considerately telling someone they don't need to worry about something.

📝 Example ① — Prohibition
ここで 写真しゃしんを 撮とらないで ください。
Please don't take photographs here.
📝 Example ② — Considerate
わたしは 元気げんきですから、心配しんぱいしないで ください。
I'm fine, so please don't worry about me.
✅ て ください (L14) — "Please DO"
写真しゃしんを 撮とって ください。
Please take a photo.
🚫 ないで ください (L17) — "Please DON'T"
写真しゃしんを 撮とらないで ください。
Please don't take photos.

L3 N を ください — "Give me N" (object request)

L14 V て ください — "Please DO ~" (positive request)

L17 V ないで ください — "Please DON'T ~" (negative request)

The request system is now complete: you can ask for things (L3), ask people to do things (L14), and ask people NOT to do things (L17)!

Grammar 3

V なければ なりません

"Must do ~"
📐 Structure
V ない-form (drop ない) + なければ なりません

📖 How it works

This pattern expresses obligation — something that must be done. Literally, it means "if you don't do it, it won't do" (なければ = if not, なりません = it won't become/work). Note that this expression is NOT in the negative even though it uses ない — it's a double negative that creates a positive meaning! Take the ない-form, drop the final い, and add ければ なりません.

📝 Example ③
薬くすりを 飲のまなければ なりません。
I must take some medicine.
💡 How to build it step by step:
1. Get ない-form: 飲のみます → 飲のまない
2. Drop final い: 飲のまな（~~い~~）
3. Add ければ なりません: 飲のまなければ なりません
It's long, but the process is mechanical once you know the ない-form!
Grammar 4

V なくても いいです

"Don't have to ~"
📐 Structure
V ない-form (drop ない) + なくても いいです

📖 How it works

This pattern says something is not necessary — "you don't have to / need not do." It's the release from obligation. Like なければ なりません, take the ない-form, drop the final い, and add くても いいです. Notice the parallel with L15's ても いいですか (permission) — なくても いいです is essentially "even if you DON'T do it, it's fine."

📝 Example ④
あした 来こなくても いいです。
You don't have to come tomorrow.
💡 Building it step by step:
1. Get ない-form: 来きます → 来こない
2. Drop final い: 来こな（~~い~~）
3. Add くても いいです: 来こなくても いいです

Grammar 5

N を → N は

Making the Object the Topic

📖 How it works

When the direct object of a verb (marked by を) becomes the topic of the sentence, を is replaced by は. This moves the object to the front of the sentence for emphasis or to establish it as what you're talking about. The meaning stays the same, but the focus shifts.

📝 Example ⑤ — Object as topic
荷物にもつは ここに 置おかないで ください。
Don't put your bags here, please.
Before (を = object)
ここに 荷物にもつを 置おかないで ください。
Please don't put bags here.
Normal word order — bags as object
After (は = topic)
荷物にもつは ここに 置おかないで ください。
As for the bags, don't put them here.
Topicalized — bags are the focus/topic
📝 Example ⑥ — Lunch topic
昼ひるごはんは 会社かいしゃの 食堂しょくどうで 食たべます。
I have lunch in the company cafeteria.
💡 Why topicalize? When something is already known or has been mentioned, making it the topic (は) is more natural than treating it as new information (を). Think of it as: "Speaking of [that thing]..." This is a fundamental Japanese discourse pattern that makes your sentences sound more natural.
Grammar 6

N(time) までに V

"By (deadline)"
📐 Structure
Time までに V

📖 How it works

までに indicates a deadline — the time by which something must be completed or will end. It differs from まで (L4), which marks the end point of a continuing action. Think of までに as "by [this time]" and まで as "until [this time]."

📝 Example ⑦ — Deadline
会議かいぎは 5時じまでに 終おわります。
The meeting will end by five.
📝 Example ⑧ — Obligation + deadline
土曜日どようびまでに 本ほんを 返かえさなければ なりません。
I have to return the book by Saturday.
⏰ まで (L4) — "Until"
5時じまで 働はたらきます。
I work until five o'clock.
Continuing action → end point
📅 までに (L17) — "By"
5時じまでに 終おわります。
It will end by five o'clock.
Deadline → must be done before this time
⚠️ Don't confuse まで and までに!

5時じまで 働はたらきます = I work UNTIL 5:00 (continuous action, L4). 5時じまでに 終おわります = It finishes BY 5:00 (deadline, L17). This distinction appears on nearly every N5 exam!

Model Conversation

会話かいわれんしゅう
💬 At the Doctor's Office
医者いしゃ: 薬くすりを 飲のまなければ なりませんよ。
You must take medicine.
ミラー: 毎日まいにち 飲のまなければ なりませんか。
Do I have to take it every day?
医者いしゃ: はい。そして、お酒さけは 飲のまないで ください。
Yes. And please don't drink alcohol.
ミラー: 運動うんどうは しなくても いいですか。
Do I not have to exercise?
医者いしゃ: ええ、今は しなくても いいです。金曜日きんようびまでに また 来きて ください。
Yes, you don't have to for now. Please come again by Friday.
🔍 Spot all 6 grammar points! なければ なりません (must take medicine), ないで ください (don't drink alcohol), なくても いいです (don't have to exercise), N は (お酒は — topicalized object), までに (by Friday deadline). The ない-form powers all three obligation patterns!

💡 Two pillars of Japanese conjugation! て-form powers positive patterns (て ください, て います, ても いいです). ない-form powers negative patterns (ないで ください, なければ なりません, なくても いいです). Together they cover the majority of grammar structures through N5 and N4!

"Until" vs "By"
まで (L4)までに (L17)
Meaning"Until" (end point of duration)"By" (deadline)
