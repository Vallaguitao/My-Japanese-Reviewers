Grammar 1

N が — The Object Marker for Special Words

好すき・わかります・あります

🎉 A New Role for が!

Most verbs use を to mark their object (L6: 本ほんを 読よみます). But certain verbs and adjectives use が instead. These are words that describe states (what you have, understand, like) rather than actions (what you do). Think of が as marking something you experience or feel toward, rather than something you actively do to.

📐 Structure
[わたしは] N + が + あります / わかります / 好すきです / 上手じょうずです...
📝 Example ① — Having
わたしは 車くるまが あります。
I have a car.
📝 Example ② — Understanding
わたしは 日本語にほんごが わかります。
I understand Japanese.
📝 Example ① — Liking
わたしは イタリア料理りょうりが 好すきです。
I like Italian food.
⚠️ Critical Distinction: を marks what you actively do to something (本ほんを 読よみます = I read a book). が marks what you experience or feel toward something (日本語にほんごが わかります = I understand Japanese). This is one of the most tested grammar points on the JLPT!

が expands through the course:

L9 N が あります / わかります / 好すきです / 上手じょうずです — state/feeling objects.

L10 N が あります / います — existence (something IS somewhere).

L13 N が ほしいです — wanting things.

L13 V たいです — wanting to do (the object can use が or を).

L17 V ないでください — negative requests.

Grammar 2

どんな N — Specific Answers

Answering with Names

📖 How it works

You learned どんな ("what kind of") in L8 to ask about characteristics. In L9, it's used to ask about preferences — and the answer can be a specific name rather than just an adjective. This makes conversation much more natural and practical.

💬 Example ④
A: どんな スポーツが 好すきですか。
What sports do you like?
B: サッカーが 好すきです。
I like soccer.
📝 More examples
どんな 音楽おんがくが 好すきですか。
What kind of music do you like?
どんな 映画えいがが 好すきですか。
What kind of movies do you like?
💡 Conversation Pattern: どんな N が 好すきですか → [specific name] が 好すきです. This is one of the most natural and useful question patterns for getting to know someone in Japanese!
Grammar 3

Adverbs of Degree

程度ていどの副詞ふくし

📖 How it works

These adverbs come before verbs or adjectives to modify their degree. They're organized in two groups: ones used with affirmative sentences and ones used with negative sentences. In L8 you learned とても/あまり for adjectives — now the system expands with five levels for verbs too!

📊 Degree Scale for Verbs (わかります etc.)

すこしa little

ぜんぜんnot at all

Grammar 3b

Adverb Examples

例文れいぶん
📝 Example ⑤ — Well
英語えいごが よく わかります。
I understand English well.
📝 Example ⑥ — A little
英語えいごが すこし わかります。
I understand English a little.
📝 Example ⑦ — Not very (negative!)
英語えいごが あまり わかりません。
I don't understand English very well.
💰 Example ⑧ — A lot
お金かねが たくさん あります。
I have a lot of money.
💸 Example ⑨ — None at all!
お金かねが ぜんぜん ありません。
I don't have any money at all.

📖 Also works with adjectives!

The textbook notes that すこし and ぜんぜん can also modify adjectives, not just verbs.

📝 Example ⑩ — Adjective + すこし
ここは すこし 寒さむいです。
It's a little cold here.
📝 Example ⑪ — Adjective + ぜんぜん
あの 映画えいがは ぜんぜん おもしろくないです。
That film is not at all interesting.
⚠️ Rules: あまり and ぜんぜん MUST be used with negative forms. You cannot say ×ぜんぜん わかります or ×あまり 好すきです. This is the same rule from L8 — now expanded to verbs!

Grammar 4

～から、～

"Because ~" — Giving Reasons
📐 Two Patterns
Reason + から、 Result。

Result。 Reason + から。

📖 How it works

The connector から ("because") links a reason to its result. Unlike English, the reason can come before the result (reason から、result) or after the result (result。reason から). Both are natural, and the textbook shows both patterns.

📝 Example ⑫ — Reason first
時間じかんが ありませんから、新聞しんぶんを 読よみません。
Because I don't have time, I don't read newspapers.
💬 Example ⑬ — Reason after
A: 毎朝まいあさ 新聞しんぶんを 読よみますか。
Do you read the newspaper every morning?
B: いいえ、読よみません。時間じかんが ありませんから。
No, I don't. Because I don't have time.
💡 Key Point: When giving a reason as an afterthought (answering "why" questions), it's very natural to put から at the end: 読よみません。時間じかんが ありませんから。 This is more common in conversation!

L4 から (from) — time/place origin: 9時じから.

L7 から (from) — source of giving/receiving: 銀行ぎんこうから.

L9 から (because) — reason connector. Different から!

L8 が (but) — contrast connector: おいしいですが、高たかいです.

L9 ～から、～ and L8 ～が、～ are a natural pair: から = because, が = but.

Grammar 5

どうして

"Why?"

📖 How it works

The interrogative どうして asks for a reason. The answer uses から at the end. There are two ways to ask: either include the full question (どうして ～ますか) or use the shortcut どうしてですか to ask about what someone just said.

💬 Example ⑭ — Full question
A: どうして 朝あさ 新聞しんぶんを 読よみませんか。
Why don't you read a newspaper in the mornings?
B: 時間じかんが ありませんから。
Because I don't have time.
💬 Example ⑮ — Shortcut どうしてですか
A: きょうは 早はやく 帰かえります。
I'm going home early today.
B: どうしてですか。
Why? (= Why is that?)
A: 子こどもの 誕生日たんじょうびですから。
Because it's my child's birthday.
💡 Conversation Shortcut: When someone says something unexpected, you can simply say どうしてですか ("Why is that?") without repeating their whole statement. This is very natural and common in daily conversation.

Compare

から vs が — Connector Pair

Because vs But
🔗 から — Because (L9)
時間じかんが ありませんから、行いきません。
Because I don't have time, I won't go.
Reason → Result (cause and effect)
↔️ が — But (L8)
高たかいですが、おいしいです。
It's expensive, but it's delicious.
Positive ↔ Negative (contrast)
💡 Easy Pair: から = "because" (explains WHY). が = "but" (shows CONTRAST). Together they give you the two most important sentence connectors in Japanese conversation!

💡 Key Insight: が and を both mark "objects" but in different ways. を = things you act on (read, eat, buy). が = things you experience, feel toward, or that exist (like, understand, have). This distinction is fundamental to Japanese grammar!
