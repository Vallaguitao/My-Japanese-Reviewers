Grammar 1

N が 欲ほしいです

"I want N"
📐 Structure
(わたしは) N が 欲ほしいです

📖 How it works

欲ほしい is an い-adjective meaning "desired / wanted." The thing you want is marked by が (not を!). Because it's an い-adjective, it conjugates exactly like other い-adjectives — the same L8/L12 rules apply for negation and past tense. This connects directly to L9's pattern where が marks the object of feelings (好すき, 上手じょうず, わかる, ある).

📝 Example ① — Wanting friends
わたしは 友達ともだちが 欲ほしいです。
I want some friends.
💬 Example ② — What do you want most?
A: 今いま 何なにが いちばん 欲ほしいですか。
What do you want most right now?
B: 車くるまが 欲ほしいです。
I want a car.
💬 Example ③ — Negative
A: 子こどもが 欲ほしいですか。
Do you want children?
B: いいえ、欲ほしくないです。
No, I don't.
💡 Conjugation reminder: 欲ほしい is い-adjective, so: 欲ほしいです (want) → 欲ほしくないです (don't want) → 欲ほしかったです (wanted) → 欲ほしくなかったです (didn't want). Same rules as L8/L12!
⚠️ Notes

欲ほしい — Important Restrictions

注意ちゅうい
⚠️ Note 1 — Speaker/Listener ONLY!

欲ほしいです can ONLY express what the speaker wants, or ask what the listener wants. You cannot use it to talk about what a third person wants. This is because 欲ほしい describes an internal feeling — and in Japanese, you can't claim to know someone else's inner feelings directly.

✅ OK — Speaker or Listener
（わたしは）車くるまが 欲ほしいです。
I want a car.
何なにが 欲ほしいですか。
What do you want?
❌ NOT OK — Third person
× 田中たなかさんは 車くるまが 欲ほしいです。
Tanaka wants a car.
You can't speak about someone else's desires this way!
⚠️ Note 2 — Don't use for offering!

欲ほしいですか is NOT used to offer things. Asking someone "コーヒーが 欲ほしいですか" sounds too direct, like interrogating them. Instead use:

✅ コーヒーは いかがですか。 (How about some coffee?)

✅ コーヒーを 飲のみませんか。 (Would you like some coffee?)

が marks objects of feelings & states:

L9 N が 好すき / 嫌きらい / 上手じょうず / 下手へた / わかる / ある

L13 N が 欲ほしい — adds "want" to the が family

L17 Third-person desires: ～たがっています / ～欲ほしがっています — how to express others' wants

Grammar 2

V ます-form たいです

"I want to do ~"
📐 Structure
(わたしは) V ます-form + たいです

📖 What is the ます-form?

The ます-form (also called the ます stem) is the form a verb takes when used with ます. You simply remove ます to get it. For example: 行いきます → 行いき (ます-form). 食たべます → 食たべ (ます-form). 買かいます → 買かい (ます-form).

📖 Adding たい

Attach たい to the ます-form to say "I want to [verb]." Like 欲ほしい, たい conjugates as an い-adjective: たいです → たくないです → たかったです → たくなかったです.

📝 Example ④ — Place
わたしは 沖縄おきなわへ 行いきたいです。
I want to go to Okinawa.
📝 Example ⑤ — Food (を or が)
わたしは てんぷらを 食たべたいです。
I'd like to eat some tempura.
💡 を or が for the object? With 〜たい, the object can be marked with either を or が. Both are correct! てんぷらを 食たべたい ≈ てんぷらが 食たべたい. In practice, を is more common in formal/written Japanese, while が emphasizes the desire.
Grammar 2b

〜たい — More Examples & Notes

〜たい のつづき
💬 Example ⑥ — Shopping question
A: 神戸こうべで 何なにを 買かいたいですか。
What do you want to buy in Kobe?
B: 靴くつを 買かいたいです。
I want to buy some shoes.
📝 Example ⑦ — Negative reason
おなかが 痛いたいですから、何なにも 食たべたくないです。
My stomach hurts, so I don't want to eat anything.

⚠️ Same restrictions as 欲ほしい!

欲ほしい vs 〜たい

ものが 欲ほしい vs ことを したい
🎁 欲ほしい — Want a THING
車くるまが 欲ほしいです。
I want a car.
Object = noun → particle が
🏃 〜たい — Want to DO something
車くるまを 買かいたいです。
I want to buy a car.
Object = verb action → particle を or が
💡 Summary: 欲ほしい = want a noun (N が 欲ほしい). 〜たい = want to do a verb (V ます-form たい). Both are い-adjectives. Both conjugate the same way. Both are limited to speaker/listener. Both should NOT be used for offering. They're a matched pair!
Grammar 3

Place へ V ます-form に 行いきます

Purpose of Movement
📐 Structure
Place へ {V ます-form / N} に 行いきます / 来きます / 帰かえります

📖 How it works

This pattern expresses the purpose of going somewhere — "I'm going to [place] TO DO [something]." The particle に here marks the purpose of the movement. The verb before に must be in the ます-form (remove ます). You can also use a noun before に when the noun implies an activity (like 買かい物もの, 勉強べんきょう, お祭まつり).

📝 Example ⑧ — Verb purpose
神戸こうべへ インド料理りょうりを 食たべに 行いきます。
I'm going to Kobe to eat Indian food.
📝 Example ⑨ — Noun purpose
神戸こうべへ 買かい物ものに 行いきます。
I'm going shopping in Kobe.
📝 Example ⑩ — 来きました
日本にほんへ 美術びじゅつの 勉強べんきょうに 来きました。
I came to Japan to study art.
💡 Events count as purpose! When a noun denoting an event (festival, concert) comes before に, the purpose is understood as "to see/attend." 京都きょうとの お祭まつりに 行いきます = "I'm going to (see) a festival in Kyoto." (Example ⑪)
⚠️ Which verbs take this pattern? Only movement verbs: 行いきます, 来きます, 帰かえります. The purpose must also use the verb form N を します / N を V ます, NOT just any verb. For example: 買かい物もの (=買かい物ものを します) ✅, 勉強べんきょう (=勉強べんきょうを します) ✅

Grammar 4

どこか / 何なにか

Indefinite Pronouns

📖 How it works

Adding か to question words turns them into indefinite pronouns: "some-" words. どこ (where?) → どこか (somewhere). 何なに (what?) → 何なにか (something). The particle へ comes after どこか, and を comes after 何なにか — but both particles can be omitted in casual speech.

💬 Example ⑫ — Somewhere
A: 冬休ふゆやすみは どこか[へ] 行いきましたか。
Did you go anywhere for winter holiday?
B: はい。北海道ほっかいどうへ スキーに 行いきました。
Yes. I went skiing in Hokkaido.
📝 Example ⑬ — Something
のどが かわきましたから、何なにか[を] 飲のみたいです。
I'm thirsty, so I'd like to drink something.
Question Word+ か = IndefiniteMeaning
どこ (where?)どこか [へ]somewhere
何なに (what?)何なにか [を]something
だれ (who?)だれかsomeone
いつ (when?)いつかsometime
💡 Particle position: The particle (へ, を) goes after どこか / 何なにか, not between. And it can often be dropped: どこか[へ] 行いきましたか = どこか 行いきましたか. Both are natural!

The か system builds throughout the course:

L13 Question word + か = "some-" (どこか, 何なにか, だれか)

L10 Question word + が = used in existence questions (何なにがありますか)

L15 Question word + も + negative = "no- / not any" (何なにもない, どこも行いかない)

L18 Question word + でも = "any-" (どこでも = anywhere, 何なんでも = anything)

Grammar 5

ご〜 — Honorific Prefix

敬語けいご

📖 How it works

The prefix ご is added before certain words to show respect. It elevates the word to a polite/honorific level, typically used when talking about or to someone you're showing deference to — like a customer, a superior, or a stranger in a service context. This is your first glimpse into the world of Japanese 敬語けいご (honorific language).

📝 Example ⑭ — At a restaurant
ご注文ちゅうもんは？
May I take your order?
✅ Common ご〜 words

ご注文ちゅうもん — order (honorific)

ご家族かぞく — family (someone else's)

ご意見いけん — opinion (honorific)

ご住所じゅうしょ — address (honorific)

📖 ご vs お

ご is typically used with 漢語かんご (Chinese-origin words): ご注文ちゅうもん, ご家族かぞく

お is typically used with 和語わご (native Japanese words): お名前なまえ (L1), お茶ちゃ, お祭まつり

💡 Don't overuse ご/お! These prefixes are only added to specific words — you can't add ご/お to just anything. Learning which words take which prefix comes with practice and exposure. For now, memorize the common ones you encounter in the textbook.

ます-form Quick Guide

ます形けい

📖 Why ます-form matters

The ます-form (also called the verb stem) is the base for many important grammar patterns going forward. L13 is the first lesson where you need to extract it, and it will appear again and again in later lessons. Getting comfortable with ます-form now will pay dividends!

Dictionary / ます Formます-form (Stem)+ たい+ に 行いく
行いきます行いき行いきたい行いきに…
食たべます食たべ食たべたい食たべに 行いく
買かいます買かい買かいたい買かいに 行いく
飲のみます飲のみ飲のみたい飲のみに 行いく
見みます見み見みたい見みに 行いく
しますししたいしに 行いく
来きます来き来きたい—
💡 Simple rule: ます-form = remove ます. That's it! 行いきます → 行いき. 食たべます → 食たべ. This stem is the foundation for たい (L13), ながら (L18), やすい/にくい (L20), 方かた (L14), and many more patterns!
