Grammar 1a

🔴 Group I — Dictionary Form

辞書形じしょけいのつくり方かた

📖 The Third Major Verb Form!

The dictionary form is how verbs appear in the dictionary — it's the "basic" or "plain affirmative" form. After て-form (L14) and ない-form (L17), this completes the trio of essential plain forms. For Group I, the final sound of the ます-form (always い-column) shifts to the corresponding う-column sound.

Grammar 1b

🔵 Group II & 🟣 III

グループ II・III の辞書形じしょけい
🔵 Group II — Add る

ます-form stem + る

食たべます → 食たべる

見みます → 見みる

🟣 Group III — Irregular

Memorize these two:

します → する

来きます → 来くる

勉強べんきょうします → 勉強べんきょうする

⚠️ 来きます → 来くる — Reading changes again!

Just like the ない-form (き→こ), the dictionary form shifts the reading: き→く. Three different readings for the same kanji: 来きます, 来こない, 来くる. This verb is the most irregular in Japanese!

💡 All three plain forms, one comparison:
て-form (L14): きて, 食たべて, して
ない-form (L17): 来こない, 食たべない, しない
Dictionary form (L18): 来くる, 食たべる, する
Group II and III follow the same simple pattern across all three forms — the challenge is always Group I!

Grammar 2a

N が できます

"Can do ~" (with Nouns)
📐 Structure
N が できます

📖 How it works

できます is a verb meaning "can do / be able to / be possible." When used with a noun, the noun represents the ability or what's possible. The object of できます is marked with が (not を). Nouns indicating skills (にほんご, ダンス, スキー, ピアノ) and nouns indicating motion/sports can be used directly.

📝 Example ① — Language ability
ミラーさんは 日本語にほんごが できます。
Mr. Miller can speak Japanese.
📝 Example ② — Situational possibility
雪ゆきが たくさん 降ふりましたから、ことしは スキーが できます。
A lot of snow has fallen, so we can ski this year.
💡 が marks the ability, NOT を! With できます, the ability/skill takes が: 日本語にほんごが できます (can do Japanese). This connects to the L9 pattern where が marks the object of feelings (日本語にほんごが 好すきです) and L13 (車くるまが 欲ほしいです). Abilities, feelings, and desires all use が!
Grammar 2b

V-dict. こと が できます

"Can do ~" (with Verbs)
📐 Structure
V dictionary form + こと が できます

📖 How it works

When expressing ability with a verb instead of a noun, the dictionary form + こと turns the verb into a noun phrase ("the act of doing ~"), which then connects to が できます. こと literally means "thing/matter" — so 漢字かんじを 読よむ こと = "the act of reading kanji." This noun phrase then takes が to connect with できます.

📝 Example ③ — Reading ability
ミラーさんは 漢字かんじを 読よむ ことが できます。
Mr. Miller can read kanji.
📝 Example ④ — Payment method
カードで 払はらう ことが できます。
You can pay by card.
💡 こと = verb nominalizer! こと turns a verb into a noun: 読よむ (read) → 読よむこと (reading / the act of reading). This is one of the most important grammar tools in Japanese — you'll use こと to turn verbs into nouns in many future patterns!

L9 N が わかります — "Understand N" (comprehension)

L9 N が じょうずです — "Good at N" (skill description)

L18 N が できます — "Can do N" (ability/noun)

L18 V こと が できます — "Can do V" (ability/verb)

All ability expressions use が to mark what you can do/understand/are good at!

Grammar 3

趣味しゅみは N / V こと です

"My hobby is ~"
📐 Structure
わたしの 趣味しゅみは { N / V-dict こと } です
📝 Example ⑤ — Noun hobby
わたしの 趣味しゅみは 音楽おんがくです。
I'm interested in music.
📝 Example ⑥ — Verb hobby (more specific)
わたしの 趣味しゅみは 音楽おんがくを 聞きく ことです。
I like listening to music.
💡 Noun vs Verb version: 趣味しゅみは 音楽おんがくです = "My hobby is music" (general). 趣味しゅみは 音楽おんがくを 聞きく ことです = "My hobby is listening to music" (specific). The V-dictionary form + こと version lets you be much more precise about what exactly you enjoy doing. This is the go-to self-introduction pattern in Japan!
Grammar 4

～ まえに、V₂

"Before ~"
📐 Structure — Three Types
V dictionary form + まえに、V₂
N の + まえに、V₂
Quantifier(time) + まえに、V₂ (no の!)

📖 How it works

This pattern expresses that V₂ happens before the action/event/time in まえに. The verb before まえに is always in dictionary form regardless of whether V₂ is past or non-past — the tense of the whole sentence is set by V₂ alone. With nouns, add の before まえに. With time quantifiers (1時間じかん, 3日か, etc.), connect directly without の.

📝 Example ⑦ — Verb + まえに (past)
日本にほんへ 来くる まえに、日本語にほんごを 勉強べんきょうしました。
I studied Japanese before coming to Japan.
📝 Example ⑧ — Verb + まえに (non-past)
寝ねる まえに、本ほんを 読よみます。
I read a book before going to bed.
📝 Example ⑨ — Noun + の まえに
食事しょくじの まえに、手てを 洗あらいます。
I wash my hands before eating.
📝 Example ⑩ — Quantifier + まえに (NO の)
田中たなかさんは 1時間じかん まえに、出でかけました。
Mr. Tanaka went out one hour ago.
⚠️ V before まえに is ALWAYS dictionary form!

Even if V₂ is in the past tense, the verb before まえに stays in dictionary form. In example ⑦, 来くる まえに (NOT 来ました まえに). The dictionary form is "tense-neutral" here — まえに itself indicates the time relationship.

Compare

まえに vs てから

"Before" vs "After"
⏪ V dict まえに (L18) — "Before ~"
寝ねる まえに、歯はを 磨みがきます。
I brush my teeth before sleeping.
Dictionary form always, regardless of tense
⏩ V てから (L16) — "After ~"
歯はを 磨みがいてから、寝ねます。
After brushing my teeth, I sleep.
て-form always, regardless of tense
💡 Beautiful symmetry: まえに uses dictionary form (plain affirmative) to say "before." てから uses て-form to say "after." Both are tense-neutral — the final verb alone sets the tense. Together they give you complete control over time sequencing!

Grammar 5

なかなか + Negative

"Not easily / Not as expected"

📖 How it works

なかなか is an adverb used only with negative expressions. It conveys that something doesn't happen as easily or as quickly as expected — "not readily," "not easily," "hard to ~." It adds a feeling of frustration or unmet expectations. The sentence always ends with a negative verb.

📝 Example ⑪
日本にほんでは なかなか 馬うまを 見みる ことが できません。
You don't see many horses in Japan. (It's hard to see horses in Japan.)
💡 Always negative! なかなか MUST pair with a negative ending: なかなか できません, なかなか わかりません, なかなか 覚えられません. It expresses the gap between expectation and reality — "I expected to be able to, but I can't easily."
Grammar 6

ぜひ

"By all means / Definitely"

📖 How it works

ぜひ is an adverb that intensifies expressions of desire or hope. It means "by all means," "definitely," or "I really (want to)." Unlike なかなか, ぜひ is always used with positive/hopeful expressions — たいです, て ください, ましょう, etc.

📝 Example ⑫ — Strong desire
ぜひ 北海道ほっかいどうへ 行いきたいです。
I really want to go to Hokkaido.
📝 Example ⑬ — Warm invitation
ぜひ 遊あそびに 来きて ください。
Do please come and visit me.
😤 なかなか + Negative
なかなか できません。
"Can't easily do it"
🌟 ぜひ + Positive/Desire
ぜひ 行いきたいです。
"Definitely want to go"
