Grammar 1

形容詞けいようし — Adjectives

Two Families

🎉 Major Milestone!

Until now, you could only describe things using nouns (N₁ は N₂ です). Lesson 8 unlocks adjectives — words that describe the state or quality of something (big, small, beautiful, quiet, etc.). Japanese has two types of adjectives, and they conjugate differently:

🔵 い-adjectives

Always end in い in dictionary form.

高たかい — expensive/tall
安やすい — cheap
大おおきい — big
小ちいさい — small
おもしろい — interesting
🩷 な-adjectives

Need な before nouns. Often katakana or don't end in い.

親切しんせつ[な] — kind
静しずか[な] — quiet
有名ゆうめい[な] — famous
にぎやか[な] — lively
きれい[な] — pretty/clean
⚠️ Trap Alert: Some words look like い-adjectives because they end in い, but are actually な-adjectives! The two most dangerous: きれい (pretty) and 有名ゆうめい (famous). They use な before nouns, NOT い-adjective rules. You must memorize these exceptions!

Adjectives grow throughout the course:

L8 Non-past adjectives (aff & neg) + modifying nouns.

L12 Past tense adjectives: 高たかかったです / 静しずかでした.

L12 Comparing: N₁ は N₂ より Adj です ("N₁ is more Adj than N₂").

L16 Adj て-form for connecting: 安やすくて おいしい ("cheap AND delicious").

Grammar 2

N は Adj です

Adjective Predicates

📖 How it works

Adjective sentences end in です for politeness, just like noun sentences. But the way they form the negative is different for い-adjectives and な-adjectives.

🔵 い-adjective: Affirmative
富士山ふじさんは 高たかいです。 ②
Mt. Fuji is high.
🩷 な-adjective: Affirmative
ワットさんは 親切しんせつです。 ①
Mr. Watt is kind.

🔄 Negative Forms — They're Different!

🔵 い-adj Negative: drop い → くない
高たかい → 高たかくないです。
It's not expensive.
あそこは 静しずかじゃ（では）ありません。 ③
🩷 な-adj Negative: + じゃ ありません
親切しんせつじゃ ありません。
He's not kind.
Same as noun negative! (L1: N₁は N₂ じゃ ありません)
💡 Key Difference: い-adjectives change their own ending (い→くない). な-adjectives use the same pattern as nouns (じゃ ありません). This is the fundamental difference you must master!
⚠️ Special: The negative of いい (good) is irregular: いい → よくないです (NOT いくない!). This is because いい comes from よい.

Grammar 2b

Adjective Questions

形容詞けいようしの質問しつもん

📖 How it works

Questions using adjectives are formed exactly like noun sentences (add か to the end, L1) and verb sentences (L4). To answer, repeat the adjective. You cannot use そうです/ちがいます — those only work with noun sentences.

💬 Example ⑤ — い-adjective
A: ペキンは 寒さむいですか。
Is it cold in Beijing?
B: はい、寒さむいです。
Yes, it is [cold].
💬 Example ⑥ — な-adjective
A: 奈良なら公園こうえんは にぎやかですか。
Is Nara Park busy?
B: いいえ、にぎやかじゃ ありません。
No, it isn't [busy].
💡 Answer Pattern: Repeat the adjective — はい、寒さむいです / いいえ、寒さむくないです. Do NOT use そうです/ちがいます — those are only for noun identity questions (L1).
Grammar 3

Adj + N (Modifying Nouns)

形容詞けいようし + 名詞めいし
📐 Structures
🔵 い-adj: 高たかい + N = 高たかい 山やま

🩷 な-adj: 親切しんせつ + な + N = 親切しんせつな 先生せんせい

📖 How it works

When adjectives come before a noun to modify it, い-adjectives keep their い, but な-adjectives add な before the noun. This is where な-adjectives get their name!

🔵 い-adj: Keep い
富士山ふじさんは 高たかい 山やまです。 ⑧
Mt. Fuji is a high mountain.
🩷 な-adj: Add な
ワットさんは 親切しんせつな 先生せんせいです。 ⑦
Mr. Watt is a kind teacher.
💡 Comparison: Before です → い-adj keeps い, な-adj drops な. Before a noun → い-adj keeps い, な-adj adds な. The な only appears when directly modifying a noun — that's the whole reason they're called な-adjectives!

Grammar 4

～が、～

"But" — Contrast Connector

📖 How it works

The connector が joins two clauses in an antithetical (contrasting) relationship. When the first clause says something positive, the second says something negative — and vice versa. Think of it as "but" in English.

📝 Example ⑨
日本にほんの 食たべ物ものは おいしいですが、高たかいです。
Japanese food is delicious, but it's expensive.
📝 More examples
この 部屋へやは 広ひろいですが、暗くらいです。
This room is spacious, but it's dark.
💡 Key Point: が connects two clauses about the same subject, with contrasting qualities. Positive + が + Negative, or Negative + が + Positive. This is different from でも (L1) which starts a new sentence.

L8 ～が、～ = "but" (contrast connector between clauses).

L9 ～から = "because" (reason connector).

L14 が in a different role: subject marker (～が ほしい / ～が わかる).

Grammar 5

とても / あまり

Degree Adverbs

📖 How it works

These two adverbs modify adjectives to express degree. とても (very) is used with affirmative sentences, and あまり (not very) is used with negative sentences. They come before the adjective they modify.

✅ とても — "very" (affirmative only)
ペキンは とても 寒さむいです。 ⑩
Beijing is very cold.
これは とても 有名ゆうめいな 映画えいがです。 ⑪
This is a very famous movie.
❌ あまり — "not very" (negative only!)
シャンハイは あまり 寒さむくないです。 ⑫
Shanghai is not very cold.
あまり 有名ゆうめいじゃ ありません。 ⑬
It's not very well-known.
⚠️ Critical Rule: とても = affirmative sentences ONLY. あまり = negative sentences ONLY. You cannot say ×とても 寒くないです or ×あまり 寒いです. This is a common test question!

Grammar 6

N は どうですか

"How is N?"

📖 How it works

The question N は どうですか asks about the listener's impression, opinion, or feelings about something they have experienced, visited, tasted, or encountered. It's perfect for asking "How do you find ~?" or "What do you think of ~?"

💬 Example ⑭
A: 日本にほんの 生活せいかつは どうですか。
How do you find life in Japan?
B: 楽たのしいです。
It's fun.
💡 Key Point: The answer to どうですか uses an adjective! This is the natural question that gets adjective answers — perfect practice for everything in this lesson.
Grammar 7

N₁ は どんな N₂ ですか

"What kind of N?"

📖 How it works

The interrogative どんな modifies a noun and asks about the nature, type, or characteristics of something. It translates to "what kind of ~?" or "what sort of ~?"

💬 Example ⑮
A: 奈良ならは どんな 町まちですか。
What sort of town is Nara?
B: 古ふるい 町まちです。
It's an old town.
🔍 どう — asks for impression
日本にほんは どうですか。
How is Japan? (your feeling)
Answer: adjective predicate (楽たのしいです)
🔎 どんな — asks for characteristics
奈良ならは どんな 町まちですか。
What kind of town is Nara?
Answer: adjective + noun (古ふるい 町まちです)

Grammar 8

そうですね...

Thinking / Hedging

📖 How it works

You learned そうですね in L5 for expressing agreement. In L8, the same expression is used with a different nuance: it shows the speaker is thinking before answering. It's like "Well..." or "Let me see..." — a natural pause while forming your opinion.

💬 Example ⑯
A: お仕事しごとは どうですか。
How's your work going?
B: そうですね。忙いそがしいですが、おもしろいです。
Well... It's busy, but it's interesting.
🤝 L5: Agreement
そうですね。
"Yes, that's right." / "Indeed."
Responds to a statement you agree with
🤔 L8: Thinking
そうですね...
"Well..." / "Let me think..."
Pauses to consider before answering
💡 Context Clue: When そうですね responds to a statement, it means agreement (L5). When it comes after a question and before the speaker's own opinion, it signals thinking (L8). The intonation is also slightly different — the thinking version trails off more.
