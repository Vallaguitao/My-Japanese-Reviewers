Extended Sounds for Foreign Words

Learn the V-sounds, W-sounds, and other special combinations

Why Do We Need Special Katakana?

📚 The Problem:

Traditional Japanese doesn't have certain sounds found in foreign languages, like the "V" sound in English (violin, video) or some "W" sounds (Wi-Fi, woman).

💡 The Solution:

Japanese created special katakana combinations to represent these foreign sounds more accurately!

Examples of Foreign Words:

Violin → ヴァイオリン (vaiorin)
Video → ヴィデオ (video)
Venus → ヴィーナス (viinasu)

V-Sound Series (ヴ)

How to Create V-Sounds:

ウ (u) + ゛(dakuten) = ヴ (vu)

Then add small vowels to make va, vi, ve, vo!

💡 Remember:

ヴ is just ウ with dakuten marks (゛). Think of it as a "voiced" version of the U sound!

⚠️ Important Note:

Many Japanese speakers actually pronounce ヴ as "B" (バビブベボ) in casual speech. So "violin" might sound like "baiorin" instead of "vaiorin"!

W-Sound Series

📖 Background:

Traditional hiragana has わ (wa) を (wo/o) but historically had ゐ (wi) and ゑ (we). These are obsolete in hiragana but katakana recreates similar sounds for foreign words!

Sound Katakana Romaji Construction
WA ワ wa Standard katakana
WI ウィ wi ウ + ィ
WU ウ wu/u Standard ウ (u)
WE ウェ we ウ + ェ
WO ウォ wo ウ + ォ

💡 Pattern:

W-sounds use ウ (u) + small vowels, except for ワ which is its own character!

Other Special Combinations

🔤 More Extended Sounds:

Japanese katakana has many other combinations for foreign sounds!

F-Series (ファ, フィ, フェ, フォ):

ファイル (fairu) - File
フィルム (firumu) - Film
フェスティバル (fesutibaru) - Festival
フォーク (fooku) - Fork

T-Series (ティ, トゥ):

ティー (tii) - Tea (sometimes written as テイー)
パーティー (paatii) - Party
トゥデイ (tudei) - Today

Even More Special Sounds!

D-Series (ディ, ドゥ):

ディスク (disuku) - Disk
ディズニー (dizunii) - Disney

SH-Series (シェ):

シェフ (shefu) - Chef
シェイク (sheiku) - Shake

CH-Series (チェ):

チェック (chekku) - Check
チェス (chesu) - Chess

J-Series (ジェ):

ジェット (jetto) - Jet

Practice Tips & Summary

💡 Key Takeaways:

1. V-sounds: Use ヴ (ウ + dakuten) + small vowels
2. W-sounds: Use ウ + small vowels (except ワ)
3. Purpose: These combinations help represent foreign sounds more accurately
4. Usage: Mostly found in loan words from English and other languages

📝 Practice Exercise:

Try writing these words in katakana:

1. Virus → ヴィールス (viirusu)
2. WiFi → ワイファイ (waifai)
3. Vodka → ヴォッカ (vokka)
4. Western → ウェスタン (wesutan)

⚠️ Remember:

Not all Japanese people use these special sounds! Some might write "バイオリン" (baiorin) instead of "ヴァイオリン" (vaiorin). Both are acceptable!
